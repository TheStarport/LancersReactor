Garibaldi

Wed Apr 30, 2003 9:01 pm by Garibaldi

It depends on your playing style.

Choose the Titan if you want a big target that moves like a tank but has the heaviest armour and can hold the most shield batteries/nanobots

Choose the Eagle if you don't mind having quite a bit less armour and shield batteries/nanobots, but want a much smaller target that maneuvers noticeably better.

Choose the Sabre if you can get by with the reduced shield batteries/nanobots but are willing to sacrifice having a smaller target (and maybe some maneuverability) for slightly heavier armour and the ability to have a forward firing turret.

Personally, I don't mind the looks of any of them. That shouldn't be the main factor in how you choose your ship either. I like the Sabre most because the way that the turret is mounted gives it the most forward facing firepower. I have noticed that I seem to lose weapons much quicker with the Titan than I do with the Sabre and the Eagle as well.

Garibaldi

Tue May 06, 2003 1:24 am by Garibaldi

Yes, everyone has their own opinion about which is best. But I like the Sabre the most. It has a good balance of armour and maneuverability. Being able to make use of a forward firing turret is another big factor too. When you are going up against fleets of Very Heavy Fighters, I would rather be able to kill them quicker than be able to fend off their attacks more. I also find that I lose weapons quicker with the Titan than I do with other ships. I have experimented with different ships and weapon combos and my personal favorite is the Sabre with 4 Nomad energy blasters, 2 Kraken type 2 guns, and a Salamanca turret. They all have the same rate of fire and I am usually able to take out 8-12 very heavy fighters at a time without using any shield batteries or nanobots. That's what I use, that's what I recommend, but everyone has their own preference so just use what works best for you.
