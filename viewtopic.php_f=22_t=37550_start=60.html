It depends on if the mod changes the version/build number.

If it changes the version/build number, you can only connect to servers with the same server/build number. Freelancer.exe has to have the same version/build number as the FLServer.exe of the server you want to connect to.

If you're just playing by yourself, you don't need to worry about it.

Earendil

Sat Oct 21, 2006 12:15 am by Earendil

Bladerunner, don't know if you got your answer but I certainly have encountered your problem before. The short answer is you can't do what you want to do (two servers on the same LAN) without two Internet (not LAN) IP addresses.

Regardless of the different ports on your LAN, the FL global server merely sees server info coming from your one Internet IP address. Sometimes it will be one server in the list, sometimes another, depends on when your servers refresh the FL global list server. I used to have this when I had two servers. I ended up getting a dual WAN gateway (Xincom XC-DPG502) and two ADSL lines. This means I have one LAN but have two pipes to the Internet and thus two Internet IP addresses. I also have to tightly bind the server's fixed LAN IP address to the specific Internet pipe (ADSL line) I want but that's just details.

Nevertheless your setup works fine for any players connected to your LAN (but not from the Internet). In your LAN you have both different ports and different IP addresses and thus the FL client (the FL game) can detect those servers seperately in it's FL server list.
