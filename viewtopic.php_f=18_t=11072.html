coxpwr;

Easy to answer you, and if you'd actually read any of the ship editing posts here you'd see what your error was:

In market.ini, each line for a ship denotes a few things, and if you set them all to zero the ship WILL disappear from the market. Here's the rundown as to what they mean, as known so far (using your example):

Marketgood = gf1_package, 1, -1, 0, 0, 1, 1, 1

The _package denotes the name of the elements from shiparch.ini that taken together form the "package" or the parts of the ship.

The first number (1)is the level for that station at which you may buy the ship; in your example, the Starflier (gf1_package) may be bought at level 1.

The second number (-1) is the minimum faction rating at which you may buy the ship; -1 is full hostile, 1 is full friendly, 0 is neutral. Fractions can be used here - express 'em as decimals; i.e. -.5 is almost hostile.

THe third and fourth numbers, TAKEN TOGETHER, dentoe whether the ship is buyable at that station. What each individual number means yet is unknown - in your example, setting the third and fourth numbers (0, 0) to (1, 1) makes the ship buyable at that station. (1, 0) and (0, 1) don't seem to do much as of yet.

Another thing to note here - You can designate as many ships as you want to be buyable on the station, HOWEVER, only the first three ships on the list for that station show in the Market - so make sure that you only have three ships for each station tagged as buyable.


The last three numbers (1, 1, 1) have a purpose, nobody knows what it is yet. Just leave 'em be.

Prices for the ships can be set to an extent in goods.ini; there seem to be some hardcoded limits by class as to what a ship will sell/buy for, as well as an age factor.

Example used is Sabre at Planet Manhattan:

Price set in goods.ini is 100.

In the Market, price on ship as new is $53,400.
Price on ship as used (selling back to dealer): $80.

Price on same ship, used, being sold to dealer at Planet Leeds: $0.
