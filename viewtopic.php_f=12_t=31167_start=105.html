It's anything BUT a console game. You'd have to play it. It was released on XBOX first, but it was developed simultaneously for the XBOX and the PC. It sort of controls like a Third Person game, but there's more to it than that. You control up to a party of three people. Some Jedi, two droids, a Mandalorian warrior, and a Republic soldier. You can customize how you're character looks, there are workbenches throughout the game to which you can upgrade and customize your weapons and lightsabers. With lightsabers, when you become a Jedi later in the game, you can find crystals that will change the color of you saber as well as change the intensity and strength of the saber. It's really cool. You can also upgrade to two sabers or a dual-bladed lightsaber. When you get into fights, it's a cross between turn-based and real-time. You can pause the game to issue commands, but you don't have to. I don't. Fights look really cool when you have a lightsaber. They get better when you go up against Dark Jedi. It's really challenging, too. It's certainly more of a challenge than any of those Final Fantasy games. You can level up any individual stats you want or let the computer make the choices for you. It's all up to you. Unfortunately, you don't start out as a Jedi, that's one of the downsides. The graphics are fantastic and the story also has a major twist when you get to a certain point. The voice acting is above and beyond anything else that I've heard in a game. The music is different, you got some original themes from John Williams, but a lot of it is done by someone else. This is one of those few games, that can get you emotionally involved. There were some points in this game that when I made a bad decision, it made me feel bad for the iinnocent people who were involved. This is one of the funnest games I've ever played.
