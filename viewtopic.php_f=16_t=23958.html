Sherlog

Mon Sep 08, 2003 7:08 pm by Sherlog

It is virtually impossible for Juni to get killed outside a story mission; even inside story missions it is very unlikely that she gets killed because her ship is indestructible most of the time (probably the result of Microsoft QA). Good thing too, for when I destroyed the two killable cruisers near Benford in mission 4 I tried to hasten the process by dropping homing mines at my prey and it took half a dozen mines or more for me to notice that Juni was nearer to my ship than the cruiser I was working on, and that all the mines were hitting her ship.

There are only two occasions where a dialog pops up during a random mission that I can think of. First, some bystanders scan your cargo hold and express desire for the contents. Not very likely, but not impossible either. Second, you had a 'capture' mission but your prey was destroyed by gun fire or environmental hazards (mines, radiation) before you tractored their escape pod; similar things could happen on a 'tractor stuff' mission.

Have you visited Tobias at the equipment dealership in Leeds before venturing out after mission 4? What is your current level and net worth, and what is the entry for 'Next Level Requirement'?

If you have met the conditions for the next story mission then usually the path to where Juni awaits you gets highlighted on the nav map, whenever there are no waypoints that you have set manually or that are the result of booking a random mission. However, it seems that the automatic 'path to Juni' feature does not always have infinite patience - in very rare cases it can give you up for hopeless if you ignore it for hours and days on end.

OmegaKnight, there must be something very wrong with your system - I've never heard about a problem like this in all the time that I've been haunting this board. Also, I have flown this mission quite a number of times both in the Starflier and in the Rhino (best time 15:45 @ Criminal Base Destroyed, with Starflier/Lavablades - in case T-Hawk is listening in ) on several different computers, and I have tried quite a couple of stunts.

I think the most promising avenues are these two:

(1) Make sure that you are running a completely unmodded game (reinstall if necessary). After very extensive testing ( ) I can vouch for T-Hawk's mod being safe to use during the campaign but I do not know about other mods. Always use FLMM to manage your mods so that you can back out without reinstalling.

(2) Check the Tech Problems forum for posts regarding sound codec problems, then check your system to make sure that you are not thus afflicted. It is conceivable that the Rogues are set to be indestructible during the 'base blows up' cutscene and that the game is missing the trigger event to make them mortal again because some sound file or other failed to play. But I think this possibility is rather remote.
