Cool, then that would make it easy for Freelancer to support joystick as an OPTIONAL controller bound to attitude control.

Consider this: the interface, as it is designed, is complete, and doesn't need a joystick, right? Now consider what makes it complete: the mouse and keyboard. Now, stay with me here, what if you used your left hand for both mouse AND keyboard, and used your free right hand to control ship attitude via a JOYSTICK?

This idea is so elegant in its simplicity that it was no doubt overlooked due to that fact. I noticed in a lot of back-threads the word "impossible" thrown around repeatedly in reference to the joystick, mostly because people assumed using the joystick PRECLUDED using the mouse.

But we have two hands, no? Why not use the mouse exactly the way it was designed to be used, but WITH THE LEFT HAND! What do you guys think? Pretty clever, huh?

I think now that we've figured that out it is pretty easy to keep the game accessible to the masses -- the original reason for RIPPING OUT joystick support -- just bury the joystick option on some out-of-the-way menu that won't easily be found by skittish crossovers from The Sims who might bolt if confronted with scary words like "flight control" or "joystick" -- an option back in the back end somewhere to bind attitude control to a joystick, and nobody has to even know about it except those who go looking for it ... sweet, eh?
