Sun Jan 18, 2004 11:16 pm by Sir Spectre

I just read the updated news on the Official site and saw the most recent news (after a year of not having anything) for the movie. Apparently the producers are looking for stages to shoot the film in. At the moment they're looking at Australia's sound stages. But this doesn't still give me much hope.

Why doesn't it give me hope? I have worked for 20th Century Fox and they invited me to their studio to have a personal tour. Before I went, the first thing I did was get all the information I could about the place and what I came across were rental prices for each stage they have.

A stage is a stage, you don't need to go to Australia to see them. You look at how much it will cost per day, figure out your construction time, your filming time, your deconstruction time and the cost of transporting necessary people and equipment to and from the stage whether it be Shepperton down the block or Fox Studios Australia on that big island next to New Zealand (when was there an Old Zealand or just a Zealand?). You look at numbers and costs.

The news feature of a producer going to visit a stage sounds like them trying to maintain some amount of emotional fervor in the fans so they don't forget about Red Dwarf or the fact they keep wanting to make a film. Maybe I'm just cynical.

Sir S
