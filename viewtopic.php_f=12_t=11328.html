The SWAT games were great! I do not dispute that. ("Basho" lol ) However, that is not what I'm getting at.

Firstly, it would probably help to understand the concept if I explained that I am a very serious Civil War buff. If you wanted me to, I could probably recite the entire command structure of the Army of Northern Virginia down to the brigadier generals. Secondly, in every war that the US has ever participated in up until WWI, commanders have had to remain close to the front lines in order to transmit their instructions. The numbers of troops involved were so great that in order to keep them all from routing, you would have to be up there with them. (most of the troops had never seen combat, and/or were easily scared)

As soon as I played Gettysburg!, I instantly fell in love with its stunning realism and flexibility. (God bless Sid Meier and the Firaxis guys!) However, as with any game, there were always a few elements that it would be better off with (or, as in the case of SL's Moose, without). I would like to see someone make it so that you could visit the front and actually rally your troops when you're in such bad shape that it's worth risking one to the head. There would be no battle map, you'd have to create it on your own from reports and hilltop views. And I've always been puzzled about how your rank never does anything but make you feel good. Your rank could actually change your role, and I'd no longer see myself commanding the entire bolshevik army as a 2nd lieutenant. Anyone who implemented these concpets would certainly get my money.

You control subordinate commanders who would actually DO something. If you had a good commander in a good position, you could tell him to attack and he'd figure out his own way to do it. But if he's not doing the job the way you want or you'd just feel more secure, you can detatch the unit and do it yourself. (Or, if it's just an issue of conflicting personalities, you could sack the SOB )
Anyone who implemented these concpets would certainly get my money.
