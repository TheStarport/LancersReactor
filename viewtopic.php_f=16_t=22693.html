Fri Aug 08, 2003 8:35 am by Sherlog

If you want a shield buster in your loadout then there is little choice since there is only one suitable pulse gun - the Corsair Borroco (600 m/s). The best hull guns that match this are, of course, the Outcast particle guns. Contrast this to the Anubis that can use the class 6 Debilitator Turret (700 m/s) which enables it to use the Xenos particle guns instead. Same damage etc. as the Outcast Pyros kit but the 700 m/s speed instead of 600 m/s makes a noticable difference in combat.

For fighting high-level ships (with class 8/9/10 shields) I'd go for a loadout with 2 x Borroco and 4 x Pyros Type 3. The two Borrocos are necessary in order to beat down the shield quickly, to create more torpedoing opportunities. Against lower level ships - this includes the SP campaign - one Borroco is enough and you could use the other class 5 slot for a hull gun (Pyros Type 2) outside the campaign and a Catapult launcher during the campaign. For missions against unshielded enemies (12 and 13) replace the Borroco with a hull gun or a second missile launcher.

Using only one pulse gun has the advantage that you do not have to deal with weapon groups - simply toggle the Borroco on and off as needed via the caps lock key.

Starkiller torpedos are a must - they are the reason why you buy a HF instead of an LF in the first place - and for mines I'd choose Seekers except for some campaign missions where you have to destroy plenty of big targets and where the Drillers can be helpful.

Sherlog

Sat Aug 09, 2003 12:18 am by Sherlog

The Borrocos are sold only by the Corsairs, on all their bases:
o Cadiz Base, Omega-5
o Leon Base, Omega-41
o Planet Crete, Omicron Gamma
o Tripoli Shipyard, Omicron Gamma

The Pyros Type 2/3 guns are sold by the Outcasts, on all their bases:
o Cali, Tau-23
o Ruiz, Omicron Beta
o Planet Malta, Omicron Alpha

Note: after mission 7 the Outcasts are neutral and that is enough for the class 5 Pyros Type 2 thingies but for the class 6 guns you have to get them to 20% friendly-neutral. So you may have to buy a bribe or shoot plenty of their enemies.

The situation with the Corsairs it better: they start 65% hostile and you can get them to stop shooting by killing just a handful of Mollys, Red Hessians or Bounty Hunter depots. That is enough to buy the Borrocos ... They would even sell them to you at 100% hostile but of course then you cannot dock with their bases.
