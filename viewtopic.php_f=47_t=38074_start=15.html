Thu Mar 10, 2005 4:46 pm by Wilde

Tanya supported Rob on her shoulder as she limped towards the hangar, checking behind her shoulder for the pursuing blue-haired bounty hunter. Rob was quickly fading from consciousness, and she needed to get to their falcon immediately.

Suddenly, as she was entering the door of the hangar, a jet-black Sabre cruised expertly into the landing pad before her feet. The gilded, gleaming words Ocean Soul , followed by a series of intricately welded symbols, could be seen on its sleek black body. Tanya frowned. Where had she seen it before...?

Her heart jumped as a man vaulted forward from it, long hair flying behind him, black ink scarring his skin. Tanya saw the bulge in his jacket almost immediately, and ran without a second thought behind a Kusarian statue, her heart pounding at the second assassination attempt in as many minutes. The blue bolts flying from his rifle punctured holes in the statue between Tanya and her attacker. She lay Rob down on the ground, safe from harm (she hoped), and pulled out her own laser.

Smoothly somersaulting out from behind the column, she readied her weapon and shot five blasts violently at the dark haired man attacking her, running forward to confront her attacker.

________________

Damien had seen the woman run into the hangar as he piloted his cruising ship down onto the landing pad. With no thought but the laughing, cruel face of Mason in his mind he put into practice 10 years of Hessian training and vaulted out of his ship, firing blasts towards her.

Now the woman--Tanya--moved quickly closer to him, kicking the gun out of his hands and twisting her foot until it landed directly in his chest. Damien flew backwards, crashing into Ocean Soul. He instantly leaped up again and grabbed her fist incoming towards his face, twisting it around in an armlock and forcing her towards the ground. Damien grabbed the rifle on the ground, holding it to her head. The job should be done quickly...and he would see Nikolai again.

But she twisted out of his hold, and still holding her laser turned her face towards his. It was so beautiful, he had never realized it. ...It had something of Nikolai in it...

His face came into Damien's mind, and he stopped, falling back. The woman stood up, pointing her laser still down at him. He rose to his knees. Cast the gun away. Fell forward. Damien's eyes filled with tears. Nikolai...Nikolai...
_______________________________________

Tanya stood over him, her foot on his back. She didn't understand. He was conscious, surely, and still in fighting shape. And yet... he lay there on the floor, sobbing, his hand clutched around a small metal serpent. Muttering, even...her ears pricked up. She had heard him say the word "Mason." Setting the power level on her firearm to "stun", she fired one shot into his back and dragged the unconscious man into the hold of her falcon, pulling out a first aid kit and running back to attend on Rob.
______________________________________


"He looks Slavic, or Rhinelander. I think I saw him once, when I was working on the Hawaii. His face wasn't easily forgettable," Tanya said into her intercom.

"A Hessian, probably. I don't like them too much. But they're damn good fighters...maybe this guy could be some use."

They were back in their ships and soaring away from the base, Rob sufficiently patched up. Tanya was shaken after the fight, and mystified at the man's behaviour...but maybe there would be an answer when he awoke.

Tanya had set Rob's ship to follow her own in formation, once they left Gas Miner Naha.

She sighed as the huge metallic form of the permanently-stationed hydrogen gas collector receded into the distance, as their Falcon's sped away at cruise speed.

Rob's fully helmeted image appeared in the top left of her HUD and she noted how the freelancer's head seemed to be bobbing up and down slightly in a drowsy fashion.

"Guess we made it," he managed to whisper, somewhat weakly than before.

Tanya knew the shot wasn't fatal and had stemmed the flow of blood using a 'slap-patch'. Minor surgery was needed to remove the bullet, but from her initial diagnosis that's something she could accomplish on her own. Nothing some stiches, alcohol and a burning hot blade wouldn't fix.

"Yes. We did," said Tanya still concentrating on staying aware. That young bounty hunter named Dev seemed excitable, but was harmless enough. She understood Rob's reasoning behind his actions, Dev would be too keen to get involved in their business and possibly, become more trouble than necessary. Still, she wondered if Rob's actions created another enemy she didn't need.

Of more concern was the Hessian currently lounging in her cargo hold. She turned on the vidcam to check how he was doing and smiled gratefully when his prone form appeared at the centre of the loading bay.

"Rob. We need to learn more about the man in my cargo bay," she said, frowning when she received no response after a few moments. "Rob. Rob? Are you there?"

No image flashed up on her HUD and she began to worry. Tanya quickly punched up a navmap of the local area. She knew of a Junker base in the area and though they weren't particularly friendly towards her, they certainly weren't hostile either. Tanya swiftly changed course, checked Rob's ship was still on autopilot then hailed the station.

"Yanagi Depot, this is freelancer zero-dash-alpha-two, requesting landing clearance for me and my escort."

The reply followed a static filled pause.

"This is Yanagi Depot, we have you on our scanners and you're both cleared to land, freelancer. Have a nice day."

Tanya nodded and as the station came into view, let the automatic docking sequence take over.

Tanya and the now-awakened Rob stepped into the cargo hold of Athena's ship, gazing at the pale young man still comatose therein. He was dressed in black silk, his shirt open at the collarbone that revealed even more ink tattooed to his skin.

Rob frowned. "I've never seen a Hessian so obsessed with writing images all over himself. You picked up a weird one."

Tanya laughed quietly. "He was good. Not like Hideko, but very good. And yet..."

Rob silenced her and drew his weapon, pointing towards the Hessian. He was stirring. Tanya brushed past him, drawing her own weapon and clenching her fists. The man's eyes flashed open. He stared into the cold metal gun before his eyes, the fierce woman preparing to strike him, then sighed and rolled onto his back. "I thought I was dead. Why must I awake again?" he said, to noone in particular. "You, Miss Williams, are a merciful woman. And even more beautiful than I thought." (still to the cieling of the cargo hold). He rolled over to his side again, feeling her green eyes bore into his black. His mouth creased into a smile.

Rob stepped forward. "Please, do not take offence. I am...how do you say...taken. I was merely stating the truth," the man uttered. Rob nodded. "Time for some explanations. Who do you work for, and why did you try to kill Tanya?"

A smirk passed across the man's lips. "I work for that which is right, that which is wise, and the man whom I love. "

"Go on...You're a Hessian, aren't you?"

"Please, I do not wish to be associated with the Red Hessians. I fight for the Bundschuh--and Mason sent me to kill Tanya. Please--I did it against my will, to save another...I have done wrong. That is why I stopped. Forgive me..." Tears once again formed in his eyes, and pain seared across his rugged face.

Tanya turned to Rob. "I think we should trust him."

Rob sighed. "Fine. He doesn't seem like too much trouble. He may even be helpful..."

She turned back to the Bundschuh. "What's your name?"

The man stood for the first time, stepped out of the cargo hold, and tied his hair back behind his head. "Damien Solkovsky. I am in your service. Tanya...and..."

Rob extended his hand. "Rob Schaefer. But my questions still aren't answered--why did Mason ask you to kill Tanya? Why did you accept?"

Damien looked at them both, and stepped out of the cargo hold. "It would be better to talk this through over a shot of vodka--but not here. Honshu. Let me just travel back to Naha to retrieve my ship."

Tanya nodded. "Agreed."

In the bar of the Dragon Palace on Planet Honshu, having safely retrieved Ocean Soul, Damien told them everything--his confrontation with Mason, his own obsession with philosophy, beauty, and morality, his relationship with the young man Nikolai. He listened in turn to Tanya's story of how she came to be on the trail of Mason, her relationships with Rutger and Travis, and how she ended up wandering around with Rob--who for nearly the entire conversation had been dead silent.
