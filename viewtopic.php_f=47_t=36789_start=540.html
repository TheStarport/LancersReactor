Codename

Mon Dec 27, 2004 9:24 pm by Codename

Wilde, I'll handle it. You fill in anything I miss, OK?

The problem is that the battle system as it currently stands is flawed. It prevents people from even carrying out their basic beginning of turn actions if a player posts a battle in one of their systems before the other person has posted his turn. Take me for example. Because Wilde attacked me in those various systems I was denied the ability to make moves I had planned all along. I had a feeling Wilde was going to give up on Dresden and go for some sort of all out assault (because of what happened last turn) and figured I'd reinforce my position accordingly. Unfortunately for me Wilde posted his turn first effectively preventing me from carrying out actions I had planned yesterday. "Tough cookies" you might say but in this case I'd have to disagree. It underscores a fundamental error in the system. While we all have a certain amount of free time now, I expect it will begin to diminish next week. Then we will find ourselves in a situation where the people who win the game are those who happen to have the most spare time on their hands. They will be able to post their turns first, lock people up in their own systems and generally cause havoc amongst the rest of the players by "spoofing" (that's the term I'll use for launching a tiny attack with the sole purpose of locking a system down). We have to change the rules so all players are on even footing regardless of their RL commitments. Otherwise it isn't really a fair system.

There are two ways we can change the rules. One is to say that once battle is declared no movements may be made except those made at the start of a turn. In other words, make it that even though Wilde attacked me I'm still allowed to place and move as if the systems were all open. The other choice is to institute a rule that prohibits battles from being declared until both parties have posted their primary turn information. What we can't do is leave the rules as is.

And WTF are we supposed to do if this doesn't get resolved? Pretend it never happened? Continue in this state of limbo? I need answers fer chrissakes!
