Harrier,

I found a visual of the camo I was talking about. It's properly called a "lozenge pattern" after the shape of the individual blocks of color. The idea, aside from the basic goal of all camouflage (outline breakup) is that it confuses the eye.

If you want to try it, take several dominant colors from a system, offset them a fair bit, and model the "lozenge" pattern with them. The effect being that the ship will visually "disappear" to an extent at range. The effect won't work so well in other systems with different dominant colors, naturally.
