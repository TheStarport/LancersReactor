Mine dropper:

An important weapon for any lone ship, crucial for freighters.
Homing mines did seem to work better against nimble fighters, they go 80m/s
I used Razor, about the right cost/effectiveness.


Upgrade to Driller if your foes get tougher, but if you feel the need for Cutters
then you're playing in too tough a neighborhood for a freighter.

Many players don't properly use mines in the Humpback. Here's some advice.


Target Prioritization and Mine Deployment:

The most important thing is that mines are for providing the ability to engage
a secondary enemy simultaneously with your primary target. Your primary target
is the enemy pacing you above, below, or to the side of you. They match speed
and pour their guns into you unless you engage them with your turrets. 2nd is
the enemy that is behind your ship, the target of your mines. One critical
consideration in mine deployment is never drop one if your afterburner is fully
depleted. You want to get away from your mine as quickly as possible, as it can
be hit by enemy fire and exploded too close to your own ship with disastrous
results. Try to drop mines in lulls of enemy fire if possible. If you're @ 200m/s
drop back to 80 when you drop the mine, hit thrust immediately afterwards. Don't
drop a mine at an enemy you are engaging with your turrets. Not only is it
redundant effort but you can shoot your own mine by accident.

In Humpback Turret Mode you get to watch what other pilots seldom witness;
your mine homing in and destroying an enemy. Satisfying, but the main goal of
mines is to force them to break off their attack.

What's the best Humpback?

That's simple.

If you're not the missiles type: Load it out completely with Sunblast A guns and turrets. The Humpback can barely power that.

If you're into missiles: Load it out with 2 missile launchers, and the rest max it out with Sunblast B and Sunblast A turrets, whatever the level will allow. The loss of the two guns will cut your power drain enough to handle the Sunblast Bs.

Sunblast is the untimate choice for the Humpback because it's FAST, high fire rate and fairly deadly.
