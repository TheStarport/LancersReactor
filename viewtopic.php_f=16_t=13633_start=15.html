If you actually watch battles between Corsairs and other people, you'll generally see the Corsairs take on 2-3x their number....and win. The only time when this isn't true is when the Corsairs either try to 1) kill a base, or 2) kill you. In both circumstances, the outcome is usually unfavorable to them. If the Corsairs left a 3-fighter garrison of Titans at Crete, I would bet on them against anything short of the full Rheinland Navy, and even then, I would think twice before betting against the Corsairs.

I mean, the Bounty Hunters ships tend to suck - you can slice through wave after wave of 8-10 ships with ease, where trying the same trick against the Corsairs usually results in some SERIOUS nanobot action if not complete destruction. The GMG, while they're decent against humans, also suck. The AI is simply not designed for computer-to-computer battles, it's designed to kill common human tactics. Running the AI against itself, you usually end with the losing side being the one with crappier equipment, regardless of numbers. In this case, the Corsairs stuff is simply better than the GMG's, so, in theory, any number of Corsairs will always beat any number of GMG.
