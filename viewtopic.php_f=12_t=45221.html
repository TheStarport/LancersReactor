Heh. Believe me, i know how it feels. at the start of last year, my (now Ex-) school got a new batch script that pulled usage data from the IIS server.
I just happened to be the #1 data hog (I think i accounted for something between 26% and 30% of total net-bound traffic).
Not only that, the top two websites on my list were Everything2 (How a text-based site managed to make #1 to this day eludes me), and TLR. Amazingly, Wikipedia was #6 on that list.

If i had to make a reccomendation, i'd suggest using Remote Desktop connection to connect to your home computer (Requires you have XP Pro on your home computer and you know your home WAN IP), then use the remote connection to browse unrestricted. Of course, you would have to seriously cut back on the quality settings on the RDC program as to not create a noticable bandwidth use. Typically, i've found that people don't know what the program is, so the IT operators don't lock it down. I'd say give it a try if you have the resources to do it
