You can control the difficulty to some extent: use a light fighter if you want it easy, use a heavy one if you want it hard.

The light fighters have almost the same shield capacity, armour and powerplant as the heavy fighters of the same class - the difference is just a few percent. So it is most definitely not true that a heavy fighter can take much more punishment than a light one; it can take only marginally more. But the heavy fighters have to take much more punishment than the light fighters because they are too slow to dodge enemy fire effectively. A heavy fighter would need a shield at least two classes higher in order to get the same 'survivabililty' as one of the highly manoeuverable light fighters but they have to make do with the same shield. That is why it is harder to fly the campaign mission in a HF (or a freighter, for that matter ).

The only advantage of the heavy fighters is that they can use torpedo launchers and that they have more hardpoints which means they can use more than one missile launcher without sacrificing too much fire power from guns. Against stationary or slow-moving targets (or AI ships during initial approach) they can do twice the damage compared to a light fighter, while the ammo lasts. But on the whole the HF cannot take out enemy fighters fast enough to compensate for their bad survivability.

Not all light fighters are equal, though. The best choices for the campaign are Patriot, Cavalier and Drake. The Hawk is not bad but it cannot match the Drake.
