Blitz

Fri Mar 07, 2003 8:22 pm by Blitz

Some ships will have better shields than their class of weaponry. Liberty and Corsair ships have this feature. Others, such as bounty hunter ships, have high powered weaponry, but a lower class of shield. In general, the harder-to-find ships will still be better than their house counterparts, but the house ships will still be stronger in some areas.

Basic premises of the various classes of ships

Liberty - Strong shields
Bretonia - good cargo
Rhineland - Good armor
Kusari - Strong weaponry
Bounty Hunters - Stong weaponry, weaker shields
Corsairs - Strong shields and armor, weaker weaponry
Civillian - Balanced, with higher cargo capacity
Pirate - balanced, but weaker than average ships

- Freighters will carry 100-150 cargo.
- The Crusader now handles 60 cargo, most of any fighter.
- Armor on freighters has been significantly strengthened, as have the shielding and weapons systems.
- XHF class ships have been significantly weakened, and their cargo capacities vastly reduced. They are by far the best fighters still, but have limitations.
- All ships are at least class 5 except the starflier (3) and startracker (4).

There's only one fighter with cargo capacity larger than 45, and thats the Crusader. It's got 60 cargo capacity, and can mount class 7 weapons. Three of the six freighters also can mount class 7 weapons, and all have larger cargo capacity and stronger armor. The Crusader is designed as a hybrid shp. It does not approach the cargo carrying or durability of the freighters. It is simply a ship with the maneuverability of a heavyfighter which can carry more cargo than your average fighter. In short, it will neither fight as well as a comparable fighter, or transport cargo as well as a freighter. But it can do both reasonably well.
