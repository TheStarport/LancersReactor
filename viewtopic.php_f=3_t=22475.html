Mon Aug 04, 2003 8:09 pm by NukeIt

airplane: a winged(or lifting body) craft, designed for and capable of atmospheric travel, can be pressurized and airtight, but NOT capable of exiting that atmosphere, or of re-entry into it. Usually propelled by propeller, rocket, or jet based engines.

spaceship: designed for and capable of travel outside a planet's atmosphere. It IS possible to have an un-pressurized spaceship. May or may not be capable of re-entry, may or may not be capable of reaching escape velocity. Uses chemical(either solid or liquid fuel) or nuclear rocket engines and/or a series of reaction control thrusters driven by compressed gasses.

The ships in FL are spaceships, not airplanes, because they ARE capable of space travel.

BardicHeart

Tue Aug 05, 2003 3:20 am by BardicHeart

I *think* it was probably a design decision based on the story line. You start in New York so things were kept 'low level' there and as you moved to new areas they became progressively tougher with a corresponding increase in ship capabilities. You see this increase across the board (so to speak), in weapons tech, economy, mission rewards, etc. Apparently the game's designers didn't consider how this would affect MP mode.

To be honest, I don't think they gave a whole lot of thought to MP, there are a number of things they certainly didn't seem to consider outside of the story plot.
