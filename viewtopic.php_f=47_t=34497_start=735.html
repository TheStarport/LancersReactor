ww2jacob

Thu Oct 28, 2004 6:48 pm by ww2jacob

ww2jacob, on the bridge of the Empire, gave the order to fire. Within a few seconds, space was filled with MAC shots from the HA, and energy lances and torpedos from the Axis. He smiled. Larings had no chance of dodging this.

Larings saw the shots coming, and raised his arm. Dozens of torpedos and MAC rounds froze in space. He then flicked his finger, and two MACs flew back and cut through the Hispanic ships they had launched from. The rounds continued, destroying several other ships.

The energy beams were too close. He purposely let a few hit him, and then quickly dodged the rest, Matrix style. His power was growing.

Once all the beams had gone past, Larings lashed out with an attack of his own. He took two Hispanic cruisers, and slammed them together, crushing them as flat as pancakes. He then drained the oxygen out of a Juggernaut, and the crew suffocated within seconds. He tried to enter the computers of the NR ships, but he was unable to do anything, because the AIs had been removed. Angry, he cast a beam of energy, which effortlessly cut through ten NR Warships. The ships then began launching fighters, which Larings quickly destroyed. No more fighters left the ships.

Another volley was fired by the Sirius forces, again, Larings escaped unscathed. Larings lashed out yet again, crushing another large number of Sirius ships. It was becoming more and more obvious that the Sirius fleet would not be able to stop Larings, and a retreat was being discussed. Larings was far more powerful than expected, and he either dodged, or added their shots at him to his ever-growing shield.

After the next volley, Larings identified a new target, one that had been providing a large amount of the fire that had been coming at him. It was an incredibly large Warship. Larings did not know, but that was the Empire, the flagship of the NR fleet, and ww2jacob's personal ship.

Larings sent a huge bolt of electricity at the ship, and it fried many of the ship's systems, and killed hundreds of crewmen.

************************************************************************************************

ww2jacob lay dying on the bridge of the Empire. The lightning had come so fast, there was no way to anticipate it. His bridge crew was all dead or dying, and he knew that he was not in much of a better condition.

He knew that he was responsible for many of the deaths in this war, too many had died at his hands. He had pushed too hard for total victory, and had ignored the costs to humanity. Now, with moments left, he had the insight to see what had happened. He knew what he had to do. He moved towards the pilot's console.

************************************************************************************************

Larings was wreaking pure destruction on the Sirius fleets. A smile had formed on his face. He was going to be the ruler of Sirius. Soon, the fleets would have had enough, and would flee. He doubted he would ever have to deal with them again. Victory would be his, very soon.

Larings was so wrapped up in these thoughts, and in the enjoyment of killing the enemy, that he didn't notice the Empire begin to move forward. The ship, or what reamined of it, picked up speed. It moved closer and closer to Larings. Still, Larings had no idea what was about to happen.

*************************************************************************************************

On the bridge of the Empire, ww2jacob smiled. His debt had been repaid. He prepared for the journey to meet his creator.

*************************************************************************************************

Larings turned around, a split second before the Empire hit him. A look of pure suprise and fear was the last thing ww2jacob saw. The last thing that Larings saw was the grinning and pale face of ww2jacob.

The Empire connected. The force of the impact caused Larings's shield to implode, and Larings went out with a huge explosion. The blast rocked the New Madrid system, and the survivors knew that they had victory. Not for the Axis, or the Allience, but for all of Sirius.

************************************************************************************************

Well, did you like that? An incredible ending for an incredible RPG.
We will wrap up, and then I will start the 2nd part, on a new thread, sometime tommorow.

To Wilde:

I had to read your post a couple times to make sure I had gotten it right. But no matter how many times I checked, it still said the same thing: that it was better to let the rp stagnate.

I still can't really believe I read that. I'm not trying to kill the rp, Wilde. This first round was already dying - the Axis/Alliance war that had been the focus of it's saga was coming to an end, factions already had navies so large that there was no plausible way to invent a new threat that could challenge them. The first round was pretty much over.

There are two kinds of rps, Wilde. There are those that have staying power, those that can last months, even years; and there are those that lack that power, that will die out after maybe a couple of weeks. This is the first kind, an rp that could easily last for years if we help it.

Regardless, sometimes even the best rps need a boost, a way to bring them back to a point in time where you can DO something. Nobody wants to sit there and watch as some uber-navy, built up over the course of the entire game, decimates every single other faction.

Outcast himself said this first round was only a test, to see if the idea worked. Guess what - it did. Now we get a chance to put that idea into a new framework, ironing out the little details and glitches. Stop whining about me being a psychomaniac or whatever it was you called me, and try to understand that sometimes you have to start again.
