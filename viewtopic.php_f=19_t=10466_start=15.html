Mon Mar 17, 2003 7:55 pm by RunOrDie

I've been playing MP for a while now, started from scratch several times, and I've never been "jumped by 8-15 fighters", especially never around Manhattan. In fact I've never been jumped by more than 8 fighters in Liberty space, and the only times that happened is because two different faction patrols of 3 or 4 ships each happened to cross each other and I happened to be there.

I agree completely though, the Starflier can't really hold its own if you're against 4 or more enemies with those cheap weapons you start the game with, and you're not a relatively good pilot. Your first priority should be to head to the nearest bar, get yourself an easy level 1 or 2 mission, win it, and repeat the same process over and over, progressively upgrading your weapons and ship.

Once you get a Patriot, with a Class 2 or better shield, and some Lavablades, nothing really should be able to touch you in the core Liberty systems. Especially since cruise-disruptor-wielding enemies are few and far between, and you can easily run away from a nasty situation (refer to the several "fast docking" to trade lanes or jumpgates posts floating around).

The AI itself can easily be thrown off if you use afterburners (the TAB key) often in combat. If you find yourself being repeatedly pounded from all sides, hit the afterburner and don't fly straight. And if you really feel like pulling a frontal attack on 4 incoming enemies, remember you can strafe left and right.

To the original poster:

Here's my typical opening. Sell a justice mk 1 and promptly buy 2 justice Mk2's. This is better than getting a single Justice Mk3 (24 hull compared to 2x20 hull). Do a few instant gratification missions (assassinations are typically easy). Get approximately 40000 creds (you might have to upgrade to a patriot or some other cheaply bought fighter) then purchase a rhino. Start doing trading runs that are EXCLUSIVE to the liberty system. It's not bad if you don't mind taking your time. Sure it's not the most profitable runs, but it's something. Add to that is you won't be attacked by pkers (at least on the Elite server or any other that actually enforces it's pvp rules). That's just one case of an early game...but so far it's worked fine with me. Other ppl have different early games, but's that's part of the fun: finding a method that you like on your own. Good luck to you. Cheers.
