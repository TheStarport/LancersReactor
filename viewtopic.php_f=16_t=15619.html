Chandrasekhar Limit

Tue Jun 10, 2003 9:40 pm by Chandrasekhar Limit

Nope... Protostars are pretty durn big-- MUCH bigger than planets.

Basically protostars are accreting gaseous material in order to reach a certain critical mass where its gravity would be strong enough to cause nuclear fusion to occur in the core.

Failed protostars that couldn't get enough mass to light up are brown dwarves, and these are massive-- We are talking multiple Jupiter masses.

And yes, stars are born in EGGs-- Evaporating Gaseous Globules. These are seen in that famous Hubble photo of the Eagle Nebula (Messier object M16-- The Pillars of Creation):
