@ Wolfie,

You know. You really need to try looking things up just a little harder.

Anyhoooo.

There is a Chancellor of today's Germany (Kanzler). He's the #1 political guy. AND, there is a designated Vice Chancellor of Germany for the purposes of succession (Vizekanzler). The point is that the Vice Chancellor is not a specifically constituted office. It is a responsibility conferred upon one of the Chancellor's Cabinet ministers. Usually, but not always, the Foreign Minister. So, depending upon the regime in power, the Vice Chancellor more likely also is the Foreign Minister. In the event of the untimely death of the German Chancellor, the Foreign Minister, usually, also holds the title of Vizekanzler and will take on the reponsibilities of Kanzler for some set period of time.
