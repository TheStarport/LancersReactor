Dim matter; i beleive it is the unaccounted for matter that does not fall into the category of "dark" matter. Examples would be "brown" dwarfs, stars that never get enough mass enough to initiate nuclear fusion reactions, but are warm enough to emit detectable IR wavelengths. or it could be mini-black holes which although undetectable of themselves, have EM emissions coming from the matter that falls into their event horizons.

to be fair, "dark" matter is a catch-all term used (wrongly) to label all undetected matter in the universe. there may be many different sorts of dark matter or the undetected matter may be something else like neutrinos or theoretical WIMPs. all we really know is that something is exerting gravitational force but we can't find the something itself, and that the mass of most galaxies is 5-10 times greater than can be accounted for by visible matter (the mass-to-light ratio) yet this undetectable "stuff" is binding the galaxies together, otherwise they'd fly apart.
