Indy11

Wed Feb 25, 2004 8:13 am by Indy11

According to the spec's that have been published......

There should be no real difference in voltage between Athlon Thoroughbreds and Pentium Northwoods. Both being manufactured at .13 micron thickness, the respective core voltages are at 1.5v.

Intel's Prescotts are .09microns or 90nm so the core voltages are expected to be lower, around 1.25v.

The initial batch of Athlon 64s are made with .13 micron wafers so they will not run as cool as later production runs which are to be .09 microns once AMD upgrades its production equipment.

Usually, though, as soon as the core voltage heat generation is lowered, they ramp up the operating herz to take up the new "give" so there's no actual net heat reduction.
