Ok, The battle at willard station. Destroy the gunboats first, then the bombers, then ALL remaining enemy ships. Once the last ship is destroyed, Juni will tell you she will contact you later. If you need to know how many credits you need to earn to get to the next level. Click on the (Player status) icon. Did the distress message appear and Walker respond to the caller before rushing to Willard?

Perhaps you arrived too soon and managed to destroy all the enemy ships before Walker even complained about gunboats being too small for their cruiser's turrets. In this case, you'd be stuck, even if the bombers were finally arrived and you destroyed them.

If the above is true, my suggestion is: Don't kill all the enemy ships. Leave at least 1 gunboat and 5 fighters for Walker and his men to fight. Go for the last gunboat after Walker complained about it. Also, don't destroy the remaining fighters until Walker warned about the incoming bombers; kill the bombers first then the remaining fighters, if any still survived.

Other than that, my only guess is that there's a bug somewhere.

Sherlog

Thu Sep 11, 2003 11:52 pm by Sherlog

If you destroy all enemies in the Willard skirmish well before Walker complains about the gunboats being too fast for the turrets of his cruisers then the mission ends prematurely. Juni will declare the mission over and transfer the payment, and even though the 3 Rheinland Bombers will spawn later as usual they are no longer part of the mission.

However, if you are too slow and the last enemy is destroyed only very shortly before Walker's remark re gunboats then two conflicting dialogue/trigger sequences interleave and the mission will never end. OTOH, if you are even slower and there is at least one enemy alive when Walker complains then you should be safe. IOW, an unusual game script bug involving a race condition.
