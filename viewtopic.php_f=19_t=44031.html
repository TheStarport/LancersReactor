Tue Jul 04, 2006 9:05 am by bakedpotato

One thing you can do is connect directly to the server, bypassing the global server.
You will need the IP and port number of the server to do this.

Right click on your Freelancer Icon. The Icon (shortcut) that starts Freelancer.
Select Properties
When the Freelancer Properties comes up select the shortcut tab (if it's not already selected).
In the target box you will see
"C:\Program Files\Microsoft Games\Freelancer\EXE\Freelancer.exe"
Add -sxx.xx.xx.xxx.xxxx at the end - replacing the x's with the server IP and port number.

So it looks like this
"C:\Program Files\Microsoft Games\Freelancer\EXE\Freelancer.exe -s65.67.82.238:2302"

cjabates

Tue Jul 04, 2006 4:42 pm by cjabates

some lag is caused server-side, depending on what anti-cheat software they have loaded.
some lag is caused by the network connection at the server's end.

some lag is caused by other computers at your end using your bandwidth.
some lag is caused by other programs (even ones you might not think would cause lag) running on your computer.

the ping time is the total time it takes for an FL packet to be sent to the server, handled by the server, returned to your computer, AND handled by FL on your computer, so anything you can do on your end to reduce the amount of time your computer needs to complete handling of the packets will reduce your ping, and hence, your lag.
