Sherlog

Sat Sep 20, 2003 12:24 am by Sherlog

MorganB, as far as I know none of the mods add story lines similar to the singleplayer campaign. However, you've just barely started singleplayer FL and it would be wrong to switch to mods so soon. You have yet to try all ships and weapons, find out how the reputation/faction system works and how it affects you as a fighter or as a trader, get an overview of the Sirius systems, find out where all the factions really stand in the scheme of things, and so on. A stint of trading is mandatory, as is having flown the Humpy for a while. After that you may be ready to learn how to fly & fight, and you will be ready to discover fast & efficient ways of getting rich, how to choose ships/weapons and use them effectively, or how to manipulate the faction system to achieve your ends quickly ... Having completed the tutorial does not mean 'game over' by any means.

Sherlog

Sun Sep 21, 2003 8:08 pm by Sherlog

MorganB, the Banshee is just a normal light fighter, not an exceptional one like Starflier, Patriot and Drake. You have to have normal ships in a a game, to make the best ones stand out. An additional drawback of the Banshee is that there are no excellent class 7 guns (like the Xeno/Outcast particle guns in class 5 and 6), and so the only way to utilize the class 7 slots to advantage is putting a Debilitator and a Lancer missile launcher there. Fill the class 6 slots with either Xeno particle guns (Adv. Tarantula) or GMG photon guns, and you get a package that mows down enemies much faster than the Drake. It is a bit more expensive to operate than a Drake with class 5/6 Tarantulas, but it is fun nonetheless. An alternative would be using a class 7 GMG photon gun instead of the Lancer missile launcher, but it is not as much fun. The Banshee has a surprisingly beefy powerplant but as there are no excellent class 7 guns (and no class 6 shield buster guns either) it is difficult to exploit this advantage.
