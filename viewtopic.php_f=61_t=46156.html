Rankor

Tue Jul 24, 2007 7:24 am by Rankor

Chris, you can check the physical size of your ship by using the hard cmp editor.
With that tool, you can open the cmp file for your ship and view it in 3d wire and textured formats.
If you have to change the size of the ship, FLmodel tool does that job nicely.

Another thing you must also consider is whether or not your shiparch.ini is referencing your ship cmp or 3db file.

I learned from previous mistakes that when I was going to create a 3db file, was to never use the cmp file for that. That requires opening the mat file and then saving it "as" a 3db file. Such a mistake has cause ships not to appear and even sometime cause the game to crash.

Rankor

Wed Jul 25, 2007 1:19 pm by Rankor

This may seem a little silly to ask this..
but...
check your SHIPS folder and make sure you have your ship folder in it.
In that folder, make sure you have the cmp, mat & 3db file.
Depending on where your cockpit.ini is kept, just make sure it and the ship is correctly reference in the shiparch.ini .

there are areas (or files) that the ship must be declared in and referenced to.
SHIPS folder:
shiparch.ini (this declares where the cmp, mat, 3db and cockpit files are that the game can find them) As well as describes the ship stats, what can be mounted on it and references the ids names & info.
-----------------------------------------
EQUIPMENT folder:
goods.ini (Two entries for your ship. One is the price of the basic hull and the other declares what mounted equipment is "added on" to the price of the ship)

marketships.ini (declares where you ship is sold)

loadouts.ini (declares what is mounted on your ship when it's bought)


IMPORTANT! Make sure the spelling is the same for all your entries.
