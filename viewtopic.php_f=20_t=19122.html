Kbuhln, on your spec sheet, you didn't list a sound card type, but you might wnat to check and see it your sound card and the video card are sharing the same IRQ's and memory addresses. Easiest way to check that is that on your desktop, click Start->Run->type "msinfo32" (without the quotes)->click on the + next to Hardware Resources->IRQs. If it's not sharing an IRQ, then go to Conflicts/Sharing and check to see that it is not sharing any memory addresses. If this doesn't help you out, post back here and I'll keep digging around for more. Zapie, same goes for you on the sound card, because certain sound cards will conflict with the video card you have. I just retired mine because the GPU burned up. I was sharing an IRQ with my SB PCI 512. I'm now running with an Asylum GeForceFX 5200 Ultra and a SB Live 5.1, and I haven't experienced any problems yet (178 hours of play time!!!!).

Hint: Don't have anything in the PCI port next to the AGP port, because your BIOS will have a tendancy of having those 2 ports share and IRQ and memory addresses together. Trust me, I found out the hard way by burning up my nVidia GeForce Ti 4200 and my SB PCI 512!

Kbuhln, I figured out what might be causing your problems. Based on what you've posted, you have a VIA-powered motherboard with an intergrated AC'97 sound card right? Well, after consulting with my t/s team, we might have a solution for you. You need to identify your motherboard manufacturer. Go to www.guru3d.com and download the Sandra 2003 freeware program. Use it to identify the m/b and then goto the m/b manufacturer website and download their latest CMOS/BIOS driver for your m/b. Flash your BIOS which will update the on-board audio codec that the AC'97 uses, then go to www.viaarena.com and download the "4-in-1 VIA Hyperion Driver Update". Contained in this update is the new Win XP drivers to correct the problems with the AC'97 conflicting with the audio codecs used by WinXP. After doing all this, you should be good to go.
