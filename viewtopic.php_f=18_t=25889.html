Wed Nov 05, 2003 6:48 pm by Chips

The only thing is that you can bung in hundreds of systems - but to make them interesting enough to a) Fly in
b) Want to fly in
c) Stay in

takes time, and lots of time.

Systems require a great attention to detail, and immagination in the layout to make them playable. You could simply churn out hundreds of systems in a few minutes, but to populate the bases, add and check the missions work, check all the details of wrecks, zones etc etc. It takes a long long time. Also - systems can prove to be very buggy, so it requires someone with alot of time to create the systems and make them interesting enough to play in!

Ricardius

Tue Nov 11, 2003 12:59 am by Ricardius

Was on a buisness trip over these past few days and I had this very same idea. I was wondering if there was anyone out there just making new, strictly Freelancer...(no Babylon 5; Star Wars; Star Trek etc....) systems to explore, play and stay in? No big massive mods, but simple systems to add to the existing game?

I figured that new system mods, could be created, and intergarted in to the existing game as simple system expansions. Example: I created The Wyoming Territory for my personal game. Located it outside, next to, the Colorado System and use it as a home for Trent. Nothing special since it's just for me. But...think, if modders created systems, with all the fun stuff with it, then systems could be played and used at will by players looking for a personalized game expansion.

All of us could create new systems for ourselves, but it's just no fun if you know all the about that system and it's playablity. However, if we created systems and swapped them about, then we could have a small level trade section for new system game expansions. Think of it as trading cards and building the ultimate playable deck. It would, could, make the game grow in size, without detracting from a strickly Freelancer game environment.

Frankly, we all know that the ships make the game fun, but it's the systems that make the game interesting.

I know it's a "thought in process" ramble, but do all kinda get the idea?
