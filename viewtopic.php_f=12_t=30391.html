Tawakalna

Tue Apr 13, 2004 1:20 pm by Tawakalna

Ed's right. the usual cause for this, if it's not fragged to f***, is too much stuff running in the background, eating up RAM. The second usual cause is too little RAM (ok that's much the same, but some base systems which work fine on spec, can't really cope with the demands being made on them) This latter is esp true of the dreaded shared memory mobos (usual oem and branded stuff) WHERE THE ram provided XMS and also VRAM. Thirdly, antivirus progs installed after the pcs been purchased, if hamfistedly installed can be tremendous drain or resources. The fourth and also v v common reason is the hard drive may be so full as to not allow the paging file to expand and contract properly. There may also be corruption of ActiveX components which cause slowness and lack of response, also very common in home systems.
