Solar Pilot

Wed Aug 04, 2004 12:32 pm by Solar Pilot

Alistair Grey/Cavalier/California Jump Gate, Magellan System/Bretonia Police Authority

(Note: Figured I might as well give the Bretonian constable a life of his own, and I know a new Bretonian was needed, so... here we go.)

Chief Constable Alistair Grey of the Bretonia Police Authority was not a happy man at the moment. Right now, his entire world consisted of the brig cell he'd been confined to on the BAF Yorkshire . He figured this might happen though. Sending an encrypted communique to an LPI captain was bound to attract attention. He just didn't think it'd attract the attention of Admiral Winston, the commander presiding over the invasion of the California System.

As he heard the klaxons continue to sound on the ship, Grey felt empty. This felt wrong. Even if he did answer to the Crown of Bretonia in the end, it still didn't sanction the invasion of another House in Sirius. He'd tried to prevent Admiral Winston from conscripting his BPA Cavalier squadrons into the battle, but that didn't happen when a pair of BAF security officers arrested the constable on charges of "high treason against the crown." With a charge like that, Grey knew it was only a matter of time before he was sent to Prison Station Newgate, or worse executed by a BAF firing squad.

Looking through the glowing forcefield, he gazed over the pair of security officers on duty sitting at the control desk. He knew they didn't like him. If they had their way, they'd toss him out an airlock at their earliest convenience. There never really was much animosity between the BPA and the BAF, not as much as there might've been in the Liberty equivalents of the organizations.

Suddenly, the blast doors from outside the brig opened, and three uniformed BPA officers stepped inside... stun-sticks at the ready! One of the security guards cursed as he tried to reach for his gun. But the three BPA officers had closed the distance across the room in mere blinks of an eye, tapping and stunning both security guards with the large, bulky wands. One of them worked the master console at the desk, soon enough deactivating the forcefield holding Grey in his cell. He stood off of his bench and stepped out, approaching the other three officers.

One of them saluted Grey. "Sir, we've managed to... delay Red Flight's launch, but we can't keep it off for long. And these guards won't stay knocked out forever."
"Right," Grey said, nodding as he came to the weapons locker, taking four blasters out and tossing one to each of the officers. He checked the charge on his. "Set weapons on stun. These are our countrymen after all." They all complied and rushed out, sprinting down several decks to the hangar bay. Resting on the flight deck, now devoid of BAF Crusaders, sat twelve BPA Cavaliers... eight of which were active and waiting, the remaining four belonging to Grey and his three liberators, the remainder of Red Flight 1. Not questioning orders, the pilots entered their fighters, powered up, and began to move towards the hangar doors.

"This is Bretonia Police Red 1-2," Grey's wingman announced. "Requesting permission to launch."
"About bloody time," the coordinator on the bridge replied. "You're clear to launch, Red Squadron."
Smirking, Grey keyed his comm. "All right, gentlemen. We launch, and then we make for the California Gate. Evans?"
Red 2-4 responded. Evans was a crack computer systems specialist. "Yes, sir?"
"Once we're there, you're to try to tap into the jump gate's systems. See if you can disable the gate, keep our boys on this side, clear?"
"Yes, sir."
"All right then, let's move." The hangar doors were open by now. The twelve Cavaliers streaked out one after another in quick succession, making for the California Jump Gate immediately. For now, there were only two squadrons of BAF Crusaders, but four more squadrons of Bloodhounds. Liberty Rogues.

Grey felt tense now. He knew they might not succeed, but for the sake of all those civilians in California, he knew they had to try. As they made their way towards the gate, passing the queued invading fighters, things seemed eerily quiet. No one seemed to pay them any heed, not even the Rogues. Finally, Red 2-4 got close enough to the jump gate to tap into its systems.

"Sir, unless I have the seizing codes, it's going to take me five minutes," Evans had reported.
"Get started," Grey replied, watching the waiting fighters tensely.

Suddenly, a new alert sounded over the open channel. "Red alert! Liberty fighter entering system!" Grey checked his sensors and paled at the readout:

Liberty Police Red 1-1.

Edwards!

And 24 Crusaders and 48 Bloodhounds were now starting to wake up and bear down on the Patriot.

Jack Simmons/Dagger/Liberty Rogues

Jack sat down at the bar, his dagger was in for repairs and he needed a drink anyway after a day's cardamine running. He knew all about it, the war and all, and wondered how it would end up. If Bretonia won, he would have a much harder time in his endeavours but, equally, if Liberty emerged victorious, he was sure Jacobi would stab them in the back if they helped her. It had happened before. He would not help Bretonia but he was no patriot either. His mind was made up: life would continue as normal.

"Er..., fella; you've been sitting there for ten minutes. Buy a drink, or get out," said the bartender.

"Oh...oh yeah, I'll have a Fireburst," he replied.

Liberty was a police state these days, what with the nomad crisis. Smuggling artifacts was now harder then getting cardamine from A to B, for god's sake. He would rather not touch the stuff, but money was money, especially in these hard times. His Fireburst was ready, and it tasted good. Just as he continued brooding over the matter, his friend Max came in. Max was just fitting into his new life at Buffalo, and only had a Bloodhound, but Jack could see that he had talent, and might prove useful. Both of them shared a devotion duty and had never failed a mission yet. The boss liked that, and the boss liked them.

"Hey, Max. Wanna drink?" asked Jack.

"No, I'm good," he replied. He seemed distracted.

"You OK?"

Jack knew that something was wrong, but not what it was. Max usually had a drink. But it couldn't be that bad. His life was mainly full of ups.

"They grounded it. They grounded my ship! Just because its middle fin was missing. It'll take a day to repair. A day! What will the boss say?" Max replied, finally.

"The boss'll understand. Don't worry. Here, have some credits. There's plenty to do here."

"That's not the point! I was so close. So close!"

"Close to what?"

"The Flint! If I bring it in, I'm sure to get a promotion."

"What happened?"

"Rogue asteroid. Huge. Lucky it only clipped me."

"Tell ya what, buddy. Come along in my dagger: it'll be ready in a few minutes."

"OK, sure. But I want 50% of the profit."

"Sure! You crack me up, Max. Of course you'll get half."

They'd done a good job: his dagger was gleaming and its twin Drails and Javelin launcher were as good as new. Launching, he felt excited. The Flint had been lost 2 months ago. The journey was uneventful as the navigational computer took care of everything. As they neared the wreck, Max cried 'There it is!' and he headed for it. Radiation was eating away at his hull, but they would be out soon. He aimed the Drails at the wreck, but found that he had a new target: two Liberty defenders. He gasped as he turned to flee and began to power up his cruise engines. He was about to run. The hornet came as a surprise, hitting him squarely in the tail: he had to fight.
