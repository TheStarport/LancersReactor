Sherlog

Thu Jun 12, 2003 10:29 pm by Sherlog

Activate the cruise engines directly instead of using the 'Go to' command. The standard shortcut for this is SHIFT+W. Activate mouse flight mode (hit space bar) and steer manually.

'Go to' mode will drop you out of cruise a certain distance from the destination, and it will stop the ship completely when you reach the destination. If there are any obstacles in the flight path (say, race track hoops) then you can get dropped out of 'Go to' cruise as well.

BTW, this was mentioned in earlier 'Hovis Race Problem' threads but it is hard to tell which ones. There are plenty to choose from since every noob must create their own, new thread for this, and so the answers are scattered far and wide.

Sherlog

Sun Jun 15, 2003 11:55 am by Sherlog

Countach, what is your problem? Are you missing hoops?

The race is a bit more difficult in a heavy ship like the Crusader because the heavy ships overshoot more than the light ones like Starflier or Cavalier. With the heavy ships you need to look at the hoop after the one you will go through next so that you can fly a smoother curve (because you cannot make sharp turns at cruise speed). This is especially important if you fly a freighter like the Clydesdale or Dromedary.

Sherlog

Sun Jun 15, 2003 5:41 pm by Sherlog

TIP - wkantanka's TIP applies only to ships with infinite turn rate and zero inertia. All ships that can be used for racing Hovis have a finite turn rate and non-zero inertia, and so the fastest path through the course is not a series of straight lines connecting the centers of the hoops. This is most evident with the heavier ships.
