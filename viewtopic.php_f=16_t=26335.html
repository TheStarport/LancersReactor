Nickless V2

Sat Nov 22, 2003 10:13 am by Nickless V2

what can I say. Hovis sucks big time. There is a workaround where you edit the .ini files wich resembles cheating a lot, but is not that bad as the race stinks.
If you wanna beat him the fair way, here's what to do and what not to do.

Hovis do's and don'ts:

Do:
- Buy a light fighter, it's more agile. One is for sale at BS The Hood. (Cavalier)
- Go to cruise speed. Default key is Shift + W
- Use Mouseflight. Default key is space-bar.
- Aim for the next gate while you are flying through a gate.
- Use thrusters before hitting cruise. This puts you in front of Hovis before the first gate, after wich you can engage cruise speed and lead the whole way.

Don't:
- Use the Go-To command. When you reach your target with the Go-To command, the ship will stop. And you don't want that
- Use speed mods. Default cruise speed (300) is an excellent speed to beat the race. If you go faster, it will be harder to get to the next gate.
- Use heavy fighters. Although some have done it in a heavy fighter or even a freighter, it's reccomanded for unexperienced racers to use a light fighter.
- Freak out when Hovis starts shooting at you. Just stay in cruise and aim for the next gate. When you reach the finnish, the shooting stops.
- Pay to much attention to Hovis. Just do your own race and all will turn out well.

I hope this helps.

Nah, check your ship. If the descritpion says something like HF or heavy fighter, you're in the wrong ship. You need to get a light fighter (LF) because it turns a lot better.

Next you have to make sure you stay in the track.
The first part is the easy part. All you have to do is aim for the next gate to make it through.
After the halfway point the course gets more dificult and you need to "Oversteer" to get through the gate. What I meen is, when the next gate is on the right side of the gate you are passing, then you must aim to the right side of the next gate so that you would pass it if you'd stay on that course.
This will put you in front of the gate and give you a bit extra space (room) to turn.

The best advice would be to do your own race. Don't pay attention to Hovis. As long as you make every corner at cruise speed, you will be allright, (can't tell you too much ) even if he takes the leed after the first few rings.
