Ok,

what i noticed when i first started putting custom models into FL, in milkshape you should name your primary group, or rather the first object listed in the mesh groups list to a unique name for every ship.

for example, say you have made a set of 3 fighters to create a new series. and lets say you over look the fact that the first mesh group in Milkshape is name: obj0 (for sake of arguement) now when you go into FL after converting them if they are all 3 sold at same base, or if you own one of them then go to a dealer that sells another, EG: You own the LF and now want the HF. you will encounter this problem.

It comes dow to , i believe, CRC values, and/or the 3db name under the vmesh tree.

it is the same as mat files and the textures. if the same object (object with same name) is already loaded, FL will use that one instead of teh next incarnation.

The result is what is happening to you. 2 or more ships that share the same look in some cases when they are not suppose to.

Go back in to Milkshape and verify that your first listed mesh group for each ship has a unique name.
