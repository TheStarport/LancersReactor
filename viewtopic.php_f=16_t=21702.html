Gung_Ho

Wed Jul 23, 2003 6:34 am by Gung_Ho

With a Titan, you don't have the forward firepower of a Saber, and you lack the manueverability of an Eagle. Your advantages are more armor, a greater number of nanobots and batteries, and a more powerful powerplant with increased energy generation. The armor and increased bots/bats gives you about a 9% advantage in durability in theory. In practice however, You are unlikely to use all your nanobots before you die in combat - few people do. That drops the benefit ratio somewhat. The remaining figure is probably not worth the sacrifice in manueverability/firepower.

However, If I was to fly a Titan, I'd probably try to use ALL of the advantages, and tailor my combat style to fit with the result. To do this, you might try equipping weapons that typically drain a great deal of energy. I've seeen many a player mounting 4 Diamondbacks on their eagles, and the use of these would certainly be slightly easier with the better corsair powerplant. Another possibility would be standard (high projectile speed) weapons such as Skyblasts or krackens, since using 4-6 of these at once can represent a significant drain.

I don't know that I would try a Titan, but if I did, I would certainly attempt to use it for what it was good for, and simply build a combat style around what I ended up with.
