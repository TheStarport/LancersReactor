Tue Feb 15, 2005 6:32 am by Earendil

I think Chips has the right idea. If your FL client worked flawlessly for several months and suddenly it didn't, then something changed. It could be your ISP, college ISP's are notorious for sniffing for excessive traffic on ports looking for the boad, ole P2Per and then blocking that port. But this is rare.

Chances are you changed something in you 'puter. What I have heard of at least 90% of the time in these cases is someone installed another game and it's install was not very efficient. Instead of checking and not touching a later version of DirectX, it installs it's own version of DirectX regardless. To make a long story short the recommended cure was just reinstall DirectX to get the latest version because for the most part it's backward compatible.

Was anything changed in your gateway/modem? FL client uses close to a dozen OUTGOING ports only (no INCOMING ports). Ordinarily most firewalls (especially hardware firewalls) are concerned about INCOMING ports only but some software firewalls, like ZoneAlarm, firewall both directions. Do you have ZA?

I assume the direct connection you mention to your gateway/modem is actually your ISP requiring you to use only your 'puter and it's unique MAC address with their gateway/modem. It's a neat trick by ISP's to ensure you have only one 'puter per Internet connection but there is a simple workaround. Today's gateways have the ability to be reconfigured to project out the WAN (Internet) side any MAC address. So if you get a gateway, put your 'puter's MAC address in it, then you can try a little test. Through the gateway, connect another 'puter with FL client and see if that works correctly. If it does that would rule out issues with your network, gateways, ISP and back to the FL global list server.

Hope any of this helps.

There has been talk throughout the web that the DNS are not up to their usual, efficient selves. DNS or Domain Name Server translate domain names into IP addresses. Your ISP has one but all DNS are updated from only a few servers in the world which are high level DNS. This could also be a problem when FL client tries to find the FL global list server, fllistserver.zone.msn.com. There is a way around the DNS but it has to be applied to each 'puter. In your Windoze (or WinNT) directory, look under system32 directory, then look under drivers directory, then look under etc directory for the filed named just hosts . Open this with a text editor and add the domain name and IP address of that domain.

fllistserver.zone.msn.com 207.46.204.209

You could put any other domain you are having trouble resolving, once you get their IP address usually through a WhoIs website. We have seen this help some people who's DNS seems to be a bit scrambled. Thanks to Wan Solo for the tip.

I worked with someone who posted about a similar problem on my forums. Myself and Wan Solo worked with a guy through TeamSpeak trying to get his FL client to connect to the FL global list server. We tried all the above tricks but nothing worked.

So then we delved into the steamy world of DirectX. Wan and I both ran DxDiag (in the system32 directory of Windoze). It's a diagnostic tool to test all parts of DirectX including DirectPlay. Since I know Wan and my FL installations work, we verified it by testing DirectPlay. I set up a DirectPlay chat server and Wan was able to log in and chat. We used TS to communicate IP address and port numbers. We had to both open INCOMING ports in our firewalls for each to chat with the other, I guess since either party could initiate a chat message.

When I tried it with this other guy it didn't work. He opened the port but could never connect through DirectPlay. When he tried to reinstall DirectX he said it took a very short time yet it's a 16 MB install! (english version, multilingual version is 34 MB!) So I think his DirectX is fubar for FL. The odd thing is he can play Halo MP just fine and with it being a M$ product, I expect they are using DirectPlay as well!
