My vision of post-Coalition Sol is that there's a central government that everyone pays lip service to even as they snipe and shoot at each other. The political situation is different enough to feel like a new setting but similar enough that a Freelancer would feel right at home. Like sniper said, they'd be bigger and more technologically advanced, but haven't expanded in the Sirius sector's direction (or just haven't reached Sirius yet).

Here's a scenario:

While scouting the Dom Kovash network, the Order stumbles upon post-Coalition humanity.

Orillion hires Trent to scout the post-Coalition nation to see what it's like. Trent is supposed to go undercover, so he can't take any Sirius sector ships. Likewise, Sirius money is no good in post-Coalition territory.

So Trent arrives in Sol controlled space in a pre-Exodus fighter "liberated" by the Order from a museum. He's also effectively flat broke. The fighter is an obsolete antique in Sirius, and is even more so in Sol space. However, a mysterious buyer takes the antique off Trent's hands for just enough cash to buy the cheapest, least effective fighter still in use by the post-Coalition factions. It's just enough to start Trent freelancing there.

Later in the campaign, Trent learns through a news item or rumor that his antique was in fact worth a million times what he was paid for it and is now in the hands of a museum or private collector or something.


- the First Evil
