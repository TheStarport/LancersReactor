NukeIt

Mon Mar 01, 2004 6:12 am by NukeIt

Lower your graphics settings first, and you should have an easier time winning. The lower your graphics settings, the higher your framerates, and the better your ship will respond to your commands.

The bigger your ship is, the more you may need to "lead" your turns- that is, turn slightly ahead of where the race gate is so that you're facing the right direction when you get to it. Don't turn too wide or too sharp, or you'll bounce off...and if that happens, you can pretty much write off the race.

Never use the GoTo command, as it will shut off your cruise as you pass each waypoint.

There's a reason why the Hood sells a Cavalier instead of the Bretonian HF- this race is infinitely easier in a smaller vessel. You don't need to have a ship that turns on a dime(many have completed the race in a freighter), but it will make for less frustration if you're having problems with this bit.
