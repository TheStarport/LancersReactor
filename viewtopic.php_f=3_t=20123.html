I think it depends on how much faster than light we are talking about.

Let's say the FTL drives allowed a ship to transit from Sol to Sirius (a distance of 8.6 light years) in just 1 year, i.e. more than 8 times faster than light.

Putting the passengers and crew in suspended animation would still be a great way to reduce the need for consumable supplies on the trip and thus save weight and bulk in what the ships have to carry. That's a few hundred thousand tonnes of food, water, oxygen and other consumables saved if the crew and passengers were asleep even for just one year.
