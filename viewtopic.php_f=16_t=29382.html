Bounty Hunter Jackson

I think the point of the Unknown systems is just to be something fascinating to discover. You've got to admit, they're strange places. I've been caught off guard once in a while just looking at the scenery. And then there's the fact that Nomads are there -- don't you remember the first time you found them (providing you weren't told, of course, then it ruins the surprise), how shocked you were to see Nomads cruising around as though they owned the place? I was still a pretty newbie pilot when I found the jump holes, and was pretty nervous about going near them, let alone through them. They're just something interesting to discover. And they add to the story of the Outcasts, who view them as something approaching gods it seems. Like the Hispania -- it doesn't do anything other than make you think 'ah, that's interesting -- cool' when you find it, and it adds to the story.

Megistos

Thu Mar 18, 2004 11:59 am by Megistos

The Dallas Incident of 500 AS is the reason why the Texas system is used mainly as a prison system, and also why there are the Dark Matter clouds on the west side of the system. Ageira were conducting a Jump Gate experiment with some new technology, but (surprise, surprise) it went wrong, and half the system was devastated in the cataclysmic explosion. The California Jump hole was formed as a direct result, with the radiation giving rise to the Grande and Pequena Negra clouds
