Technical vocabulary -

Meteoroid - a physical body of mass (usually rock) in free space, with an independant orbit.

Meteor - the flash or streak of light visible as a meteoroid enters a planet's atmosphere. The term is also used in reference to the glowing meteoroid.

Meteorite - the physical remains of a meteoroid that has undergone planetary entry - i.e. the chunk of rock that hits the ground.

Asteroid - one of a number of objects ranging in size from sub-kilometer to about 1,000 kilometers, most of which lie between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter; also called "minor planets".
