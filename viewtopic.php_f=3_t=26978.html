Sh|ft

Thu Dec 18, 2003 9:24 pm by Sh|ft

Cap ships have a nice little blind spot right on their underbelly (well, maybe not the Dreadnought, but the others do.). Right there their hull gets in the way of their turrets and you can just unload the guns on 'em. Careful with torps, mines and missiles, 'cause they do splash damage.

I never took a liking to mines, but when they hit, they do significant damage. A Ripper can take out a good portion of a ship's hull, which could be the difference between a win and a lose.

As for fighters, I prefer to maneuver onto their six (which is why I hate engine kill) and unleash the fury. Under prolonged battles, TDCs could significantly drain an opponent's powerplant.

there is apparently a widespread myth among users that "spamming" is what happens when you dump cans of Spam into a revolving fan.

J Dawg

Sat Dec 20, 2003 12:11 am by J Dawg

A good tactic is (in Single player only, most humans wouldn't be dumb enough as opponents to do this) this. AI ships tend to circle out far and then charge straight at you. If you can fire a few shots to get their sheilds down, fire a torpedo at them. Since the game doesn't treat the torp as a missile, the AI fighters will usually not evade them and acutally blunder straight into it. Depending on who you're fighting this will destroy an enemy in one or two hits, especially useful in a situation where you are hopelessly outgunned and need to even the odds quickly.
