My bad all!!! I totally forgot to put how my friend made a new MP ID for me but still uses his old MP ID. I got the info from this forum also. Just had to piece it together is all. I will paste the walk thru below that I made for my friend. =)

Oh and a quick note to Stinger. Not trying to make fun of yah, but if I click on Hippocool's Profile, his e-mail is listed right there =P But I totally understand your opinion and must agree that while waiting for help sucks, if you want help you must sometime wait for it.

The process is not that hard and you will not lose your MP ID. I will give instructions below. Here is how to make MP IDs using a system that can make them (i.e. a Win XP system or what Microsoft wanted you to buy to fix this issue)

- Use the MP ID utility loacted in Start/Programs/Microsoft Games/Freelancer the program is called Examine My Account ID. Write this down or print it out.

- Now back up your game saves on floppy disk. These are located in the My Documents folder. There should be a My Games folder in there. Cut and Paste or Copy and Paste this whole folder to a floppy disk. Make sure it made a copy on the disk before doing the next step. You are doing this due to the next step. Now erase the My Games folder once you have ensured you have a copy on a floppy disk.

- Put your Freelancer CD in your computer and there should be an option listed to uninstall Freelancer. This is the BIG pain in the ass part. To generate a new MP ID, the game must be removed and reinstalled. So uninstall the game, and reinstall it.

- Run the game after you have reinstalled and run the Multiplayer game. You should see a new MP ID listed when you do this. Write this new MP ID down. Do not hit the create button. Congratulations!!! You have now made an MP ID for your friend and they will love you for life (or until they forget what you did to help them). =P

- Now we need to get your system reset to how it was. Quit out of Freelancer. Now in your My Documents there there will be a new My Games Folder. Erase it and replace it with the copy from you floppy disk.

- Run Freelance once more. Check to see that your SP games are showing up in the load window. Now run the MP game again and enter your original MP ID. You should now be able to reconnect to your MP servers and play your characters.

And there it is!!! Not a simple system, and not a fast process, but it will generate a new MP ID. Lucky for us that these MP IDs are not system specific. You can also use another persons MP ID on your system to play their characters on other servers in theory. Not sure if MP saves any character data on players system, or only on server.

Any who..... Hope this helps. =)
