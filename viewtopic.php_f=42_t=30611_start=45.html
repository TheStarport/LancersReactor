Tue Apr 20, 2004 6:59 pm by uicathain

Law of Economics. If the value of the credit decreases by introducing more credits into the system, then the cost of goods increases to equivelant credit value.

The problem with the increased mission payouts is it *only* helps in single player. Which this mod was ultimately designed for by my guess.

A Multiplayer Server would want to reduce the amount of credits that go into the world by atleast half (in some cases more, like the 9 digit trade). It has been stated by the majority of those that play the MP games that the mission payouts and trade profits should be reduced, not increased.

It's that repetitiveness that gives longevity to a game. I mean, just look at games like Diablo II. How many thousands of people still play that stupid game just to get that uber-rare item in that new HC Ladder. I mean once you have 200m credits, the best ship in the game on 3 characters... what's the point in playing anymore? This very comment has been said numerous times about the TNG 1.7 mod.
