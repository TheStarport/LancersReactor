Remember that they disabled joystick support as a marketing gimmick: they didn't want to scare away people who didn't want to use one. They didn't want anyone to perceive the humble flight control as a "requirement". The barrier to its use is not technical; it is psychological. Therefore it stands to reason if they slip the support back in quietly, unobtrusively, there is no psychological damage done to the keyboard / mouse people: out of sight, out of mind.

It is rumored they'll accomplish this via a "patch" they'll release some time after the game hits the stores -- in fact that's probably already part of the plan, even before "Going Gold" -- but I'll believe it when I see it.

Bottom line: it's in there, they said so themselves ... all they need to do is switch it on. Once the non-flyers have bought a few copies and the "Stick Scare" issue is negated they'll give us old Privateer fans what we've been wanting for five years: new ships to fly and a new world in which to fly them!
