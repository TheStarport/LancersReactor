Hmm, Looks like 16-bit widechars to me.

For every 4 byte of the number-string use strtol( aptr, eptr, 16);
to convert it to char (cast the returned long int to char) where aptr
points to the 3rd byte and eptr to the byte behind the 4th byte each.

Sat Jun 21, 2003 12:45 pm by Sherlog

This is most likely Unicode, the 16-bit version that Microsoft uses. It can be converted to 8-bit text via WideCharToMultiByte() or a similar function.

But for most practical purposes you can convert it by simply discarding the high bytes as they are 00.
