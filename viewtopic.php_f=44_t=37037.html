Laine,

I moved the thread.. (you dont need to do it now - Chips.....)

Ok, ...... I will try to answer some of your questions....and provide some links

1. Barrel group not showing in utf.. not exactly sure why not.. Have you run Redeyes mesh converter? I will admit that it is not the easiest to use ... but "persistance kills resistance"... lol ....the meshconverter thread is here: Here

2. Barrels not elevating? The whole weapon should swivel. the barrels should go up and down but again the whole weapon pivots on the base.. could it be that you haven't followed the procedures to import the barrel group with the mesh converter?, as they are not listed as a separate group in the .cmp file (see question 1)
Also, do you have HpConnect and HpMount as 2 hardpoints on the base?

3. The model must be upside down and facing you in milkshape prior to export -if you used the newer cmp exporter that may explain why..you need to set it up correctly when you export..... (I use the earlier version .cmp exporter )

4. a narrow band of fire.. hmmm.... several reasons are possible for this....a) the HpMount and HpConnect are not correct.. b) the ship the weapon is attached to has had the min and max values 'fixed' in the .cmp for that weapon hardpoint... c) you have modified the values within the weapon.cmp

some additional info is available in this thread: Here

Weapons creation is full of pitfalls and not for the faint hearted as there are just so many things that can go wrong.. - no offence intended I suggest you check out Arghs tutorial and try that... as his approach is a little different to mine and may help you in your efforts... Then if all else fails post again...

btw... it would have been a lot easier to have posted your question in my weapon tutorial, or in the tutorial by argh.... By combining all your threads into an existing thread it not only:

1. Keeps all the info in one specific thread where it is able to be of most benefit to all
2. Makes searching for information easier (using the search function)
3. Keeps the forums neater - less posts about the same subject
