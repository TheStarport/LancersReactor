Hovis is easy to lose to if ...

<a> you suffer from lag ...

you have the screen size too small.

<c> you have too much cargo.

But, sell your cargo, set the screen details to the minimum with everything turned off, set the screen size large and give it a shot.

Then it is easy.

Reducing the detail levels, etc. will cure the lag.

A larger screen size will let you see the gates better (otherwise it is easy to get lost on some of the gates).

A lack of cargo improves your handling capacity.

And, you need to be in the real flight mode, don't use the autopilot, etc.

If you're only gonna post "it's so easy!!!11!!11oneone" without offering any tip on how you did it then YES it's pointless and insulting to the people who had problems with it.

Unmodded FL = okay it's not too hard, fly calmly and point ahead anticipate the next ring's position. If you're mostly a trader and not a turn-on-the-dime kinda flier then consider flying a LF for this mission. The last 1/3 of the race is a bit twisty if you're not careful because the next rings sometimes lie offscreen at a weird angle, try not to concentrate too hard on the rings and focus on the purple target instead.

Ships mods = some VHFs turn too slowly and have a large drift due to inertia, try flying a Hydra Corvette through the race some time, not only does it turn slowly, it swings wide as well, you'll get thrown off the course by the first few turns. I'm a fighter pilot kinda flier and I always win in a LF/HF but some VHFs are just too bulky.

Just buy a LF and you'll be okay. For the guys who said they flew a capship to win the race, post screenshots. I've flown a Balistner and it flies like a damn pig, there's no way in hell you can win against Hovis in that. Unless you cheat and mod the ship.
