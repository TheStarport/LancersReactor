hi christina,

welcome to lancersreactor, you are in the best hands here and many of your age play this game along with the "youngsters"

ok the mod manager has some problems with deactivating mods, but it has an option called "restore backups" that might help you to deactivate the mod
after that is done you are free to delete any mod from your computer and try others (but there is not the need to delete them - you can also keep them if you want to play them again at a later point of time)

Id suggest that you use the freelancer mod manager 1.31 instead of 1.4 in future because this old version is working better and causes less problems. You can find it here at lancersreactor

if you want to try several mods or read a bit about them before you play you can use the lancersreactor download section on our main page
you will get a very long table with all downloads, you can sort this table by clicking on its header (just click on "DLs" and you will get a list of the most favorite mods)

<- 52 y.o. Freelancer modeler and Modder/coder.

Most mods you can play were developed using the FLMM 1.3.1 ...
(I use the 1.4 beta 4 with the SDK 1.5b for development of my current mod)
However, just restoring backups doesn't entirely do the trick if you want to try out other mods, since many of them require a "clean install" of Freelancer ..especially if you want to play on-line in a MP environment. (left over files from other mods have to be cleaned out)

I've tried many mods out there and met many "on-line" aquaintances.
Of those, my favorites were three:
Crossfire, Top Freelancers and The Void. They require "clean installs" .

I've been working on my own mod particularly for one reason, that's to install my own ships. However, I've also been working on changing it's face and adding new "thrills" to it.

Take time and try them out. Find out what you like best.
