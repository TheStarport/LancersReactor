Cult films discussion: Zatoichi

Ha! I got you beaten, Taw. Just saw it yesterday, my it was such a treat! and funny as hell . The story is about a blind wandering masseur/ronin (played by Takeshi Kitano, who also directs) who stumble upon a village under siege by two rivaling Yakuza gangs - and when not slicing Yakuza scums, he also solves murders a decade old.

Click here for the official site (Japanese, Flash site). Pay attention to the stompy, percussion-rich beat in the intro Flash, composed by Keiichi Suzuki - I love that piece.

For trailers, click here or if it doesn't work, go to the site and click at the 6th flash button from the right on the bottom of the frame(buttons are in Japanese, but if you hover your mousepointer for a while over the button it'll shift and reveal the word 'Trailer' in English). Or you can see the much inferior English-speaking trailer from Miramax in Apple's QuickTime Trailers page.

Just a bit warning tho, the editing is somewhat unusual, definitely not mainstream Hollywood storytelling. Also featured: gory but cartoony CGI blood and ultraviolence, hinted pedophilic acts, and cross-dressing. Some of you will come out thinking "Wtf was that about?" especially after seeing the closing scene; which will definitely remind you of...um...Riverdance. Weird scene, but very fun and entertaining (I think this is what The Last Samurai lacked, no tap-dancing )

5 out of 5 stars

Now I just have to wait until its UK release then Taw and Archie can join me oohing and aahing. Yanks are less fortunate, you'll have to wait until June I think.
