Hmm, It sounds like alot of you guys should go have a look at Earth and Beyond - there is a fun online game, a universe bigger the FL and packed with players - thousands of players. Downside is many things had to be simplified to cut down on data transfer, no collision detection, simplified combat (computer aims for you) and pay-to-play (20permonth) There is a free 5-day trial if you can get you're hands on the game (1 gig download - worth it)
Freelancer would have benefited from the sort of variety of missions you got in the Freespace campeign. Fleet battles, covert ops, recon etc.
But I wouldn't complain about lack of clans, 32 player limits and technology limits. What you are asking of the developers is called "scope creep" and becomes unmanageable if you want to develope the game within a couple of years.
The game is very nice for it's SP campeign (how do you spell that ?) at the same time as providing a reasonable MP experience.
Whereas E&B can be a blast when in a group, but can quickly lose it's luster when playing by yourself.
Comparing E&B and FL would probably show you what I mean when I say that the best of both worlds is probably a bit much to ask for at present.

Even a kick up the pants is a step forwards...
