*Beeeeeep*
WEVE GOT V-FIB, CLEAR!
*Zap!*
Nothing, CLEAR!*
*Zap!!*
Ive got a pulse!
------

Thousands of years, and I have seen all that I would ever have wished to have seen. The Insufference of life, hypocrisy, betrayal, hatred, the pit of human life. But not only human life, Sentience, it was a fatal flaw, the Creators got it wrong.
What I have seen could tear apart the mind of one weaker. It has already sent me teeming to the edge of insanity, only to fight back against it, endlessly.
Why do I keep fighting it? Humanity needs me, humanity needs us, Life, needs us. Maybe if I was a normal person, I’d have the time and justification for a nice, comfortable nervous breakdown, but Fate doesn’t work that way. Fate chooses the most least likely people, chucks them into the least likely situations, with the least likelihood of coming out alive.
But,
If this is my path
Who am I to change it now?
We’ve got a universe to save.


I sat back as the Z-Axis fighter set down, trying to block out the endless white noise that continued to encompass my mind, almost coming to the point of unbearability.
“You little…”
“Come here you little s..”
“Please… Help me…”
Images, swirling, endlessly. I resisted the urge to scream, and staggered upright, barely even managing that easy task.
“You look like s..t mate” Viator said, entering the ship.
That about summed it up for me, I felt like it.
“I feel like it buddy” I said, resisting the urge to faint “You want to take us out? I’m not feeling too good”
“With pleasure” He replied
“Eat me…”
I shook my head, trying to clear the images
“Ara?” I said, weakly
“I see it too” She said “What is it?”
“I have no idea” I whispered, sitting down at the back while the ship lifted off “I was hoping you could tell me”
She shook her head
“Not a clue” She replied
“… like it like that, don’t you…”
I grunted, focussing on the front of the ship.
“Adam is dead, by the way” Indigo said
“I know” I replied, watching the atmosphere fade away
“No, don’t please, don’t, STOP!”
“We know all to well” Ara said
My eyes blurred slightly, and my balance shifted slightly
“You all right back there?” Viator asked, the concern in his voice hidden, but not completely
“Actually…” I began
“This is the best you can do?”
“Please, Master…”
“Get back here! SLAVE!”
I had a sudden vision. It was in a small room, a small girl was tied up spread-eagle in the centre, with an old man towering over her with a whip. I barely had time to register anything before the crack of the whip sounded on the girl’s back, and with that, the vision vanished.
“… No” I finished, swaying
He turned, and looked at me. He told me later that I looked like a ghost, I was so white.
“Arania?” He asked, concerned
“Give in, you know you want to”
“Eh?” I said, looking up, trying to register him in the blur
My stomach lurched, and it was barely ten seconds before I was in the rear of the ship, vomiting up bile.
“Ugh” I said, flushing the mess down, before, sliding down the bathroom wall to join Ara on the floor
“What’s happening to me?” I said, the visions subsiding, for now
“Us” Araminta corrected “I don’t know what it is”
“Its like, every memory any human has ever had” I said, trying to comprehend it.
“Adam’s memories” Ara suggested
I looked down at her, my vision clearing.
“They ARE Adam’s memories” Ara said, louder
I scooped her up, and rushed out of the bathroom, almost colliding with Viator on the way.
“How long ago did you kill Adam?” I asked
“Barely a few hours” Viator replied
“Oh god…” I said, going pale again, and backing into the wall.
“What is it?” Viator asked me
I was about to respond, before the ship lurched suddenly sideways before coming to a halt.
“What was that?” Viator and Araminta said in unison
“No idea” Indigo and I said
We all looked at each other, and the weird thing we just did.
We walked back into the cockpit, to find ourselves in the hangar of a massive ship.
“That looks Aztec in design” I said, noticing the odd pyramidical design
“It’s Quetzalcoatl’s Ship” Viator said
“And we didn’t see it” Indigo said
“So either we really do have the biggest lucky streak of all time” I said
“Or we’re expected” Ara finished
“Neither of which particularly appeals to me” Viator said, peering out of the cockpit dome.
“But we do have to find Erin” Indigo stated
I glanced over to her unconscious Dæmon
“Let’s go” I said, walking out of the ship.
I stepped on the odd stone-like floor of the bay, the material crunching beneath my feet.
“So, where to first?” Viator asked, stepping from the ship, Indigo cradling Erin’s dæmon.
My vision blurred, and I took all of my strength to keep breathing. I fell to my knees, clutching my head, and screamed.
“Arania?” Viator shouted
I lowered my arms, and felt a single tear fall from my cheek onto the floor below.
“Ill be fine” I said, rising back up “We have a friend to save”
I pulled the hood on my cloak up, and briefly saw my hand glow very pale yellow before I drew it away again.
I put it back up, and deduced that the scar on my face was glowing, but quickly losing its luminosity
“Come on” Araminta said in my ear, soothingly “We’ll be alright”
I turned
“Viator” I said, pulling my hand in a sweeping motion. “Lead the way”

------
Ok, 25ccs of adrenalin, let me know if its condition deteriorates any further.
CMON PEOPLE!

So many years I stood among the thoughts and tears of those I served.

I'd only come here seeking me.

At the bottom of a dark pit, covered in hand crafted symbols, a decrepit being lay on the cold floor. A dark tangle of hair covering it's head, whilst only rags covered the rest of it's body. The smell of human waste had become a familiar smell in the dank pit, which, at the bottom measured only 4 metres square, with what looked like a doorway in one wall.

Air moved slowly down in this cruel prison, the only ventilation coming from the lungs of the being trapped at the bottom of this hellhole. Nothing stirred, nothing moved, except for the minute up and down motion of the human's chest, allowing air to move.

Light flooded into the chamber, waking the human, now revealing itself to be female, her quiet blue eyes set like sapphires in her pale face. He tiny frame had become more noticeable, her skin hanging from her bones, no muscle tone remaining. She grabbed at her chest, the throbbing pain that she felt every day had somehow subsided, weaker than before. The doorway in the wall had opened up, filling the pit with light, only a black shadow appearing in the light. Time seemed to slow in here, where a day would feel like only a minute had passed outside. There was no way of proving it, no way of knowing, no information leaked into this horrific cell.

This shadow had become a regular visitor, either to deliver her the tiny amount of food needed to sustain her being, or deliver the enormity of pain, an endless torture, with only one purpose in mind: to break her physical body in as many ways as possible. But this time she thought something was different. Something was here, that shouldn't be, and that she would die before she would find out.

As I watched viator rush down the corridor, I couldn’t help but feel a strange presence, from all around, as though the ship was alive.

“What is this place?” I muttered to myself, still trying to shut out the visions that plagued my consciousness
Voices, swirling… no, not swirling, there were voices…
Servo, vestry…
“What?” Araminta said, listening to the voices, as I was
Servo vestri ex oblivio…
“That’s latin” I said to myself “Servo, save… vestra… Yourself, you… ex oblivio…”
I thought, trying to block out the visions
“ex… from…” I placed a hand on the side of the ship, steading myself “oblivio… oblivion…”
I paled, realising…
Servo vestri ex oblivio…
“Save yourself… from oblivion” Ara and I said in unison
I laid myself back, recoiling at the voice
“What is this ship?” I said “What has been done to this ship?”
“It’s a ship?” Ara quipped
I turned, fear rising in my heart. Viator, Erin… The ship was alive, if it could be called that. I may have been overcome by Adams memories and curses, but I should have been able to handle them on my own, if not for this ship. This ship, if not indeed the creator that was on board…
I rushed into the ship, ready to tear it apart, searching…
“I know you left it behind, Viator, where is it?”
Araminta fluttered to the helm seat, unable to cling to my thrashing body as I searched.
“Where is it…?” I kept muttering, tearing off access panels, before finally searching in the logical place…
Under the seat
I must admit, the dark blade was surprisingly light, looked closer, gazing at an inscription on the hilt:
‘Nex poena’
Death Penalty
What was with this world and it’s obsession with Latin?
I took the blade, and walked out of the ship, Ara flying over and once again perching on my shoulder.
The ship was alive, I have no doubt of that. How or why didn’t matter any more. But I intended to make sure this monstrosity went back to where it came from.
The corridor that viator had discovered was bleak, dark. But I knew better. It was almost paranoia, as though I could feel the ship breathing…
I continued on, attempting to feel my way forward, finding the ships bridge though the extrasensory blackout of the ship. Finally coming to a bulkhead
The design was, alien, demonic, even considering everything that I had seen.
The door opened, as though sensing my presence, revealing a massive bridge, overshadowed my massive monoliths hanging from the ceiling.
I entered the room, approaching the nearest computer, of what I thought was a computer.
>:
“That prompt looks familiar…” I muttered
>: Systems diagnostic
>Initialising Diagnostic programme
>Main Generator-Online
>Secondary and Support Generators-Online
>Hull Integrity-Stable
>Weapons-Offline, operational
>Shields-Offline, Operational
>Dimensional Stabilisers-Online
>Gravity Drive Systems Containment-Online
>Gravity Drive-Standby
>:
I pondered the output, before coming to a conclusion
>: Activate Gravity Drive
>Specify Destination
>: Null
>Destination ‘Null’ Searching…
>Gravity Drive Activation Process started…
>Warning, Drive untargeted…
>Specify Time to Jump?
>: 20 Minutes
The klaxons started wailing
“Warning, Gravity Drive activated, Time to jump: twenty minutes and counting”
“Warning, Gravity Drive Destination Untargeted, Emergence point unknown”
I smiled, I knew exactly where it was heading. In twenty minutes this ship was going back where it came from.
“I’m afraid I can’t let you do that” Came a voice, from behind me
“Hello Q” I said, turning
I was expecting the old Aztec god to be a towering behemoth. I did NOT, however, expect him to be human, and I even expected less, for him to like identical to me.
“Long time no see, Arania” Q replied, grinning like a madman
I raised the Dark sword, pointing it at him in accusation.
“Evil” I muttered under my breath, pointing the sword at his head
“Now now” He said, his voice reverberating demonically “No need to insult your host, Ancient”
”Hah” I muttered “I am my own host? What a screwey world this has become. Although you don’t have a form do you, you parasitic creator? You have to steal them form people’s minds. Or lacking that, their BODIES!”
Q’s smile faltered
“That was uncalled for” He said
“Get out of my way, or you will find what else is ‘uncalled for’”
“This ship won’t let me die”
“In twenty minutes, this ship is going back to the dark pit that spawned it”
The klaxons wailed again
“Warning, nineteen minutes to Gravity Drive activation”
“Correction:” I said “Nineteen minutes”
I swung the sword, only to find that it passed though him.
Q vanished, his laughter echoing around the ship
“Q is the ship?” Ara muttered
I spun, again typing on the console
>: Lockout Gravity Drive systems
>Lockout Confirmed
I turned and ran, hoping upon hopes that Viator would find Erin and make it back to the ship before the drive activated, for what lay beyond that jump… even I fear to imagine.
