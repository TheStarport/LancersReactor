Newsletter:

Work on the Idun Shield Generator has been completed on Los Angelas. This technology is available to all factions of Sirius, and has been built for the good of humanity.

Using counter-annhilation and disruption signatures, the Idun Shield is capable of deactivating incoming missiles and lasers bursts, essentially rendering the user invincible. Due to ionic radiation and jump residue, it is impossible to mount an Idun Shield on a starship, but we believe that it may prevent innocent civilians being lost to orbital bombardment.

The shield was deliberately programmed to ignore starship signatures, as not doing so would prevent the landing of friendly ships. Therefore, it is still possible to land or take off from a hostile station or planet while an Idun Shield is in operation, just not blast the planet or station into oblivion without actually landing.


THE SHORT VERSION:
The Idun Shield is (ooc) actually a tool for making battles for planets and stations a bit more interesting; if the planet or station has such a shield in place, its actually important to invade the planet or board the station, has opposed to simply blowing it apart with an orbital bombardment.

Please remember, all the technologies used and/or developed by Ageira are available to all factions. That means that anyone is free to use a Hermes Cannon, Mercury Star Drive, or Idun Shield in there tactics.

Moving on...

Work on Marathon Station is complete! All employees on Planet Manhattan are now in the middle of their voyage across the galaxy to the New Columbia system.

Ageira Bases Fortified! Ageira is proud to announce that Detroit Munitions, Marathon Station, and Styx Station have all been equipped with Idun Shield Generators, making it impossible to capture or destroy any of the three without an infantry assault.


And finally, some more role-playing.

Security Office, Deck 4, Marathon Station, New Columbia System.

Henry Gairns sipped his coffee, trying to stay awake as he watched the inventories scrolling by. He had been assigned to ensure that the labs on Deck 4 were suitably equipped, one of the most boring tasks a guard could be issued.

Something caught his eye. That, right there... that readout looked strange...

He scanned it. "Holy... what would they need THAT for?"

He looked up at the control panel, then back at the screen. Finally, he announced, "Computer, cross-reference equipment in Lab Delta, Deck 4 with projects currently in operation by Ageira Technologies."

There was a hum as the computer did its work, before it responded, "No matches found."

"No matches? Computer, cross-reference equipment in Lab Delta, Deck-4 with projects completed or cancelled by Ageira Technologies."

"Running.... One match found."

The readout in front of him displayed a single file, labeled "Promotheus".

"Computer, open file, Promotheus."

"Access Denied. Information on the Promotheus Project is available only to the executive and to project head Doctor William Larrings."

"Computer, this is Security Officer Henry Gairns. Iniate protocol 517. The information in file Promotheus has been deemed as vital to current station security activity. Open file IMMEDIATELY."

"Understood. Iniating protocol 517. Alerting general station to potential security threat. Opening file."

Gairns stared at the writing on his readout. It couldn't be... this was impossible...

"Oh, no..."
