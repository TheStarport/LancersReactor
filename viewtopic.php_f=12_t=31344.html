gromit

Wed May 12, 2004 2:25 am by gromit

nice work esky....only one comment...

your planet and sun appear a little flat and I think its because they each have an almost perfect circular edge...there's an easy photoshop way of getting around this. Select a ring of about 30-50 pixels around the edge of your planet layer and play with gaussian blue settings. If you stagger the blur so it sharpens more towards the centre of the planet you will find it gives it a more "rounded" effect.

Good use of filtering though, nice colours, good work

mustang

Wed May 12, 2004 3:12 am by mustang

Good going Esq, those are looking neat. I'd have to agree with the guys though, they are looking a little two dimensional. Along with groms advice you could get that planet looking more rounded with some more detailed shading. Remember that planets are pretty well much black on their dark side, then hue it out. I usually like to do this manually with the burn tool with the exposure set to about 50-70% and you can gradually lighten it up as you get to the point where light outweighs dark. Remember to curve it at the edges, so you'll get the appearene of of a 3 dimensional shape. Try to get your light source right too, otherwise your pick can look very lop sided. Here's one of my dodgy jobs to give you an idea of what I mean.

EDIT: Actually I can see you've used the light source tool in the planet pic and it's seriously not really up to the task. It's great for creating a spotlight like effect, but it sucks for pretty well much everything else i've tried to use it for.

From an artistic point of view the warp one is very asthetically pleasing and I've always been a sucker for cool colours. Personally I think it's by far the best of the bunch.
