I'm not into crowds either...my gf thrives on them and just can't understand when I say that I hate packed places. Its one of the reasons I hate london...she just doesn't get it. Personally I think its an intravert thing.

@chips glastonbury crime...aaah now theres a subject...let me tell ya a little story....one of the times I was there, I think it was 93/94 (kind of a blur ), we were camping quite close to a little wood thats in the centre of the glast. site. This would was well known for being the place to buy your "stuff" if you didn't dare bring any with you. Each dealer has his own "patch", sort of a small group of trees where he can scream "ggggggeeeeeet your smoke, ganja, pot, mary jane, gear, grass, sensimelia, northeeeerrrrrrrnnnn lights, marijuana, cannabis HEEERRRREEEE.....buy one get one bag of mushrooms free" etc etc as loud as he wants to. There are thousands of police there....often surrounding that area....they just let it go on....as long as there's no trouble and there's no one selling anything other than "naturals" they don't care. (kinda why it makes me laugh when I hear politicians talk about "Police attitudes to cannabis", but anyway thats an entirely different subject) This particular year one of the dealers strayed from his patch into another dealers area. The other dealer wasn't happy and an argument insued. The argument got more and more heated and eventually resulted in the p*ssed off dealer pulling out a small pellit gun from his jacket and shooting the other guy in the ass. The police collapsed laughing as the guy hobbled around clutching his buttocks...they ushered the shot guy off (he eventually went off to soak his rear end in ice and life went on as normal) and I remember clearly seeing one of the policemen approach the guy with the gun, wag his finger at him and then buy a joint. Nothing to report at all, but it was hilarious to watch. Now 3 or 4 days later I arrive home to be greeted by a flurry of answerphone messages from my parents in distress....so I immediately call them....and they proceed to tell me...

1. My picture was in the "Mail on Sunday".
2. It was taken in what looked like a moment of sheer panic...apparently I looked really scared.
3. The picture also showed 4 police men and a guy holding a gun.
4. Headline read "Festival scarred by Drug-fuelled Gun Riot"

Luckily my parents are quite understanding and believed the true story. This was especially backed up when the monday papers came out with an odd mixture of headlines...

"Shotgun Maniac on rampage at Festival" (I think that was The Guardian)
"Guns and Roses" (The News of the World led with a picture of a bunch of roses that had been laid at the trees after the "incident"....they managed to cut off the woman who was sat next to them flogging them)

and surprisingly, The Sun was the only paper that managed to get anything close to the truth, and ran with "SCARRED" and picture of the guys arse.

My longwinded point is this, every year I read the same old crap in the papers about the festival...and all it comes down to is a bunch of fuddy-duddy cigar smoking, whiskey drinking fat bald men in fleet street don't like the festival. The truth is this. There is crime there yes. But its mostly petty theft - the kind of stuff you'd find in any large crowd/city....and its in proportion. 5 years I had no incidents, 1 year I saw the "shooting" and one year I had my tent nicked! The honest answer is this - you couldn't be safer, they put one policeman on duty for every 5 people...they're literally everywhere (but they don't seem imposing for a change)...if you go, you take NOTHING with you that you consider to be valuable...and that includes your tent. Make sure your tent is old and crappy and no one will nick it. Mine was nicked because it was about a week old and straight out of the box. You spend a lot of time away from your tent so don't leave anything in it. And thats it! simple rules they really are! The amount of people that complain when they leave their tent for 3 days in the middle of a campsite with expensive cameras, clothes and walkmans etc sat there ready for the picking, and some opportunist comes along and takes something. Truly pathetic really. 500,000 people...you only need 5 thieves....know what I mean?

Anyway sorry about the length of this post, hope you enjoyed the story

"Scarred"
