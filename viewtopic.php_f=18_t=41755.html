FriendlyFire

Thu Nov 17, 2005 11:07 am by FriendlyFire

Also, I suggest you choose between both professions. After all, they are really different from eachother.

Modeller will ask you style, imagination, art talent, few maths (Knowing how 3D space coordinates work and such), but will not require a lot of coding.

System Maker, on the other side, will ask you style and imagination, but no real art talent. It will require you know a little more maths (To position things correctly), you will have to be able to transpose numbers into images (As launching the game for each position change would be overly long), and you will have to code quite a bit.

Manhattan_Guy

Thu Nov 17, 2005 12:08 pm by Manhattan_Guy

I think nothing speeks against doing both things -- but one at a time.

I myself started as a coder specialized in weapons, then slowly learned all the other coding stuff and I found modelling to be much harder for newbies. I am still learning the basics of 3d modelling today.
Coding, especially systems, also requires logical thinking, like: "Will the system crash if I have a pirate patrol going through a military zone and both patrol and zone have densitiy restrictions, that will be broken?" Next question would be "How to avoid the crash?".
As a coder, you also have to think a lot about balancing and wether or not some things fit together or not. Finding errors in a system can be hard work, just like finding an error in a 3d model is.

So both things can be hard work and sometimes very long-winded and difficult to get completely right. But in both cases, all your effort pays off. It really is your decision.
