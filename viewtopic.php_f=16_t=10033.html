Yes, as soon as he says go, turn on your cruise engines.
(in fact on my screen it popped up "it's a good idea to use cruise engines" before the race)
Then what you're going to want to do is aim your cursor just inside the inside side of the ring (does that make sense?) This will allow you to waste less time flying where you don't need to go. Third, you really don't want to be in a freighter unless you're a very good pilot, it isn't easy to turn them as needed. I can't be sure, but I THINK i had the cavalier at the time. It flew through wonderfully. I was way behind for most of the race (for some reason his cruise engines turn on immediately) however he slows down towards the end and it's just basically a straight flight through the last few rings. Ignore the weapons platforms, they don't really do much damage.

Belter John

Sat Mar 15, 2003 6:56 am by Belter John

I beat Hovis in a Dromedary Freighter, the trick is to hit cruise only to start the race. That will get your ship moving and once cruise engages you will be at top speed as soon as possible. Then use your port and starboard thrusters to cut the corners more sharply than Hovis does. In that way you will gain ground on him.

It will still be a close race, but you will win it pretty soon.
