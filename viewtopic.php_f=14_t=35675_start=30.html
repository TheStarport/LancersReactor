it is when it comes to the long-running struggle of the Tawakalni people against the tyranny of Rabbitry!

I'm quite pleased that this thread has come to life again because there are ideas that have been running around in my tea-towelled head for some time now.

When all's said and done, this is the The Lancers Reactor. We discuss lots of other space/combat games, and quite right too, as nothing exists in isolation (except Esquilax) and SL/FL are continuations of work by Chris and Erin Roberts and show clear descent from the WC series. But as the games have aged and only modding keeps them alive (well, FL really as hardly anyone bothers with SL, more's the pity) we've moved very far away from that Lancer's genesis. This is perfectly natural and to be expected as there'll never be another official Lancer game (don't hold your breath anyway!)

so, wonders your Mullah, what can we do to revive some interest in the original Lancer series. here are a couple of ideas..

1. what about interviewing or getting a piece from Chris and Erin Roberts, or anyone else involved in the designa nd production of SL/FL like Jorg Neumann? A retrospective from years afterwards, if you like.

2. Interviews or articles on the cast, which in the case of Fl would be quite extensive. Ok they're voice actors but they also have other careers, although Gimli is so busy I doubt he'd even remember his voice-acting work for FL as he was LOTR-ing at about the same time.

3. ask M$ how the games performed financially over time, and if they lived up to expectations. This would be of particular relevance to FL as if it wasn't for M$ baling out DA we'd never have seen the game, which was in "development hell" (and anyone reading this who's familar with the dynamics of software development will know exactly what that must feel like!) I've often wondered if M$ made ever made any money out of FL and if it ever repaid its development costs.

I'd just like to read something else other than "join my clan" or "play my mod" and I'm still Lancers at the core and don't want to see the series drift into the land of memory. There may not be much playable life left in them but they're the core of the site and the reason we all came here in the first place way back when, and we still recruit new members on the strength of them.
