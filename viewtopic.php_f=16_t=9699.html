keeper, your argument is somewhat flawed.
Say you are a constant aim and hit your target exactly 60% of the time.
Now say it takes 1000 damage to destroy ship x.
You can use gun a that shoots 1/sec but does 200dmg
or
You can use gun b that shoots 2/sec but does 125dmg

Now with 6/10 shots hitting, it will take you
1000/d * 1.66666666 / s seconds to kill the enemy
where d = dmg and s = shots/s
So for gun A it would take ~8.3 seconds
Gun B would take ~6.6 seconds

Or more with the game, a Sabre with 8000 shield points till missing 60% of the shots would take
~5.2seconds for 4guardians
~4.4seconds for 4nomad blasters
~4.35seconds for 4nomad cannons

This may not seem like much, but when there are 10 ships attacking you, that's ~9 seconds saved, which is a lot of time to lose more shields and take more damage.

Now, you may say that the lower fire rate comes with a higher accuracy rate, however, if your mouse is over the X/+ 60% of the time, it's over the X/+ 60% of the time.

Though I guess, once you reach lvl38 and have one of the 3 "ultimate" VHFs.. it doesn't really matter unless you're doing PVP.. against AI you could probably go with 3lavablade MK IIs and 3 class 6 weapons and still kill just about any AI ships.
