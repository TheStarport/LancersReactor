WapCaplet

Thu Mar 20, 2003 9:39 pm by WapCaplet

My biggest suggestion to DA would be more variety in missions. The biggest feature mentioned about the game prior to its release was the fact that you could choose your own destiny. In fact, the box itself lists 6 different "professions" you can pursue; (I can't remember them right now and I'm at work so I can't check my box). The official website even touts that you can become a lane hacker, pirate, policeman, etc. Microsoft really makes the game sound open-ended, which I think is their main selling point.

The problem is that the missions do not really reflect this. When it comes right down to it, there is only ONE kind of mission; blow up ships. The game would be so much better if DA simply added more types of missions.

Now, I am not a programmer, but I do have a reasonable amount of computer knowledge (as many gamers do), and it doesn't seem like it would be that difficult to add a variety of missions including cargo deliveries, passenger deliveries, recon missions, etc. Nothing fancy, but it would add a little more variety to the game and would give players that true "freelancer" feel. The merchant player would be able to take missions for carrying goods or smuggling equipment and drugs. The bounty hunter player could assassinate or (even better) capture and return notorious criminals. The navy/police player could do recon missions, scouting missions and, of course, combat missions. The pirate player could get missions from the "bad guys" of the game to attack certain freighters, steal certain cargo, disrupt certain convoys or trade lanes. The possibilities are endless and it would only take DA designing 5 or 6 different mission "templates" and throwing in some random variables like they do with the "blow up ships" missions.

The game is awesome, but I think it would be truer to the "freelancer" spirit if there were more variety of missions.

My 2 cents...

Wap

Some nice features...

More and better custom filters for multiplayer selection. First and foremost - being able to use regular expressions to match a specific server or group of servers you want to connect to.

Second, let the players define their 'favorite' servers a little better. This can be done two ways. First off, when you are on a server, give the option to 'remember it.' Then, when you go to search for a server to connect to, do like in Jedi Knight 2 where you can look either on the global server for a game or only poll your 'remembered' servers to see if any are online and list only them. Another option that would be nice here is allowing players to enter the server they want to via an in-game dialog box where you could specify things like ip and port without having to screw with the shortcut files. Put these ones on your 'remembered' or 'preferred' list and do like above where you can search through only those ones.

Aside from that, what other people have said here about random missions would be awesome. From what I can see of the game and from my coding experience, assuming they had a good model for the game and didn't hack it together, it *should* be relatively easy to add in new mission types such as the ones mentioned here. To add to the list, I'd like to see higher level missions where, if you were doing a mission for one of the Navys, you could get not only fighter support, but capital ship support. Imagine blazing away at a Xeno compound with 10 wings of defenders that deploy throughout the mission while 2 wings of Navy fighters support you and a battleship takes on the base...

Another thing that would be nice, and which was mentioned above as well, would be a more realistic economic model. There is nothing here that accounts for something as simple as supply and demand. Now, I will say this in Digital Anvil's defense - the amount of cargo a single fighter is insignifigant when compared to the 60,000 in the trade convoy ships, but still, a bit more fluctuation in prices would be nice.

I'd also like to see some expansion regarding the Hispania. It's kinda glossed over in story mode, but it should be easy for them to put together say 5 or 6 story type missions dealing with their descendants. Following along with this idea, it'd be really cool to see several different 'stories' you could access by flying around to different bases and talking to people. Then you don't have one overriding story, but you've got mini-campaigns that you can do at your leisure.

I'd also like to see more things to do in MP, especially that race! Imagine if you could race NPCs or even other PCs and place bets on who would win and stuff. I can't think of any original ideas for this off the top of my head, but it would flesh out MP some and gives things for people to do other than run trade routes for insane amounts of cash, kill xenos, or kill other players...
