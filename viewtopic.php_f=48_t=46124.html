The thing is that if you want to get people to be interested in working with you, you first need to make them interested to work on the mod. Here are two fictional cases:

CASE 1:
Ey tere me is maknig new mod me needs help I need m0deler textrers nd wutver is neded to mke a cool MOD cmon join me my MODs cool!!!!!!!!

modder@site.com

CASE 2:

Hello, I would be interested in starting a brand new mod. This mod will feature 30 new ships, 20 new systems, 100 new equipment in total and much more. I'm planning for a Total Conversion which will be set in my own universe. I've already started the storyline that will wrap it all up and I'd be looking for some help in modelling and texturing areas. Also, if some experienced coder could help me in the more obscure areas that I still do not properly grasp, I would be thankful. You can contact me at modder@site.com address or simply register and post on these forums: www.somesite.com

--------------

The two cases ask for basically the same thing, but just the fact the second one states what the mod will be about already graps your attention a lot more. Now, I'm not saying you're Case 1 (Not at all ), I'm just contrasting to help you better understand what I mean. If you can detail what your mod is, refer to a post where it is already all explained and, even better, include some screenshots of what's already done, you will probably get a much better response.
