megaburn

Tue Feb 27, 2007 2:43 pm by megaburn

Well this has turned into a case in my original point for this thread.

IF mods, fan projects (i.e. fan built FL2) , a new/overhauled site, new server, minor coverage of new games (i.e. DS1) , community resource/service projects (i.e. the forge and coop) , better community coordination/communications, community consolidation, and even a completely new "next gen" game thats now sort of an indirect FL sequel, cannot give TLR the boost it needs to survive... then, seriously, what will?

At this point that question is starting to become rhetorical and the answer could very well be nothing.

For the past six months I have tried to push for TLR to coordinate with the greater FL community to come up with a solution to this problem. There are a lot of people still active on the various FL sites and many of those sites owe their existence to TLR in one way or another. If the community were to rally together to solve this problem and coordinate an effort to implement a few (or even all) of those ideas, I think everyone would be surprised at the results, the community would revitalize itself.

If I could rally the community I would probably try but I'm not in a good position to do that and I don't know the community outside of TLR that well. I still believe TLR staff are the only ones in a position to effect any kind of change in the FL community. So I'm trying to do what I can with my projects to provide the various community leaders with building blocks they can use to build a foundation for a large scale cooperative effort...

The results are: OL was largely ignored as a possible FL sequel, and if not ignored then met with indifference bordering on hostility, even after it started to show real progress. The forge site was largely ignored until I started to move ahead with it, then BP asked me to put it on hold until TLR got its new server in "a few weeks" (that was back in December on OL's IRC channel). The coop got about the same response, largely ignored. My suggestions for rallying the community, other ideas for community cooperative projects, ideas for the website, ideas for a dozen other things...all largely ignored.

All of the stuff people largely ignored adds up to a lot of talk, planning, and research, with no real action. Now I've had enough and my patients have finally run out.

Unless someone has a better idea I'm going to move ahead with the forge and coop on my current VPS account. Its not much but it will bring results for OL, its sister projects, and maybe a few others willing to join us. From there I'll move to change OL's focus, rather than something connected at the hip to TLR, OL will blaze a new path forward for any members of the greater FL community and any TLR members looking for something better than...well, this stagnated mess.

In a couple weeks I'll submit a news article to TLR with my progress like any other FL community project.


-MegaBurn

Lead Designer and Webmaster,
Openlancer Project

If we are ever going to see a Freelancer 2 (Freelancers: The Return) etc..or whatever..
