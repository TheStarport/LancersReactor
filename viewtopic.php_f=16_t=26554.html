T-hawk

Mon Dec 01, 2003 7:14 pm by T-hawk

The campaign is doable in a Rhino or Starflier (I've done both) because you almost always have several friendly ships around to help draw fire. Flying 240k missions by yourself is a bit harder...

Personally, I've never tried to fly high-end missions in anything lighter than a Drake. It's certainly possible with any ship (especially if it's an assassination mission and the target appears in the first wave). You would just have to do a LOT of running and dodging. Shoot enemy a couple times until your shield goes down, then do the thruster-enginekill getaway, which the agile Starflier can do very quickly.

The Defender is able to do the high-end missions because 1) it has enough hardpoints to mount two or three Stunpulses, and 2) it can use torpedoes. Using only one Stunpulse and a Slingshot would be a fair bit harder (takes 8-12 Slingshot hits to kill a Hammerhead, and that's after pulsing the shields), but it's definitely not impossible. Hard enough to make it not worth trying though, for me at least.

NukeIt

Tue Dec 02, 2003 3:30 pm by NukeIt

Mines are always a good idea on LF's. They're the only weapon which you can use while still evading enemy fire, so they're perfect for ships that need to dodge a lot. You just need to practice a bit to learn when to release a mine and when not to, or it can get pretty damn expensive(especially since a single Ripper will cost as much to replace as a Sunslayer torpedo, for roughly half the damage). Missiles for the slightly more experienced pilot.

Missiles take up valuble hardpoints which could be used for more shieldbusters...for something like a Starflier, Patriot, or other low-level LF, I consider those to be the most important thing besides the thruster and shields. If you can use mines effectively, you should always use that isntead and just mount the extra stunpulse the missile would have replaced.
