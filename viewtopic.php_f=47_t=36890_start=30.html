Capital Ship Madley.

'Sir, Technicians are reporting several failures on all the level 3 decks. Power is starting to go offline on level 2 as well.'

'Run on minimal power and compensate. Emergency lights online.'

'Sir... they exploded...'

'WHAT ?'

The Commander was shocked... even him knew less about his ship than the whole bridge. Then, the technician put online a video of engineering. It was a sort of computer room. 1 large bookshelf-like structure, holding several electronic devices. A control panel, and some wires/pipes running through the corridor - Or whatever was this place. It didn,t matter. Then, suddently, all the switches of the panel went to off or standby, whichever existed on the said switch. The monitors started darkening and shut down. The valves on the pipes bursted, passing thru the palm of the hand of an engineer passing by. Then, the end of the corridor started to collapse upon it's own weight, and the rest of the corridor quickly followed. The monitor's glass broke, and the camera's one did too. After that, it is suposed that the lenses died under pressure, because the film ended there.

Everyone stood there, frozen in place. On the radar, a big blue light appeared, next to the ship. Slowly, the ship began its course toward it.

'Engineers, deactivate Aux. Panels 235D to 267A on floor 2, reroute the power intake toward floor 4 instead of floor 3, and shut off the gas pipes ongoing toward the bottom of the ship. Order lockdown of all floors beside 2 and 1. Emergency lights only, power level must be minimal. Begin powering up cruise eng-............

Time seemed to stop. The whole bridge was like a disco, all kinds of lights going through the glass applied themselves to the surface present here, and several BIG, WHITE flashes happened.

....-ines.

The Commander began using his neutral net to set waypoint to Gas Miner Naha.

*No known way found* *Failed* *Negative* *Refused* *Error* was all the Commander could get from the computer. Then, everyone stared at the giant window.

'Where are we ? Unknown. The ship's radar is down. Setting course toward the nearest known base.'

/End of post.

Bundschuh Guerilla Squadron Theta 1, 2, and 3
Location: Dresden

Erich's wing soared through the orange-yellow clouds of Dresden having just completed another mission successfully and without fail. Not even a shield down on this one--the poor bastards they had assassinated were guarded by nothing more than a couple of starfliers. Anyways, some corrupt official was dead, far from civilization. The Bundschuh was one step closer to its ultimate goal.

<Demon Fires, this is Berne. High Command has orders for you. Please Dock at Vogtland for briefing concerning this mission>

<Roger that, Berne. We're on our way.> Erich switched channels. <Demon Fires 1 and 2, this is Fires leader. Change of Plans. We'll rendezvous at Vogtland.> The pending orange mist muffled all sound surrounding the ships, and Erich relaxed in his cockpit in the silence. Rhineland was crumbling.

Erich met his commisioner, Stel von Vergaart, at the bar on Vogtland. He was a middle-aged, balding man who had been close friends with Erich's father. Vergaart looked like he would have been pathetically weak on the battlefield, but Erich knew better. This man was one of the champions of the Bundschuh.

"Erich. Good, you made it. Take a seat. Let me guess--Darkhundt Lager, right? Waiter! great. Now, to business. You remember our contacts with the Order. Of course you do, your father. How could I forget? They need something...artifacts. Several artifacts."

"So...you want us to travel to Corsair territory, discreetly pick up the artifacts and fly back with them in the cargo holds disguised as fertilizers?"

"No, no they already have a shipper moving the artifacts. The only thing is, its against the will of the Corsairs. So the shippers have a high likelihood of being attacked while they're on their way, and we can't let that happen. The Order needs those artifacts.

We've heard of these shippers, the Merry Men, and we know theres another job we need them to do. We also know there's not a high likelihood of their taking the deal unless we give them a reason--besides credits, of course--to do it. Protecting them from Corsairs is a good reason to help us ship the documents."

Erich sighed. "We're no escorts, we're guerillas. But I'll take it."

"Good man. You'll go to Omega 11, jump to 41 and wait by the jump hole for the transports to come. I'll also send two more Sabre wings out to help you. Try not to engage any Corsairs."

"And Stalner..."

"Yes?"

"Reliable" Randy Sega and the Merry Men

-------------------------------------------

The trip to Omicron Theta was uneventful. Too uneventful. Randy couldn't help but get the feeling that is was the calm before the storm. Still, the Merry Men were busy loading their ships with the artifacts and so far nothing had gone wrong. Thank space for small blessings, I guess. He thought as he surveyed Freeport 9's docking area. He and his squad all agreed that getting this mission over and done with as quickly as possible was the best plan. As such the docking bays were living incarnations of orgaized chaos. People were running seemingly willy-nilly from place to place, deperately trying to get their part of the job done. There hadn't been any major league collision as of yet though so the loading was progressing nicely.

"What are you thinking right now?" Georgina had come up behind Randy and slipped her arms around her waist.

"Mmmm..." Randy sighed contentedly. "A permanent cabin on the Hawaii would be nice. And after this mission we'll definitely have enough money."

"And then some." Georgina said. "But would you really give up flying?"

"Hell no." A new voice interjected. It was Arnold Masterson, one of three former Bounty Hunters currently working for the Merry Men. Arnold and his siblings, Doug and Lisa, all came from the mean streets of Texas. They quickly worked their way up the criminal heirarchy of Texas, eventually becoming some of the most wanted unaffiliated criminals in all Liberty. That sort of attention got them caught, though not without some serious effort on the part of the various Liberty security services. It was the LSF who finally managed to nab the Mastersons but, instead of locking them away for good, High Command offered to set them free if they would agree to become Bounty Hunters. Not wanting to give up their freedom, the Mastersons agreed. They quickly racked up an impressive kill score, a fact that attracted the ire of many long-time members of the Guild. Soon the Mastersons found themselves under fire from criminals and fellow hunters. Randy was heading through Sigma-19 when he came across the three siblings. They were fighting a force of Bounty Hunters more than 4 times their size. The Masterson's had one advantage though. Their strength increased exponentially with each sibling. Where one may have been good enough to take on two or three fighters alone, two could take on at least 9 and three were parctically untouchable. Randy saw that this was a winning combination and immediately put them all on the short-list.

"Haha, yeah." Randy said. "Arnold's right. But I'd sure as hell quit working. Maybe get myself a nice Eagle off the Zoners and just go for joyrides every so often."

"Maybe." Arnold said. "For now you might want to know that the crews are almost finished loading and we're going to start prepping for launch."

"Hmmm. Good idea. I'll be off then, love. See you in space." Georgina released Randy and jogged towards her ship, Beta Train.

Randy nodded to Arnold and the two men went their separate ways, Randy to his Train, Arnold to his Bounty Hunter Piranha. As Randy clambered up the ladder to his cockpit the crew cheif signalled that the loading procedures were finished and the deck-hands were clear. Randy hopped into his chair and began running the startup sequence.

"Okay guys, time to run 'em up." Randy announced over the comms. "Freeport Nine this is Merry Men Alpha. Request permission to launch."

"Acknowledged Merry Men Alpha. Good Journey." Came the controller's reply. Docking bay doors opened and mooring lines fell away.

"So boss, how we gonna get to this Nansei place?" Manny asked.

"We'll take the jumphole to Sigma-17, then it's on to Sigma-19 and then Kusari space." Randy answered. "Form up on me and hit your cruise engines, people. It'll take a while to get to the jumphole and I don't want to waste time."

The comm squawked with 20 simultaneous acknowledgements as everyone went to cruise. The Merry Men tore across space towards the jumphole. Suddenly Randy's Neural Net pinged.

"Uh, guys," he said, "I'm getting a tight beam transmission. It's...it's from the Bundschuh..."
