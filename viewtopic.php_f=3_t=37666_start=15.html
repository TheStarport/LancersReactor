A bit of physics maybe?

In a way, if they made a kind of light-speed drive (or slightly slower), you may reason that it would take years to get from system to system. It would. But not for the people inside.

Now you see, as you approach the speed of light, time dilates. It spreads out. So if you travel at almost the speed of light for 4.5 light years (say from here to Sirius), it will only have seemed a few seconds to you. Something at nearly the speed of light thanks to spatial distortions could have a crew that should be, in all rights, hundreds of years old, but not have aged more than a few hours.

Of course, there's the problem of getting to that speed, but let's assume they did something like that, because that would be really the only way.

They might have found jump holes and sent probes in, sending radio signals back. But radio waves can only travel at the speed of light, so they would perhaps never get their signals back...!
