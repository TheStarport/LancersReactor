Thu Mar 17, 2005 3:09 pm by Argh

Hmm. Well... here are my thoughts.

You *could* implement a cutscene as a THN file. But THNs, in a multiplayer environment, are generally only triggered by landing events or entering a Room. And these are on the client-side, not the server-side... you cannot "push" such events or content to clients.

You cannot see THNs in normal play- they were designed for cutscenes within the game engine during SP, landing events, entering Rooms, etc... and that's all.

Sooo... so far as I can tell, it wouldn't be possible to, say... show a "news broadcast" to every player on a MP server at once. But there *are* some ways that you could get close. Unfortunately, these ways all involve your players being willing to download a new version of the mod every time you put out a broadcast.

For example, you can very easily change the startup movie to whatever you want. It's really not a problem to do so. Same deal with the music used- check out my mod (Warriors of the Sky) or any of the other big TCs (such as Free Worlds) to see what I mean. These are easy-to-implement and minor changes.

Implementing an all-new THN is *not* a minor change, and is rather tricky to implement. Moreover, animation with THNs is very crude in most respects- all IK animation, for example, is part of the file structure of CMPs, and the heavy-duty modders we have around here (I'm just a remora, nibbling off of their technical expertise) haven't figured out how to implement new animated parts just yet, unfortunately.

Check out the THNs used by the Free Worlds mod to see modded THNs at their most advanced. A character named Wasabe (who shows up here once in a blue moon) created most've these scripts, and unfortunately the process used isn't documented very well, so far as I am aware.
