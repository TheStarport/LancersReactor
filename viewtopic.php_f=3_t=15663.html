What I dont like about Freelancer and a few questions

my opinion is based on the demo version of freelancer, so please correct me if I get anything wrong.

- controls and ship systems are much too simple. makes the game much more of a action game than a action simulation like privateer and others were. I dont care about that joysticks arent supported, but at least they could have made the mouse more configurable and make it disable the flight controls when you access map view and other windows where you need a mouse pointer. that would have made the free flight totally unneeded. at least all other functions can be controlled with the keyboard. I would also like to see a thrust control via the mouse wheel, if I cant use the controller on my joystick...

- I really dont like the racial systems divertion. you know, english sector, japanese sector, american sector, german sector. that makes the whole universe behind this game very childish and even a bit racist.

- the sound (esspecially voices) sounds like it comes out of a pc speaker. but maybe thats only in the demo version. correct me if Im wrong.

- the cutscenes and character interaction graphics suck. period. why did game designers start to make 3D cutscenes lately? 3D graphics arent that well (esspecially animations) yet to make them cutscene-worthy. well... at least in this game.

- conversation is boring and simple. very simple. It also makes the game look cheap if your character always repeats his sentences like "uhhhh huh, interesting, whatcha got, etc. sometimes it even doesnt fit like when a man in a bar asks you if youre new and your character answers "whatcha got?"
tho this system makes the radio traffic in space AWESOME!

and I also have a few questions since I only played the demo yet:

1. is there 4.1 or 5.1 sound support in the full version? my rear speakers wont work in the demo version, but it may be my computer since on some games they work and on others they only work when I disable voice com (half-life i.e.).

2. in the demo some conversations are being ended before all is said. is that the case in the full version aswell?

all in all the game is still very nice and I will buy it for sure. but I still think it hurt the game a lot that chris roberts left and/or mircosoft took over digital anvil. I think it would be almost perfect now if chris roberts would have been the lead designer till it was finished!

Chandrasekhar Limit

Thu May 01, 2003 4:47 am by Chandrasekhar Limit

Having a country of homogenious population in and of itself is not "racist." People who speak the same language, observe the same customs and had ancestral roots from one region tend to aggregate together and want to govern themselves. That's no crime and is perfectly acceptable as long as they do not discriminate against another group and persecute others based on things like race, religion, etc.

You can look at the Houses in FL as political entities. Libertarians prefer their American-style government with 3 branches. Bretonians prefer their monarchy. Rheinlanders prefer their system of Chancellorship and Bundestag. The Kusaris prefer whatever it is they got ("shogunate???" That's definitely too medieval for my tastes.) There is no racism in having different political systems.

Remember in the WC plot there was a war against an alien race bent on the annihilation of the human race. Under such conditions humankind would unite to fight a common foe. But do you remember what happened after the Kilrathi War ended in WC4? Political fragmentation into Confed and Border Worlds. Is that racist? I don't think so.

Same story with FL-- After making their escape from the Coalition to Sirius, the alliance population no longer have a common threat to bind them together. And since Mr. Life-of-the-party Trent (sarcasm of course) and Company nipped the common threat (Nomads) in the bud, there still isn't any reason for the Houses to band together into one political entity.

That all-humans-as-one-country is not necessarily in humankind's future, and there is nothing "racist" about that.

Chandrasekhar Limit

Thu May 01, 2003 11:38 am by Chandrasekhar Limit

to Aidle:

No disagreement from me on most of your points, except one...

The number of CDs a game comes on is not exactly an indicator of its complexity. Privateer 2 and Starlancer have more than 1 CD not because the games are more complex but because they used quite a bit of streaming full-motion video playback, particularly Privateer 2, which used human actors and shot actual scenes (and takes A LOT Of disk space). Starlancer did utilize more on-the-fly renders for certain transitions, but it still used FMV playback too (though instead of using real actors it is computer-rendered).
Freelancer on the other hand used FMV only for the intro. All the other transitions and cutscenes are rendered real-time. That's why it only has 1 CD.
