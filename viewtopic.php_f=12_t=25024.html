Ugnaught

Tue Oct 07, 2003 6:32 pm by Ugnaught

I built a whole bunch of model rockets when I was a teenager. It can be an expensive hobby (when your only source of income is mowing lawns) because it's really easy to lose the rocket. I shot rockets off all the time when I lived in Boise, but then I moved to Eastern Washington where it's a lot more windy and I was too chicken to launch any there. I didn't have any friends who liked rockets so I didn't build any more. My best one is a Phoenix Missile, but I've never launched it.

Model rocketry is completely safe if you follow the Model Rocket Code. And in any case you wouldn't be able to blow yourself up unless you were making your own engines.

One thing I learned the hard way when I was twelve. Don't use hot-glue to put together model rockets. Sure, you can build one really fast that way, but the heat from motor will melt the glue and you can lose your fins or nose cone. This happened to me. My rocket went up and when it deployed the parachute the nose cone and the parachute floated into some neighborhood, and the rest of the rocket fell like a stone, but it's pretty useless without a nose cone.
