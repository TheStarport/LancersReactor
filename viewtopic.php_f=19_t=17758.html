Neo_Seeker

Sun May 25, 2003 2:53 pm by Neo_Seeker

In PvP games, Manuverability and surprise are the most IMPORTANT points in the game. If you have an eagle and the enemy has a Titan for example, you can easily evade him and just fly around him, killling him. However, surprise is the key. If you can get in the back of someone (dont be cheap, though) and he doesnt notice you you can most likely down his shields and do half his hull health. Also, fire rockets, mines, torpedoes when your enemy least expects it or in a situation where you cannot miss

Thorkild

Thu May 29, 2003 12:37 am by Thorkild

I don't use the engines anymore, just the thruster / reverse / sliding thrusters. I wish they had thrusters to slide me directly up and down, but that is not the case. Leaving the engines disabled allows you to maintain very high velocity without dropping below 90% on your thrusters. Leaving the engines on will constantly bring you back to 80, while disabling them will keep you up at 190 - 200, even through a turn.

I have been killed twice in PvP by freelancers using torpedoes. I'm not sure how they did it, as I was moving laterally at high speed. I hope that it wasn't a cheat, because I'd like to learn the technique. I'd like to add that, aside from those two torp kills, I've been relatively untouched in PvP combat.
