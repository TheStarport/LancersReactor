Xenogear_0001

Thu Aug 28, 2003 5:09 am by Xenogear_0001

The biggest problem that I've estimated people would face with a mod like this is that I have no idea how a space fighter could, in a practical and useful manner, transform WHILE FLYING into anything else. Animations alone won't cut it. Think about it, the entire function of the ship shifts when it goes into gerwalk or soldier form. They move slower, have better maneuverability, and can use a wider array of weapons in soldier mode, and how do you get the FL engine to just change that stuff on the fly? If indeed that is possible, then it would require HEAVY HEAVY modding of hard-coded materials inherent in the game. Personally though, I would be happy with just a Valkyrie jet model for a ship. Give me a VF-19 Excalibur, VF-1 whats-it-crap, or a VF-11 Thunderbolt any day and I'll be a happy camper. Especially the Excalibur though, love those reverse canard wings!
What I've noticed in general about new ship models and the like coming from the modding community is that, like the ships already in the game, they all share a common design feature: Really big cockpits and relatively small main bodies and wings. I guess it wouldn't matter in space, but when I think of a fighter, I think fighter jet. And if you look at 'em, the main body and wings are quite large in comparison with the cockpit. I guess that if I wanted a jet fighter in game, it would have to be pretty big, and I'm cool with that. Just an observation, is all.
Guess I'll get cracking on making that Valkyrie FL compatible.

From my experience, it goes like this. You need a picture of the valkyrie preferebly from the top, left and front angles. After that, create a mesh that is more or less the same shape as the valkyrie and edit from there making sure to keep an eye on the angles. This method doesn't work too well for curved objects but it should suffice.
