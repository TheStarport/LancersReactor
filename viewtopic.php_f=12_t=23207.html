mustang

Thu Aug 21, 2003 3:06 am by mustang

It's saying that it can't find the email server that you specified in your settings.

You kind of seem pretty uncertain about the whole thing so i'll give you a quick run down. Your internet service provider or ISP has a high end computer (known as an email server) that sends and recieves your email on your behalf. When you check your email, your PC will contact the email server and check if any email is waiting for you, If there is your PC will download it.

Now for your PC to contact the email server it needs to know it's address. (It's the same as if you wanted to ring a friend, you will need to know his phone number.) However with computer networks there are millions of these addresses so we prefer to give them names, for example my mail server is called mail.bigpond.net.au

So basically the conversation goes something like this.

My PC: Hey, is anyone out there called mail.bigpond.net.au?
mail.bigpond.net.au: Yeah that's me
My PC: Can I have my mail?
mail.bigpond.net.au: Who are you?
My PC: Im mustang
mail.bigpond.net.au: You gotta password?
My PC: Yep it's ******
mail.bigpond.net.au: Cool, here's your mail.

What is happening with you, is that your PC is trying to contact a mail server that isn't there. The most common mistake is mis-spelling the server name.
If you using Outlook Express check your setting by clicking on the tools menu, then accounts. Highlight your account and then click on properties, you will be able to check and change you mail server name in there.

However, your internet service provider should have given you step by step instructions on how to set up your account. If not, instructions should be on thier web site.
