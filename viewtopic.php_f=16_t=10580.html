When you get non-mission time you should use this to make money, you next mission starts when you hit a certain monetary goal, but you can hold off starting these missions for a bit and concentrate on making some cash.

As to escaping, agree on learning the intermittant afterburner thrust, also read some good threads on engine cut maneuvering and sliding. This is a tough fight for your lvl. Also you will be able to use salvaged weapons from destroyed ships a while before you can purchase them from merchants. Find a weapon type you like (I prefere a fire rate around 4 with a good punch). Explore for lost ship wrecks.

Lots to do, so little time. Good Luck

Easy Rhino

Wed Mar 19, 2003 12:56 pm by Easy Rhino

First, do the bare minimum you have to do to progress the story. Keep hitting "n" to see if you're forgetting a specific task. Here's ideas from memory

(Don't use goto, stick with free flight)
1) Immediately go to the first waypoint. (which will be blocked).
2) Kill time staying alive (I blew up some Navy ships, nothing better to do). To stay hard to hit, either use afterburner some, or for really extreme, try using cruise engines and maneuvering wildly. It's fun.
3) let Walker come in and blow up the battleship.
4) "hotlane" with the trade gate to West Point. to do this, keep in free flight, use your cruise engines, and fly toward the gate. Select the gate by clicking on it or whatever. Keep in cruise mode, and fly right towards the green-lit gate. When you get nearly on top of it (the gate circle reaches edge of screen), hit the dock key (f3), and you will dock very quickly, and take less damage.
5) Repeat at west point.

I died the first time I tried the mission. The second time, I ran away with cruise engines, and lost because I was a coward.
