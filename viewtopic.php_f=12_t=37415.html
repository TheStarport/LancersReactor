Chips

Sun Jan 30, 2005 1:56 am by Chips

Nice post Neo, can see why you would have then reconsidered - Of course, if you wanted, you could have edited it simply enough.

As for making a thread for 'serious' things - to what end?

The comical thread was made because everyone who had a joke made a new thread about it - it clutters up forums when that happens.....and people have to search hard to find funny stuff - or may miss it! Having one centralised thread means that you can go and read a whole bunch of jokes/funny stuff at once. Its exceedingly 'handy', with the common theme being humour....and the 'subject' matter for each post is contained in one single post - with no need to run into other posts etc.


As for making one thread that contains all serious discussion - it runs into problems. Not least, if more than one or two discussions were going, it would be near impossible to read through, or keep abreast of.....
The scope of subject matter is so wide that the continuation of theme for a discussion upon on subject matter would be lost in amongst the many topics that may be discussed. You may have seen the 'answer question/ask question' type threads - where often instead of answering one before asking their own, you would just get a whole ream of questions in a row instead. You may not get a discussion, but lots of posts of differing things to be discussed..

To that end, its far easier to make a new thread for each discussion topic - simpler and easier on all accounts....especially for the reader - who could see a topic, and read it if they wanted....not having to wade through pages of other things that may not be of interest. How about if you wanted to post your view on an earlier discussion after a few pages had elapsed? It would break the continuation of discussion again

I personally think it would be best to discuss each in individual threads - after all - threads aren't a premium

As for not discussing religion or politics: The open nature of these forums, where any person is invited to interact with anyone else - stating their views (within the rules/regs) and opinions upon any subject that tickles their fancy....its obvious that subjects like those two, which invariably are deep seated topics in peoples hearts - and therefore they are passionate about them, create an opportunity for it going 'wrong' on an epic scale. (long sentance!)
I don't think I have ever seen a political discussion between opposing people with opposing opinions, who aren't great mates, not getting 'dirty'. Same for religious topics, and even if they manage it - all it takes is one person to say something catalystic - and it all goes 'Pete Tong'.

Thats just my view of course, feel free to debate if you disagree or think I'm wrong
