@Loc

Nice story but, sorry, not accurate. Koreans, yes. There was a fleet composed mainly of Korean sailors on warships built by Korean shipwrights that was sent to invade Japan ....TWICE in the late 13th Century. However, this was not an expression Korean imperialism. Rather, Korea had been brought under the rule of the Mongol Horde as had most of China at that point and pretty much all of Northern Asia and Western Russia right up to the Polish steppes. So Mongol soldiers also were involved in the attempted invasions.

The Divine Wind was a typhoon that wrecked a number of the invasion fleet and forced the rest to retreat to Korea.

Those invaders that landed were defeated by the defending Japanese who knew, basically, that were it not for the storms, they surely would have been defeated.

This wrecking of the Mongol invasion fleet by Typhoons happened twice and saved the day for Japan twice the two invasions were about 7 years apart.

The "banzai" cheer has a very rough translation into something like 10,000 joys. It doesn't make much sense in western languages but has meaning based upon the religious and philosophical traditions in East Asia. In one of the Chinese languages I think the same word is "mansei" or something close to that. It does not originate from the Mongol invasion as a uniquely Japanese word, so to speak.

In the Far East, in both Korea and Japan, the various Chinese languages spoken during the Dynasties that were in power have the same significance and influence upon those two countries as Latin had on Western Europe.
