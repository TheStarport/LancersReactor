[WP]Icefox

Tue May 24, 2005 11:45 am by [WP]Icefox

Honestly I lack the overview so far in which way you guys work, but for me things go into a systematic direction usually according to the following scheme. I just think about what I want to combine

1) What kind of story?
a) Pirate story
b) Freighter pilot story (such can be interesting too I think)
c) Something playing on a station
d) A political story
e) A criminal story

2) Background
Basically 2 choices
a) Freelancer with consideration of Starlancer past
b) Without it

3) If it is a war story (which in fact most stories I have seen in the forum I attended were) - who fights whom?
a) Humans against Nomads
b) Humans against humans
c) Total war including anarchy
d) Never done it, but any new idea

4) Perspective
a) One character (has hardly ever been possible for me)
b) More than one character and of course interaction

5) As a result the setting
For myself goes that my stories usually involved action on battleships. I was too much of a Wing Commander fan to do it elseway. A part also played on planets or space stations.

So out of this it is usually quite easy for me to choose a nice setup. I kind of try to surprise the readers. Character development always plays an important role for me and it is often nice to see how the thing gets a dynamic element by itself. Conflicts between characters and their emotions are a good thing to play with. The more controversial the setting is and the more conflicts it offers, the better and more interesting becomes the story in my opinion.

It is nice to make the coalition attack sirius and tell a part of story from the point of view of a renegade Corsair working for a Rhineland security agency for example.

Freelancer offers in combination with Starlancer a lot of possibilities. If one even considers the actual political situation it is really no problem to make up an interesting plot.

I hope I did not write things by now which all of you already know.
Probably most of you do, but if there is one good thing it, this post hopefully makes sense. For reading everything at one day I also lack the time.
