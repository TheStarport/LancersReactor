What if they said it took place in the distant future? Would that make it more plausible? I mean, at the moment, a situation such as that of Freelancer is not plausible, but no one has a problem with it because it takes place more than a thousand years from now. I like it because, while it's not likely to happen right now, it COULD happen. That's a major part of logic and philosophy. An argument doesn't have to be true. It's a matter of IF the premises were true, then the conclusion is plausible. So.... IF it were possible to stall the earth's core and do everything in the movie, THEN it would be plausible. Just like.... IF it were he 23rd century, THEN it would be plausible for there to be a war through he galaxy between two human factions and one could gain the upper hand, forcing the other to construct ships to carry their people to a distant part of the universe where 800 years later they colonize distant planets and systems and travel and trade among themselves and encounter an alien race bent on their destruction.

IF it were possible, THEN it would be plausible. That's what science fiction is all about. If you want reality, go outside. As for me, I'm goin' to the movies, because I'm sick of reality.
