Spanner Monkey

Sun May 15, 2005 12:41 pm by Spanner Monkey

200+ hours of work GONE


Well, unless you wrote several gigs worth of stuff in that 200+ hours - then you should have backed up irreplacable work at least. Its not necessary to back up games, and downloadable material. You don't need backups of each 1-2Gb game you have installed - because you have the disks. If its movies, I use a 2nd HDD to store them instead... so they are relatively safe. Music - I burn it to CD and also store it on my 2nd HDD.
However, they are all recoverable.

My work, however, I keep on CDs and on my own website. I have yet to write anything (program, spreadsheets or anything else) that exceeds a CD when all combined - so if your 200+ hours of work was artwork, school work, projects etc - then its your own fault for not keeping a backup. Everything else is replaceable with some downloading.

That is what a backup is - not keeping extra copies of stuff that can be obtained again in a few hours - but work that is NOT replicable that easily.
