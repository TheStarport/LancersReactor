Sat Dec 13, 2003 6:06 pm by gromit

Ahhh John Rhys-Davies, truly britains most under-rated actor. Over here he's been on our TV for decades, thankfully the rest of the world is finally discovering him.

As for the indiana jones trilogy...what can i say...they're 3 of my favourite movies of all time (well, definitely up there in the top 50, and thats saying something). I'm hoping for the new trilogy box set this christmas...

The diamond on a stick thing. Well of course you're talking about the staff which when held in the right place, at the right time will point to the location of the Ark of the Covenant on the map in the underground map room. Raiders of the Lost Ark of course. Its been a while since of seen it, but I think it was a ruby that was in the middle of the staff-head. I'm sure Uncle Taw will tell us when he stumbles onto this thread

I must however defend the second film - Indiana Jones and the Temple of Doom. Yes ok the kid and the girl are annoying. Yes I know it dragged on a bit and the script was mediocre. But! The chase scene through the mine was one of the best of its genre - truly original at the time - and still exciting to watch today. Similarly, the famous rope bridge scene was spectacular to watch on the big screen as a kid. The suspense was fantastic and many many film makers since I have tried to recreate it, and failed. Yes, overall, its a bit naff and doesn't compare to the other two - but give the film its dues, it was groundbreaking and contained some amazing stunts that I will never forget.

And the other two - I can't seperate them - they're completely different movies. One is a comedy/thriller and the other is pure adventure. But I think I would choose Raiders for the pure thrill, originality, cinematography and script...and of course because Karen Allen looks gorgeous when the monkey is on her shoulder

Look out for Indy 4 - due out in 2005 - cowritten by Mr Lucas, directed by Mr Spielberg, Harrison Ford confirmed in the title role.
