LordZach

Sun Jun 08, 2003 3:40 am by LordZach

The hornet is a starter ship not a starting ship. This means you can basically go right out and purchase it from the start. In order to buy it without crashing you need to either add the engine it uses, ge_li_engine_01 to engine_equip.ini or edit the ship package to another engine.

The OB jumphole in S-19 doesn't go to OB. If you click on it and go to the system it leads to you'll notice it says nav-map not avalible so it's probably one of the SP systems without an official designation.

To get past the "phantom guns" issue, don't try dismounting them. When you are buying a ship with phantom guns simply click on the slot and transfer it back to the dealer. You won't get any money for them but at least you'll have those mounts back.

Play through the original game first. Here are my reasons.

The Freelancer Balance Mod actually makes the game harder which is cool and it has a ton more ships. But the NPC's also fly in different sectors where they don't in the original game so if you are hostile to some they may be where you don't expect them to be and you will get whacked in a lesser ship.

By playing the original version you will get a good feel for game play and controls of your ship then when you do mod your game you will be prepared to take advantage of whatever ship you choose to fly.

Also the Rebalance mod has some bug issues which they are fixing so perhaps by the time you finish with your original game play a newer version will be out as we are on 1.4b.

Pandarr

Sun Jun 08, 2003 10:08 am by Pandarr

I agree with Pressgod. The original version is a good introduction to this section of space and all that is in it. Once you have a little familiarity with the way things work you will enjoy FLRebalance much more.

In regards to the "Can't Sell My Engine" problem : they are hard coded into the ship models, selling them would be like selling a wing or the cockpit. If you want to upgrade your engines you have 2 choices,
1) buy and mount the upgrade then dump the original in space somewhere or
2) MOD the ship file directly to have the engine you want.
At this time there is no know way to make the engines saleable.

p.s. I have NO first hand modding or programming knowledge. The above information is gleaned from reading previous post by modders/programmers and personal experience.
