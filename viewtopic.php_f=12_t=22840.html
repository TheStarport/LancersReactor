Games that reach out to you.

Amongst the many games I have played, there are only two that have "reached out" to me, if you get my meaning.

Firstly it's Morrowind, with it's beautiful sunset/rise effects (especially the sun reflections on the water surface) and it's overall scenery.
And there is of course Freelancer. It gives me a kind of homecoming feeling, when I take my regular trade run with my trusty Humpback, that takes me between the Colorado and Tau-23 systems (I won't say what I trade, cause this isn't the spoiler forum ). After doing 7-8 of these runs and getting use to them, there is nothing like coming "home" to Colorado, cruising past the Battleship Rio Grande and thinking "only one trade lane left" .

Being affected by a game like this doesn't prove that I am insane, it only proves that the game developers really did their job

I am an RTS kinda guy as well, but love anything having to do with the sci-fi aspect. I will give a bit away about myself here, but Star Control 2 really really grabbed me. After that, it was spacey-type time wasters all the way. I played Privateer on the same machine I played SC2 on, and damn was it good.

More recently, I couldn’t help but throw in StarCraft. I still LAN-play that with some of my friends. And Homeworld…like Freelancer, good story, good music, nice to look at, and very engaging. It gave the player an identity, just like Freelancer does.

It also didn’t rank incredibly high on the replayability list, and was modded inside and out. I think that, in some ways, how…shallow…Freelancer (SP, out-of-the-box) was really spurred the players into making something more out of it. Other games have had, say, 3 equivalent playable ships, but rarely have I seen them spark the debate that has come up here.

Ahh…I relish any trip down Gaming-Nostalgia Lane. Freelancer gets a prominent address there.

Thanks for the list everyone...I'll have to give some of these a shot.
