Crash-Drive

Fri Apr 18, 2003 2:57 pm by Crash-Drive

If the equipment dealer has an item in their list, and they won't sell it to you, it's not because the item is "out of stock", it's because you need to have a better rep. with the faction that runs the base... they don't trust you... you might try to use their weapon technology against them. You can either pay to have your rep. hacked, take jobs from the faction, or just kill their enemies. The more kick-ass the weapon is, the more friendly you need to be with them.

Everyone seems to gravitate to the Nomad guns as "the best", but you can't buy those, you have to "earn" them another way. However, of the guns you can purchase, the Outcast Kraken Type 2 c9 particle gun is hard to beat -- high efficiency, highest damage output (higher than most of the c10 guns). A Kraken Turret matches up with the guns nicely on a Sabre: identical range, speed, and refire rate as the guns... or, try a Corsair Rapier Turret instead, which also matches range, speed, and refire rate with the Krakens, but is a pulse weapon to tear down shields faster. The Civilian Adv. Debilitator Turret is almost identical to the Rapier Turret, but more energy efficient, and also has cool projectile gfx, whereas the Corsair pulse guns have "invisible" projectiles.

You can get the Kraken guns/turret at Ruiz Base in Omicron Beta, The Rapier Turret at Planet Crete or Tripoli Shipyard in Omicron Gamma, and the Adv. Debilitator Turret at Detroit Munitions in Manhattan or Yukawa Shipyard in Honshu.

Crash-Drive

Sat Apr 19, 2003 2:58 am by Crash-Drive

The SkyBlast B guns are fun to "hose down" your target with, but they suck down energy very quickly... and they're noisy as heck, too. And, BTW, using a SkyBlast Turret B on a Sabre in conjunction with the guns can cause problems, because the turret has a greater range than the guns, so you can waste the shots from the guns if the target is outside their range but inside the turret's range; and the turret has a different projectile speed than the guns, which messes up the accuracy of the targetting system.

SkyBlast B:
244 dmg x 8.33 refire = 2032.5 dmg/sec.
244 dmg / 61 energy = 4.0 efficiency
Range = 600m

Kraken Type 2:
586 dmg x 4.00 refire = 2344 dmg/sec.
586 dmg / 122 energy = 4.8 efficiency
Range = 700m

Now, you may like the SkyBlast Bs because they give "more chances to hit" the target, but the glass-is-half-empty view of this is that they give more chances to miss. At any rate, the numbers above speak for themselves.

Nightsilver

Sat Apr 19, 2003 3:08 am by Nightsilver

I vote for the highest possible single-shot damage setup. If you're using the SkyB's or a similar weapon, chances are you'll miss...a lot. Since those things suck energy, you might as well make the energy count and control your fire - only shoot when you're relatively sure of a hit.

That said, I would reccommend one of two setups. First, if your aim is really good, I say use this:
2x TDC (Tizona del Cid)
4x Diamondback/Cereberus (any combination of those is fine, since they're identical except for looks).
If you're using a Sabre, add on a Salamanca Turret, since your turret fires forward and Sal matches speeds with the other stuff.

If you're not quite so confident in your aim, then I suggest:
2x TDC
4x Nomad Cannon/Blaster (again, don't matter what combination you use)
Sal Turret

Then, you should always use Adv. Brigandine HF Shield, Adv. Countermeasures, Adv. Thruster, and Ripper Mines.
