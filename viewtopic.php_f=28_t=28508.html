Black Label - Privateer in Sirus - and All Good - mods - Com

Hi there.

Just thought I would let you all know about the Black Label Mod and what the intentions for it are. Basically it will be a best of the best mod, containing the best models for ships, bases and equipment currently around. To date it only contains two ships (the slipstream and the starviper) and one weapon (the chain gun) but if more high quality mods come out I will be happy to add them.

First of all special thanks go to APS Shipworks, FireBase and Harrier for all kindly granting permission for the use of their models in the mod. Thanks guys!

Second, although it is a little harder than just offering the ships for sale I thought it important to integrate the ships into the NPC factions as part of the mod. If the models are high quality, so should the mod itself be. You can actually fight against both these ships in the game, the slipstream belonging to the Bundschuh and the starviper belonging to the Farmers Alliance. Any new ships will also be assigned to factions.

If you find defects or faults, have general comments (good or bad) or feel you should bring another mod to my attention for potential inclusion in this mod please feel free to post here. That's it for now.


Good Luck Out There.
Acid.

Acid

Wed May 26, 2004 12:44 am by Acid

Just a quick update to all that have posted about mod compatibility and test servers.

1) there is no plan at the moment to implement a Black Label server of which I am aware. I am certainly not planning to do it, but if others would like to I would be happy to help them where I can.

2) At this time, I am not planning to make any changes to the mod to increase compatibility. it is simply too hard. I am working on this in my spare time, and so are other mod groups who are also making changes to their own mods. The point is that without a central authority dictating who can alter what, it becomes quite difficult to constantly alter mods to make them compatible with other mods.

I should point out here that I am NOT advocating the creation of any such central authority, I am just using it to demonstrate my point. Personally, I would just try it, sometimes (rarely) FLMM gets compatibility a little wrong, so give it a shot and see what happens.

Regards,
Acid.
