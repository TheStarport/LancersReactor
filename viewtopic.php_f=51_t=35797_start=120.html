H.Q. Victor

Thu Mar 15, 2007 4:09 pm by H.Q. Victor

The old saves are still on your computer. They are in the mydocuments/mygames/freelancer folder. When you start a new game, the old saves are moved to a folder usually with the name of the mod used oor the saved games name. Open that folder, copy and paste back to the singleplayer forum, making sure the current saves and autosaves are gone. You will also need to make sure FLMM has the same mods used in the saved game are activated.

bakedpotato

Wed Nov 07, 2007 4:23 am by bakedpotato

The TNG Mod most likely was made using the SDK which is basically most of the
Quickfix files.
I wouldn't use anything else with TNG.
What I suggest is start with a clean install of Freelancer - by clean I mean delete your Freelancer folder which is located at C:\Program Files\Microsoft Games\Freelancer and then install Freelancer again.
Next delete any saved games which are located in My Documents\My Games\Freelancer - both the Singleplayer and Multiplayer folders are sub folders in the Freelancer folder... delete any files in those two folders (Singleplayer and Multiplayer).

Next activate the TNG Mod without using any patches or any other Mod's other than the TNG Mod and you should be good to go...

The problem with TNG and most of the older mods is that they use the freelancer.exe that came with the original Microsoft Games version. Ouside of the USA you now can normally only buy a budget version of Freelancer, these don't have the M$ security systems as M$ are paranoid about letting other companies near their stuff. End result is that M$ gave them a legal version of the nocd exe to use, this is why your getting the "Insert Correct CD-Rom" message, the TNG exe is looking for the security features that your disk just doesn't have.

Head over to GCW and have a search, they have a large number of NoCD's for most versions of TNG.
