psi_op, you do not have to be at Ozu's base when you hit level 16. Your rep sheet is a little unbalanced when you are level 15 but that makes travelling more interesting. The problem is that some important, neutral factions can turn hostile in a second if a ship from a hostile (to you) ally of theirs appears on the scene; so be prepared to make a quick getaway. Also, the campaign gives you nice opportunities for a little shopping on the side during one of the later missions.

However, you don't have to worry about missing any ships. The only ship with a level 16 requirement is the one you come across during the campaign anyway.

Sherlog

Mon Jun 23, 2003 10:03 pm by Sherlog

First, you get the whole Sirius sector - systems, bases, ships, equipment, secret shipwrecks and all.

Second, you get the full campaign. Mission 1 is just a tutorial and a teaser, shooting fish in a barrel as it were. But the campaign does pick up pace and the story missions do become really challenging as you progress. Also, you'll get to see more of Juni, meet a lot of people including the guy who raised Trent, a wily professor, a guy who walks off with an advanced Liberty Navy battleship and doesn't get caught, etc. pp.

Third, you get to play MP.

Fourth, you get to use all the mods, not only the tiny handful that can be used with the demo.

The game isn't all that expensive and it is worth every cent, IMHO.
