From the NY Times Aug 3, 2004


The husband of a missing Salt Lake City woman was arrested today and charged with suspicion of murder, the Salt Lake City police said.

Mark Hacking, the husband of Lori Kay Hacking, was arrested and charged with one count of suspicion of aggravated murder as he was released from a psychiatric hospital where he had been since the day after he reported his wife missing two weeks ago. He was transferred today to a jail, where he is under suicide watch, Police Chief Rick Dinse said.

"We believed very early in the investigation that she was a victim of a crime and that her husband may very well have been involved," Chief Dinse said at a televised news conference.

Mr. Hacking told the police on July 19, a Monday, that his wife had not shown up for work. Her car was later found near a jogging trail, but her body has still not been found.

At the first word of her disappearance, hundreds of volunteers spent days searching for any sign of Ms. Hacking, who is said to have been pregnant at the time she disappeared. But this weekend, Ms. Hacking's family called off the search, saying they had new information from Mr. Hacking.

The chief would not give many details of Ms. Hacking's fate, or how it was led to the charges against Mr. Hacking, but did say that they believed that she was murdered inside the couple's apartment.

"We know where the homicide happened and we believe it happened in the apartment," he said. "We did recover evidence from the car."

Last week, the police began focusing their search on a nearby two-acre dump and indicated that they were looking at Mr. Hacking as a "person of interest," although at the time they did not call him a suspect.

The day after he reported his wife missing, Mr. Hacking, 28, was seen running around at night wearing only sandals outside a motel where he had taken a room. He checked himself into a psychiatric hospital.

Investigators focused on Mr. Hacking after learning that he had been at a store buying a new mattress just before reporting his wife missing. Investigators have refused to discuss news reports that they found a mattress in a nearby trash bin.

They also said they had learned that Mr. Hacking lied about graduating from college and being accepted to a medical school in North Carolina. The couple had been preparing to move to North Carolina.

Mr. Dinse said investigators would continue to sift through the 3,000 tons of garbage at the landfill because of the possibility that Ms. Hacking's body might be there. Such a prospect "is not something that makes all of comfortable," he said. "It's not a pleasant thought. The thought that she might have to stay there is something else we don't want to think about."

He said investigators were determined to find her body if it was buried in the landfill.

"They've been out there many, many nights," the chief said. "There's not one complaint from anybody. They're all committed to finding Lori."
