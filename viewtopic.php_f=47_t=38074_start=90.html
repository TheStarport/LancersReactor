Hahukum Konn, hidden as he was, saw two people approach; the woman, and a rather good-looking man next to her. He stepped out from under the wing, and walked around the nose of the ship to greet them. Just as he opened his mouth to speak, a Kusari man rushed up to the three of them like a man possessed, and breathlessly informed Tanya that she had a message.

After that kerfluffle died down, Konn spoke. "Tanya, I must apologize for obtaining information about you without your permission, so that I know your first name, but you do not know my name."

Tanya and Travis looked at each other and regarded the blond man before them, who they could see also wore strange-looking devices on his face: Two round lenses, bridged over his nose, with frames extending back to his ears. This struck a chord of memory in Tanya, but she could not remember what they were. Also, the man's accent was mainly Libertonian, but to Tanya's practiced ear it sounded as though it had been worn smooth by some years in Bretonia. She wondered, how did this guy know where my ship was?

Konn was continuing. "My name, thus, is Hahukum Konn. As to why I am here, I confess I've gotten you, madam, into some hot water. I told some rather large Kusari goons earlier today that you had Niobium and that I was picking it up from you, so as to buy myself some time before they took my H-fuel and ship from me.

"I then came back here, having memorized the serial number on your visitor pass, and was able to locate your ship. Thus, my apology for my unauthorized quest for some information."

He definitely spent some time in Bretonia , Tanya thought, as she began to get irritated at the way this small-time trader had managed to get her in trouble with who knew what crime syndicate was shaking down people for their cargo in New Tokyo. She got even more irritated as she realized that this was just another unwanted event in a series of unwanted events that had just happened to her, what with Rutger's sudden let-down, the urgent message from Rob and now this!

She yelled, "Mister, I don't know who the hell you think you are, but you don't have the right to involve people in the messes you make and then expect them to help you clean it up!" Tanya was itching to pull her concealed gun out and blast this wiseguy in the head; if she couldn't punch Rutger's face she could at least take out her anger on this freelancer.

Travis got nervous when he realized both of them were armed with weapons that wouldn't register on energy scanners in major Kusari population centers. Mr. Konn probably didn't know this, and was likely unarmed. Not a fair contest...

Konn had his hands held away from his body to show he was unarmed, and was speaking calmly. "Madam, I can only plead desperation in light of the fact that I was likely to be roughed up and my ship and cargo stolen in any event."

Travis spoke softly, so only Tanya could hear: "You know, Tanya, I wouldn't shoot this guy. He did try to warn you instead of just taking off from here and leaving you in the soup with some nasty Kusari goons on your tail."

Tanya was torn between physical action and logical thought. She had to admit to herself that it could have been an even more explosive situation if she had been left to fend off the criminal element in Kusari without knowing why she had to do it, and that the gentleman in the elevator had the courtesy, such as it was, to let her know he'd gotten her into his hot water.

After Tanya's face worked its way from an expression of fury to a more rational appearance, Konn breathed a sigh of relief. He certainly hadn't expected to bump into a woman who was more than the ordinary tourist she seemed, and a male companion who was apparently just as involved in whatever she was doing.

Tanya's voice was more rational as she spoke again: "Okay. I'll trust you - for now. This is Travis Carter and I'm Tanya Williams, since we already know your name. Mr. Konn. Now, tell us exactly what you're doing here and what you told those goons you encountered."

Konn replied, "Thank you, sir.. ah, Travis and Tanya. I'm ex-Bretonian military, and have been a freelancer since I finished my term, doing some missions on the side as well as doing trading runs. I got word that H-fuel was cheap in Kusari, and picked up a cargo-load at Akita. I've never been in Kusari before, so I decided to tour the system a bit.

"I stopped here, and as soon as I left this spaceport, having registered my ship with the spaceport authorities, I noticed I was followed by these three... I hesitate to call them gentlemen, and since goons is what I've been saying, I'll call them goons. I had managed to escape them by a daredevil run across a highway near that large shopping mall complex.

"As I mentioned before, we briefly met in the elevator, and then the goons caught up with me. They wanted my H-fuel as well as my ship, and had apparently known of it almost as soon as I landed. I thought quickly, and told them that I not only had H-fuel, but that I was taking on a cargo of Niobium from someone else - that is, you, Tanya - and that she was to meet with me later today to put the deal through. They agreed to let me keep my ship if I let them take the 'Niobium'.

"I'm just amazed I haven't run into those three goons again today. They must have really believed me when I said I was getting the Niobium."

Unfortunately, just as Konn spoke, harsh voices bellowed from the spaceport, and three men were standing near the Bretonian Crusader, clearly very unhappy that Hahukum Konn had given them the slip. Konn turned rather pale, and he turned back to Travis and Tanya and said, "I'm not armed. I left my weapon aboard ship. I'm going to need help, although I really shouldn't be asking you since I did, after all, get into this mess myself."

Tanya had other ideas, and they involved teaching three Kusari goons a lesson about stealing peoples' cargo.

Hahukum Konn

Thu Mar 24, 2005 1:50 pm by Hahukum Konn

Hahukum Konn paused to breathe a huge sigh and take stock as he powered up his ship. From freelance trader to wanted individual in about twenty seconds. This must be some kind of record , he thought. It's also not every day a woman your age can make you feel like you're ten years old again and being scolded for taking cookies without asking , he mentally added. He then rapidly went through the preflight check and confirmed that all was OK.

Luckily, both Konn and Tanya were able to get clearance to leave without anybody being the wiser, and zoomed off their landing pads as quickly as possible. Once in space above New Tokyo, he opened a secure channel to Tanya's Falcon and ransmitted the code-clearance sequence.

Secure channel confirmed and opened , his neural net prodded.

"Crusader calling Falcon. Do you read me five by five?"

"We read you five by five over here. What's the game plan?"

"I know only the relative location of Kyushu from here. You know the jump holes going from here to the Tau systems. I occasionally patrolled inside the Tau systems due to BMM's mining operations, so I know how to make our way through once we
leave Kusari space."

"Right. I'll upload waypoints that go as far as the entry to Tau-23 through Kyushu. After that, I'm leaving it up to you to get us safely into Tau-31. We have to go to Holman Outpost."

"Roger. Wilco." Before Konn could engage cruise flight, Tanya spoke again.

"I have a question. What are those things you wear in front of your eyes? I've been trying to remember and it isn't coming to me."

"They're my corrective lenses. The old term was 'eyeglasses'. My eyesight isn't that bad, though; they serve a covert function as well. The lenses, under the influence of a small electric field, will distort in a known fashion and can thus zoom in on whatever I look at. I had them on earlier to spy on the control booth at the spaceport. When I want to leave them at their normal setting, the electric field vanishes, and so does the distortion. The power usage is minimal, and the batteries are stored along the frames that go to my ears."

"Cool," Travis said.

Konn's neural net informed him about thirty seconds later that a series of waypoints had been uploaded, and he saw that the first ended in the middle of an asteroid field called the Kanto Field.

Tanya's voice broke in on his preparations to go to cruise speed. "By the way, you realize if you mess up or you're playing us for suckers, I'll have no problem destroying your ship and leaving your escape pod to the tender mercies of the Outcasts or the Blood Dragons, whoever gets to you first.

"I'm not completely incapable of finding my way around the Tau systems; it'll just take about three times as long and make me about ten times more frustrated searching for your miracle jump holes."

"Fully understood. Commencing to go to cruise velocity, now." At that, Konn engaged his cruise engines and set the autopilot to aim for the first waypoint.

Their luck held all the way through Kusari space, as they emerged in the Hayate Dust Field without incident, and flew galactic south and west in clear Kyushu space with little extraneous space traffic to notice them. They crossed high above the trade lane to the Tau 29 jump gate, and swiftly reached the next waypoint, the Tau 23 jump hole in the Ohka Dust Field.

Hahukum opened the secure channel again and said, "Crusader to Falcon. All right; from this point on I have navmaps of the Tau systems either from, as I said, direct patrol or from my own travels to and from Tau-37. Your neural net will be informed of the waypoints I upload in a few seconds."

"Roger. Standing by."

Konn quickly worked out from his nav maps which jump holes to use, feeding in a path that led from the Kyushu jump hole in Tau-23 to the Tau-29 jump hole, which would then put them into the Oxygen Field in Tau-29. Their final jump hole would be in the Samura Ice Asteroid Field, going to Tau-31.

He sent off the series of waypoints to Tanya, and shortly after, he heard her response.

"Confirmed, Crusader. This mess of waypoints is unbelievable. The good part is how we get to go to Tau-29 without needing a jump gate. Let's go."

"One thing. We'll be passing near the Cali Base when we go through this jump hole. I'm on the outs with the Outcasts since this is the same ship I had in the Bretonian Armed Forces, and they'll know it by the signature it transmits. I don't know about you two, though."

"We'll just have to run the risk, then, won't we?"

Hahukum could almost see an ironic smirk on Tanya's face in his mind's eye. With that, he initiated the dock sequence with the jump hole and delivered himself into the hands of fate. Everyone knew the Outcasts could be ruthless when they wanted to be, and he had no desire to replay the battles he'd fought once upon a time.

Tanya followed, and crossed her fingers...

Hahukum Konn

Thu Mar 24, 2005 3:25 pm by Hahukum Konn

Hahukum Konn didn't realize he'd been holding his breath until he saw that his contact list was empty, except for the Falcon. He took a brief moment to marvel at the contrast between the medium blues of Kusari and the dark blue, almost inky blackness of Tau-23, except for the sickly green sun off to the right in his viewport. That was a lucky break. I've lost track of how many times those sods would be gathered round a jump hole to get the drop on anyone coming through, he reflected.

Konn activated the secure channel, and spoke. "Crusader to Falcon. Please enter formation."

"Entering formation now."

He wasted no time after that in engaging cruise engines and autopilot for the next waypoint, the jump hole to Tau-29. Their ships moved swiftly through the asteroid field, with their thrusters aimed to "push" them slightly to their left to edge away from Cali Base.

Their luck held until the waypoint distance meter read "21 K", and three Outcast ships showed up in red on the contact list dead ahead of them.

Konn spoke: "Break formation, and we'll fight as soon as fired upon, so we know their range."

"Just stay alive, mister. We've been through a few of these ourselves."

Hahukum left his ship in cruise until the first blast hit his ship. At that point, he cut cruise mode, and aimed towards one of the Outcast ships heading towards him, while using his side thrusters to move slightly left or right to keep the other ship from getting a good aim. He kept the firing cursor aimed at the Outcast ship while blasting away with all six of his weapons. The blue shield gauge on his ship still dropped precipitously as the Outcast's own bolts occasionally landed, but he was rewarded with the the blue shield status on the opposing ship having dropped about 10 percent.

As soon as his ship passed the Outcast, he cut thrusters back on and zoomed after the other guy, both of them weaving and jinking through a cluster of asteroids trying to avoid each others' shots while getting off a direct hit of their own. When Konn's shields came back up to almost full, he decided to try and pour it on in a straight run at the other ship, a Stiletto, which he knew to be roughly comparable to his own in weapons loadout.

Konn saw the other ship just beginning to turn around for a run back at him, and decided to go for it. He put thrusters on full, and as he did so he held down the firing button, gripping the controls as he aimed steadily at the Outcast ship, pouring energy against the other's shields. He was rewarded when, just as his own shields were about to quit, the HUD showed the other's shields to be completely gone. He instantly hit the shields-restore button, and whipped back 'round to press home his attack.

Konn was able, with some dexterity, to inflict about 25% damage to the other ship's hull before he saw the blue shield-status come back on for the opposing ship. He cursed and his focus lifted for a second as he saw a fireball momentarily light up an asteroid just below him. The contact list still showed the Falcon, so he knew that Tanya and Travis had not been the unfortunate victim.

The answer to that very suddenly easy victory came when he got closer to the sun, having decided to get out of the arena of battle for a second to let his ship's shields restore, and all of a sudden the "Danger: Radiation Damage" voice came on.

"BUGGER!" yelled Konn as he hit nanobots, shield restore, and thrusters all at once. He realized what had happened. The Outcast who'd gotten blown up must have incautiously flown into the radiation zone, hoping to lead the Falcon into it, but then forgotten to activate his nanobots, leaving his hull and weapons in a permanently weakened condition.

He decided the best way to finish this off would be to double-team the Outcast fighting Tanya and Travis. He soared up from below, weapons firing at the Stiletto which was trying to fire at both of them at the same time. Konn cut engines again, sailed past the Outcast which jinked off behind an asteroid, Tanya following. He went over the asteroid, and took a couple of pot-shots at the Outcast as it cut thrusters back in and zoomed away before further damage could be inflicted on it.

He followed, as did Tanya, and both blasted away at the ship as its pilot tried a lunging run at them, apparently hoping to blow one or the other of them up before he was done in, but the Stiletto's shields failed too quickly under the combined onslaught, and when the distance meter read "100", a well-placed missile from the Falcon caused a brilliant pyrotechnic display as the ship disintegrated.

The final battle with the last Outcast ended much the same way, although not without a "shields failed" warning on Konn's ship a couple of times and some dicey maneuvering around the asteroids to avoid wasting too many shield-restoration batteries.

After the last Stiletto disintegrated, there was some discussion about the loot, and as the Crusader still had all the H-fuel in it, Tanya took the various cargos that had been dumped by the ships after their explosions, and Hahukum took whatever weapons were floating around. He wiped his brow, and resumed course for the waypoint to the Tau-29 jump hole.

Not bad for a rusty ex-BAF guy, Konn thought. This had been one of the longest battles he'd fought since he'd left the BAF. Freelancing, for him, amounted to getting the best value for the least amount of trouble, and so if the odds didn't favor him, he'd just dump half his cargo and watch the vultures start trading shots with each other for the right to get around 5000 credits' worth of free Niobium, or Diamonds. He had been pleased that his fighting instincts hadn't left him, though, and that his technique would probably still pass muster with his commanding officer.

The rest of the journey was almost anti-climactic, with the only nasty part being the Oxygen gas pockets in Tau-29 that they had to watch out for; Tanya and Travis had a bad scare when a gas explosion nearly took out all of their shields, but otherwise their luck held out all the way to Holman Outpost.

Hahukum initiated the dock sequence first, and said over the secure channel, "I'll see you guys at the bar, I guess?"

"You certainly will," chuckled Travis.
