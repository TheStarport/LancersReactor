Certainly for me Morrowind is what I play when I don't play FL. Of course with my server I get little chance to play either.

Wan's got the right of it about the admin of a server, the software needed and the sheer amount of cheats and cheaters out there.

As for the server setup itself it's been said around here many times. A couple of things though, the running of flserver (the executable that IS a FL server) requires only ONE INCOMING UDP port opened (the setup is sometimes called a tunnel or a virtual server) in your gateway (mistakenly called a router by the rest of the world, it does more than just route). This is the port other players (clients) are FLing in on from mostly the FL global server list. You do not need to open even a single INCOMING port to be on the FL global server. Of course if you are on the same 'puter or are in another 'puter on the same LAN (the Local Area Network on the home side of the gateway) all you will see is your server listed as a LAN server, always. If you get fancy you could firewall on just your gaming 'puter all OUTGOING ports to just your server's LAN address and THEN you would see your server as an Internet server because it went all the way out to the FL global server list and got the info on your server. If the info got to the list. If it is not on the FL global server list it most likely is a DirectX problem. Insure, by uninstalling/reinstalling if necessary, you have DirectX v9.0b installed correctly.

The assumption above is that with most gateways, their firewall portions are designed to firewall INCOMING packets and ports only.

As for players playing on a new server, it takes DAYS for players to notice and log on. It will take MONTHS to get a good, constant amount of players. Boston has been up a year and we have a good community.
