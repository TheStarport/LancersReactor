I had a problem with Win2k and mentioned it a little here. I have been reading around and found one of Win2k's service packs is bad for FL. As for the client side of FL I have had connection problems through my LAN. I get the global server no problem but my server which is on my home LAN I only see it from the global server! Not how I want to play as my Internet pipe is maxxed as it is with all the other players. I found the problem was my firewall, Zonealarm. Even after you "train" it after so much running FL the firewall server (a program seen in the task manager as vsmon.exe) starts really sucking up the CPU (ZA website cites vsmon.exe as suddenly checking every port continuously) and sound gets spotty. If I kill vsmon.exe ZA immediately prompts me to restart it. Once you do the CPU utilization is cool again.

Now what does this have to do with you? I think something in Win2k is firewalling your connection to the global server. Just to play FL you need over a dozen ports open. To connect to the global server itself I think its only a few but that may be enough to prevent your 'puter connecting. My suggestion? Surf around and ask which service pack to install on which FL client works. Or you can run the client computer and start killing services until FL client works. You'll probably spend so much time troubleshooting your Windoze machine that your HDDs will arrive before you find a solution! Good luck nonetheless.
