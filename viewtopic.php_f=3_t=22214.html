Comet

Thu Jul 31, 2003 10:50 am by Comet

Comparing a campaign game with an open ended space sim is very hard. Freelancer was and still is the TOP Campaing space sim. Its multiplayer component was also revolutionary. It was one of the first games to have a built in ladder system. WIth rankings, ensign commander. It kept track all the player statistics. Very configurable. You could put your clan logo in. And it showcased medals that you won. Unlike other multiplayer components in space sims, this one was reallly really complete. You could do skirmishs, as well as CAMPAIGN missions ONLINE. Freespace had an editor with it. And the briefings were done brilliantly.
Freelancer for its genre, the only big thing it brings really, is the "lively" universe. Quite enjoyable to see really. There is no place alike, it is also the first in its genre, to allow multiplayer in a open ended universe, with real-time space fights. To be fair we cant really compare the graphics...Freespace is waay old. And still it kicks ass.

So lets be fair. Freespace 2 was the BEST of its genre in its time. And it survived for quite some time. In fact it still survives. But Freelancer, really, is not a "finished" product. Wheres the capital ships fights, wheres the dynamic trading? To be fair, I loved Freelancer cause it is the first to allow me to play a open ended universe online, and because of its beautifull universe.

SO FOR ME Freespace has the lead, in its genre. Freelancer , well Freelancer is "almost" there, but not quite.
