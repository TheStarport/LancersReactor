Well it's been a long time since I last posted in these forums... tho I still have been reading them. Finished my last final earlier today, so now have some freetime again to get back into things.

Excelcia had the unfortunancy of dying out a couple months ago, this of course means I am no longer held by my obligation to keep the ships I make strictly for that mod, meaning I can put all of these out and open to anyone to use, no point in keeping them for a discontinued project.

Now this of course brings some other questions to mind,
1st: is anyone out there even interested in them anymore? They are pretty old models now. Don't really want to put the time into making a mod of them if no one want them

2nd: If I do take the time to put them out... what would the prefered format be? I don't think many strictly ship pack mods are really played, rather they tend to be taken and merged into larger mods. I could make a working mod but odds are it's just the ships people are going to extract out and put into their own. So I could just put out the cmp and mat files for the ships, and include a txt document which has a listing of all the hardpoints on each of the ships, which would faciliate putting them into another mod.

So which is prefered? A complete working mod which people can run and play sp with? Or just putting out the cmp, mat, sur, and a txt file of the hardpoints each ship has as just the essentials for other modders to put it into their mods?
