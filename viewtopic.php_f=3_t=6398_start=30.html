Now, to get this thread out of the "texture wars" and back on subject

I admit being irritated by the "we don't own this base, but we have an understanding with those that do" stuff over and over. It would have been nice to have a "welcome to Battleship (insert name here)" when you're on a battleship, for example.

But actually, the biggest gripe I have is the trading system. It would *really* be helpful to be able to see who produces what, no matter if you're on the ground, or don't have any of that particular commodity in your hold. I haven't been able to do enough cargo moving to check and see if there is actually a dynamic market system implemented, as intimated in the early previews. Dynamic markets, supply and demand, pirates trying to cut off vital supplies to a planet and smugglers trying to break blockades... all these would *tremendously* improve the logevity of the game.

stevedroid

Tue Feb 18, 2003 11:20 pm by stevedroid

They weren't taken by him, they were promo pics made by Digital Anvil. As far as I can tell from the beta there is no free camera with the exception of the turret cam. However, you'll never be able to get a picture that good with the turret cam because the interface will still be there, and all the guns will be at funky angles.

I do have some further comments: This is sort of in regard to my complaining about wanting trilinear filtering because of the mip-map lines. It seems the mip-mapping in this game is way, way too aggressive. I think that part of the problem of the game looking like it has really low res textures. It actually does seem to have some nice textures, but you get little more than 10 feet away from an object before its nice high res texture is mip-mapped into a blurry mess. I began to realize as I was playing the game only like 10% of the textures I saw were the actaul textures, the rest was all various degrees of mip-mapping. Anyway I checked my drivers settings for my GeForce card and I realized that my mip-map detail setting was set to "Highest-Performance" which of course produces the worst image quality. I changed it to "Best Image Quality" and now things are much better, but I still notice the mip-mapping is really agressive. I wish I could turn it off all together.

Hmm....I wonder if I add that NoMipMap flag under GeForce3 in the video card database file........
