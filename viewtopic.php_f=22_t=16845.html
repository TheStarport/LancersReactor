I have been looking into this myself. Even through we have a very nice server, 4 Xeon 700 Mhz Compaq server, the performance, at this time, is not has good as we had hoped. FL-Server does use mult-processing, but I have yet to get a baseline on how efficent it is. I suspect, and Browclops noted this to me, that two high speed processors are better than four mid speed processors. Therefore, I have been researching into a dual MP system.

The best bang for your buck seems to be going with dual AMD 2600+ MP processors. Processors and Motherboard (egghead) will run about 650, but YMMV. So, for around $1000-1100, depending on RAM, HD, Case, and P/S you use, you could have a nice FL server. This is assuming that you use a low-end video, inexpensive HD, since the server is hosting, not playing the game. Those spare parts you have lying around gathering dust may work fine for a bare bones FL server. HD speed is not much of a factor speed wise for FL-Server. For $200 more, you can use the new AMD 2800+ MP processors with the newer Barton core and a larger cache. Increase in performance may not be worth it, however. If, in the future FL dies or you get tired of it, buy a nice top of the line video, fast HD, and you have yourself a nice gaming computer too. The new Quake, BTW, makes use of multi-processors...
