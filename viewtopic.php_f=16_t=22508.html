Hi !
Intrigued by this story about Tau 37, i have been see by myself. And i discovered another thing. Not an object, just something about map.
Go in the direction of Freeport 10. So, you see the base in front of you, horizontally.
Stop your engines and look at in the direction of your feet. Not south, of north, or east, or west, but down.
You can see a background like a giant cloudy mass with a sort of spiral corridor inside.
There is no object to target. Then i have targeted Freeport 10 for have a reference objet
Then, i have fly in the direction of the center of the spiral. Without targeting point inside, is not easy to maintain the course but if you use your point of course (freeport 10) like a reference, the indicator of direction (purple rhombus) must be motionless on a side of the screen. So, try to maintain it stable.
I regulary have stop my engines for check my position on the nav map and i have realize that in spit of the big traversed distance, my position on the map was not the same, but near the previous.
And when i look at back, i can see the stars and the two clouds of Tau 37, but very far and litlle.
I have traverded 273K before to be out of limits of the map. So I have realize that i was in the deep of the map. Yes, this is a 3 dimensional game, dont forget it. Even if the nav map seem to be in 2D, the universe is in 3D.
Then i remembered the Primus/Gammu system, the strange corridor inside and the star very far but visible and targetable on the nav map.
In fact, when you fly towards this star, you fly deeper in the nav map. You really realize that you fly in 3D.

The far star in primus/gammu system is enough big for see it and for this reason you can target it. But if there are small objects (ships, or even a little planet or moon), deep in the system, you cant see these objects, you must approach , and then detect.

Maybe there is nothing to find far of the center of a system, and when i think that i was at 273k of distance of Freeport 10 in Tau 37 without be out of map limits, i have the giddiness.

To big for exploration !! Maybe with chance ??
