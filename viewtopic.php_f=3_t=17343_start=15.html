First Evil

Sat May 24, 2003 3:22 am by First Evil

Lurking device deactivated!

I replayed Tachyon recently (finally did the Galspan line), so I had the opportunities to compare it with Freelancer.

Space in Tachyon is realistically proportioned unlike FL. It's huge and the only way to get from place to place in a single system is via gates. On the other side, FL can hide things like wrecks while there's no way Tachyon can do that.

Space stations are HUGE in Tachyon, but you can't really dock with most of them! I found this frustrating LONG before FL was ever released. Replaying Tachyon now just makes that more evident.

Tachyon has that lovely Slide maneuver, but you have to point the whole friggin' ship to aim. FL gives you a wide frontal firing arc, but no slide. Argh!

Tachyon gives you a wider variety in missions but once you've used them up, they're gone. FL has a limited variety of missions (outside the campaign) but can go on forever.

No Trading in Tachyon. While it makes realistic sense, I wandered the whole Galspan campaign since New Vegas hauling around a crate of spare parts I couldn't get rid of.

FL doesn't put shields on capital ships or even gunboats, while Tachyon does. I've always wondered about this technical oversight.

FL fighters can repair themselves in mid-flight. Tachyon fighters...can't.

In general, I want a game that combines the best features of these two.


- the First Evil
