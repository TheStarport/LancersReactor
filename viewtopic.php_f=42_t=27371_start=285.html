@Warzog along with Tolwyn & Bob McDob, I can easily see your deep love of Privateer and I share your enthusiasm for it. I still play it to this very day and the first discussion I ever joined on Lancers Reactor was a "Freelancer vs. Privateer" thread. I am so enthralled by a game that was on the forefront of its time and is exceeded by none in its fun and enjoyment!

To recreate that is an admirable goal for which everone of us here at TLR will be grateful, me above all others. You are accomplishing something I cannot at this time. So my expectations of a truly great Privateer mod lies in your hands and I have the utmost respect for that.

I have added my support and what little help I can. But I also come from a wary position knowing two games are about to be combined into one. Freelancer is a huge, enormous game which its shear content looms over the tiny Gemini sector in many ways. I fear that a mere recreation of only the Gemini sector might make your mod feel a bit small in comparison to the massive Sirius Sector. As shown by the pictures that sp00n and Bob linked, there is a much larger universe than just the Gemini Sector. And they're all connected.

Replicate the Gemini Sector exactly, no alterations, no changes from Chris Roberts' first endeavor. I completely agree with that. But don't limit the mod to just that one sector, realize that you have the opportunity to expand on what Privateer was and take the Privateer/WC universe to a level Origin and Chris Roberts never could. In the Gemini sector there will not be Border World ships, but with this Freelancer game engine a player could go to the Border Worlds and there should be those ships.

None of the Privateer or WC games had the freedom to see everything in the creators minds nor where the other games took place. You have the tremendous opportunity to exceed the creators of your favourite game instead of just copying one portion of their creativity. All the pieces from all the games can fit together seamlessly into one package like never before possible. And the Gemini Sector can be as unique a Privateer experience from the Border Worlds, or Vega as Liberty space is unique from Bretonia or Kusari or Rheinland.

You don't have to complete it all at once, but know this: you have the possibility to accomplish something, not just great, but ... unparalelled to any other mod and certainly reaching a height that far exceeds the original Wing Commanders and Privateers in scope and breadth.

Lofty goals, I know, and you do spend your free time on this, I understand. I can see the frustration in some of your posts, what I have offered as advice was never meant to intrude on the first goal of recreating the Gemini Sector, but to add hope and persuasion that it can be so much more than that.

Thank you for your hard work. I know I'll be enjoying the fruits of your passion soon, along with many who visit this site.


Sir Spectre

@Warzog, I am speaking of thruster capabilities for the size of the systems. I could thruster across an average Gemini sector system in 2 minutes. It takes me 8 to 10 minutes (Cruise speed) in Freelancer (thruster 20-30 minutes). You could probably fit 4 Privateer systems into one Freelancer system, remove any non-story trade lanes and set up inter system jumpholes to recreate those Gemini systems that only had two jumpholes and an asteroid field (Pender's Star).

To recreate Pender's Star as it's own system would seem to be a waste I think. And the best part of Privateer was feeling like you're always on the frontier, hence no tradelanes. So you need the jumps closer to your routes and destinations, thus leaving lots of open space (in a Freelancer size system).

In that extra space fit in other systems, as long as the open space distances between Privateer systems within the 1 Freelancer system is really enormous, most people would want to take the intersystem jumphole.

But that is only my thoughts on a way to accomplish it. You're probably already locked into how you're doing it.
