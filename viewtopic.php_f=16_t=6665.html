Actually I don't think that's a bug, I think its a planned balancing feature.

I remember like just after playing mission 5, I tried galavanting around the Tau systems and had a similar experience. Everytime I went through a gate or a trade lane, I'd encounter 10 or so criminal ships and have my ass handed to me.

I was messing around in the Tau system because you can get missions there for 9,000+ when the missions in the Bretonia system that were actually suited to my level were around 4000-5000. The point was that if I could wander around Tau with ease, I would have met my next mission requirement in just one or two missions and probably not experience a lot of the game. Actually stations in Tau 29 and 31 wouldn't even offer missions because my level was too low, which was my first clue - besides being killed 20 times - that I shouldn't be messing around in the Tau systems yet.

Now that I've just about reached the last mission playable in the PR demo (I've been playing slowly) I decided to go back to the Tau system. I found that I encountered a lot less ships, or at least it seemed that way. I can distincly remember emerging from a gate or two and not having 10 attackers immediately upon me. I still have much more enemy encounters than in 'normal' areas, but that's to be expected because of the nature of the region.

So though I have no definitive proof, it seems that the game purposely ups the enemy encounter rate to keep you from messing in 'high-level' systems too soon as a balancing feature. Not realistic, but after all it is a game not a simulation.
