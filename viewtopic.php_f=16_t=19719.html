Radiation Fields Vs. Ships

Has anyone explored how Radiation Fields effect ships? I have three theories:

1. Only hull strength matters. Titan lasts longest.

2. Size matters. Within a class, armor matters (VHF Titan longer than VHF Eagle).
However, a 3600 hull Dragon lasts longer than a 3600 hull Dromedary, less surface area.

3. All ships decay at the same percentage rate, therefore last the same amount of time.
Only variation is in Nanobot supply.

Measurement:

A small field, say the one around the Flint wreck would be best repeatable test ground.
Two data numbers; first is time from radiation warning to hull breach warning.
Second data is total survival time expending full Nanobot supply.

Anyone done a similar test? Not gut feeling, a real test? Anyone want to give it a go?

Sherlog

Thu Jun 19, 2003 9:11 pm by Sherlog

I think hull armour is the crucial factor. The ship stats in the game data do not contain the surface area, and I don't think that the game uses the mesh data of the ship for this.

The armour of the Titan is more than ten times as strong as the armour of the Starflier and the difference is extremely noticeable if you visit the radioactive cloud with the Unknown Jumphole in Omicron Gamma, for example. The radiation eats through the armour of the Starflier at an alarming rate and the hull strength indicator goes down really fast. Same with the radiation in the minefield surrounding the experimental shipyard in mission 10 (Frankfurt?); for a class 6 ship the radiation damage is is hardly noticeable but a Starflier or Patriot won't make it through without thrusters or cruise engines (or nanos).

Radiation is very bad for expensive external equipment, though. You can run up really huge repair bills if you remain in hot zones for long, and it seems that nanos do not help in this regard. You could probably even lose external equipment like guns or shield (!) that way.

The shield may affect radiation damage also to a certain degree. ISTR that I had some nasty surprises in connection with jettisoning cargo in a strong radiation zone, but I am not sure that I remember the circumstances correctly.
