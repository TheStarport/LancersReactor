The SP game gives you a great oppurtunity when you first enter into Bretonia to get on good terms with all of the criminal factions. Your rep goes bad with the bounty hunters until you get another ship, you can use this to your advantage. Don't change ships immediately, you can go to some base and they will always be there to greet you when you come out. The Essex in Dublin is one of my favorite places to do this. When you come out of the Essex there will be 4-8 BH's every single time, kill them all, go back into the Essex and repair, cash in weapons dropped, etc. and go right back out. It won't take long before every criminal faction goes nuetral to friendly with you. When you like the state of your rep, go by a new ship and the BH's go back to nuetral. There also seems to be a bug in the game, sometimes the BH's will go nuetral after you get a new ship and then revert to hostile again for no apparent reason. Their hostility doesn't seem to last if you take another story mission though.

Thu Jul 24, 2003 12:18 am by Sherlog

The Xenos will stop shooting pretty quickly if you kill criminals, preferrably not while in the employ of house law (police, armed forces) or the Bounty Hunters. They hate some corporations as much as criminals, law and Bounty Hunters so avoid companies that give you missions against Xenos. If you fly missions for other companies or factions then the positive effect of killing criminals will override the small negative effect of working in the employ of somebody the Xenos do not like.

The Mollys are well-connected criminals and they hate mostly Bretonian law and Bretonian companies. They are friendly with most local criminals of other houses and neutral with most of the rest but they dislike Outcasts, Corsairs and Xenos. For some reason they are friendly with the IMG so you could get the Mollys non-hostile by shooting Outcasts for the IMG. Of course, this is if you are role-playing a lawful character. Otherwise the quickest way is to blow up a couple of depots of Bretonian law or Bounty Hunters.

The Gaians are the Bretonian house kooks; their connections are a bit complicated and they hate some corporations much more than Bretonian law. How to get the Gaians to stop shooting may involve campaign spoilers and so I won't tell here, but shooting plenty of Liberty Rogues, Xenos or Red Hessians should get you there in any case.
