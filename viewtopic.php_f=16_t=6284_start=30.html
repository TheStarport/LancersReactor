Switchblade

Tue Jan 13, 2004 5:43 am by Switchblade

actually, the aspect of the game that has always most dissatisfied me is the way that any given ship is limited by which faction it belongs to.

for example, I absolutely LOVE the way a Kusari Dragon looks, but the damn things just can't go toe to toe with the bigger, meaner ships, as it just doesn't have the hull, shielding and firepower to compete.

to my mind, this is just wrong, especially considering the way that the storyline details quite a few examples of Liberty ships slapping around Rheinland ships, when the liberty ships are actually far inferior. The same goes for the scenario of a starflier and a patriot taking down a squadron of Order VHF's.

I think that it would be much better to make which ship you choose to fly a matter of aesthetics, rather than necessity. This would be done by allowing the player to purchase performance modules at planets and bases, such as thicker hulls, better power systems, more advanced weapon mounts.

so, you could buy new engines, which would improve the handling of your ship, making it more agile, but presenting you with additional power drain problems to cope with.

New hulls would improve your armour rating, but would drag down your agility.

new reactors provide you with more power for the guns, but cost a lot.

weapon/turret and shield mounts of higher levels could also be purchased, potentially leading to the scenario of a patriot with level ten guns, but the higher level weapons present a geater drain on the reactor.

expanded cargo holds improve the amount of cargo you can tote about (by 25%, say) but degrade your armour

and so on. if you ask me, that would be a better system.

The maneuverability rating of a ship often makes it superior, regardless of what class it is. Sure, in a head-to-head slugathon an LF will lose to a VHF, but you can't kill what you can't hit, and there are some damn fine LF pilots out there. Don't make the mistake of judging a ship based on what weapon/shield class it holds.
