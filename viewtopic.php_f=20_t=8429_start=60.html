Regarding the System32 folder:
Since %WINDIR%\System is in the %PATH% variable on both OSs and %WINDIR%\System32 is in %PATH% for Win2K, it should find any .dll files without a problem. I'm betting that it's a MFC function (C++ library/dll) that is causing the problem. Make sure that your MFC ("Microsoft Foundation Class" if I remember right) libraries are up to date (try searching the MS site or Google for them).

Hello all =)

I figured i would let you know about my Microsoft Customer Support result since I seem to have gotten help. I know it sounds wierd that MS would actually help someone with this issue, but they did. The support rep name was Yogi, and while he did take a week to help me he did send me a MP ID to use. By then a friend had generated an ID for me so i could play on his server, but they did help me. So I suggest you do what i did.

What did I do?

- Go to the Freelancer website.
- Click on the Support link in the top right hand corner.
- On this page you should have the option in the lower left if I recall to Contact a Customer Support Rep. Make sure to have your Product ID handy. You can get your Product ID by putting the Freelancer CD in you computer and the screen that pops up after it Autoloads should have the Product ID in the upper left hand corner.
- Now I used what is called Microsoft Online assisted support. You will need to setup a .NET Passport if you do not already have one. You will also need to enter you Product ID or MS will want a credit card for the $40 fee that they charge if you do not have a Product ID.
- Once you are in the system you will have to give them a basic description of what you issue is. I choose Other as the problem type, marked that it happened every time, and explained that Freelancer was not making a MP ID so I could play online. Now I suggest you mention your OS is Win 98 or ME (what ever you have) and tell them that the MP ID Utility in the Start Menu says that <NO MP ID WAS FOUND>. I did not and had to tell them in a second posting which made my wait for help longer.
- Once your info is entered you have to wait until a Customer Support Rep reads and responds to it. They e-mail you that a response is availible when ever a new one is added. It look alot like a forum when you open all the correspodence.

I told Yogi about this forum and linked this thread for him to read. I also summurized some of the info for him in my second post incase he did not read this thread. This thread had great info and allowed me to explain exactly what seemed to be causing the issue. That limited MS to having to answer to my exact problem and I feel is why they did not send a canned response twice.

Thanks to all the posted here. If they will not help us, then we will help ourselves.

Hope my info can assist you all =)

JGP
