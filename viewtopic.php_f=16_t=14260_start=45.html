Gung_Ho

Sun Jul 27, 2003 4:23 am by Gung_Ho

Heat does not travel through space. EMR does, and the heat we 'recieve' from the sun is a conversion of heat energy to light energy (EMR) and back to heat again when it strikes our skin.

This is an important thing to remember. Heat would not 'bleed' through gaps in a sphere, like it might do on earth (born by the gaseous mix we know as Air). The light would eventually find it's way to the gaps, but not before it had touched down and reflected many many times at various points on the interior of the sphere(creating thermal energy). Putting gaps in will limit the sum total of energy contained within the sphere to a specific level. However, that level is bound to be very high when using only small gaps. The larger the gaps, the lower the level is going to be when equilibrium is reached. Also, the more reflective the surface of the sphere is, the lower that level will be (When speaking of a gapped sphere). In order to make that level hospitable to human life you'd need some pretty titanic gaps and/or a very shielded (and reflective) surface. If the surface was the same as the earth's, then the gaps would have to much larger than the actual surface area of the sphere itself. Remember that 2X the light energy (1x from direct light, and even 1x from reflected light) would cook the earth in VERY short order.
