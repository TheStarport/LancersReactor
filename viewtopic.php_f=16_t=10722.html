the FL cynic''s voice (1)

First, let me say that I love FL and already spent more thasn 50 hours playing it, with maybe another 50h coming up. Still there are some things in this game that plainly _hurt_ the rational mind.

Take those tradelanes, for example. Hmmm, so they connect bases, jumpgates and planets. Now, while this isn't much of a problem for bases and jumpgates, there's one concerning the planets.

You know... planets have a nasty habit.
They MOVE.
Around their sun, mostly. One full circle each 'planet' year, just like earth itself. In Summer, earth will be at one end of the solar system, in winter at the directly opposite end. But... well:

Scenario 1) "tradelanes are immobile"
Hm, so that means the planet will move away from the ending point of the tradelane corridors during a planet year. In the worst case, you'd come out of a tradelane just to notice that the planet you were looking for is right on the other side of the system because the exit is set to its "winter" position and they currently have summer on it... bad.

Scenario 2) "tradelanes move with the planets"
Ok, lets say the tradelanes are drawn along with the planet, "following" it around the system. Oops, nope - bad idea. Since no matter from which point of the system the tradelane starts, if it ENDS at some planet, then it will cross the sun's path sooner or later as the planet moves around. Simple, unavoidable geometry...

Hmmm, so it's either "coming out in the boonies" or "stellar toast". Oh yes, I think Sirius is in _desperate_ need of those Nomad Hypergates. Otherwise, half of its economy would crumble and die in half a year from now, because most planetary tradelanes would end in the middle of nowhere then.

(mind, this is not a rant or something - just a slavishly loyal cynic's voice. )
