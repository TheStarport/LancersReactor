Actually the ship already sailed. Freelancer fell off the top ten list fast. Considering the five year development time for Freelancer I seriously doubt MS will throw good money after bad and bankroll an add-on or a sequel. A game really needs a huge following to warrant that kind of financial risk. Look around the Freelancer community and what do you find? One major fan site, this one. Now look at a blockbuster, any one of them, and you will see tens of fan sites just as busy as the next.
Remember, the only reviews the money men care about are the print reviews. Internet reviews have no credibility, and therefore no weight. The exception to this rule might be the larger corporate game sites, but even those don’t carry the same journalistic credibility as a magazine. Internet reviews are meaningless. This is a fact of life, not opinion. The reviews overall have been good, but not great. Great reviews would have been helpful in convincing the brass at MS to put up the cash for an expansion or sequel.

Painman

Tue Jun 03, 2003 8:29 pm by Painman

I see where he's coming from and I have to agree... here's this huge, rich environment to play in, but in the end all there is to do is ferry stuff from one place to another and blow people up. That wouldn't be so bad if the random missions gave some interesting backdrop to it, but they don't. They stink. Fly to the nav point and blow something up, maybe seize/destroy some cargo. That's it. Privateer 10 years ago had better missions than this. Cargo runs during which you were guaranteed to be attacked. Multi nav-point patrols. Multi-system bounty hunts that had AI bounty hunters competing with you. Priv 2 had random SOS calls and such while you were out flying from place to place.

Now that I've gone everywhere and got everything, I feel like there's only 3/4 of a game here and it's mostly because of the boring missions. Plus other things that IMO are obviously unfinished (can't mention them here, they may be spoilers). I just don't think the game was quite ready to ship when it did, and it's a damn shame because it was close to 6 years in the making. A little bit more time to properly polish and flesh things out wouldn't have been so horrible, but I guess the suits at M$ studios couldn't sit tight, and had to give the "ship it or die" order. Blah.

Maybe an expansion pack will give me what I was hoping for... for another $40 of course. Rebalance mod might keep me going for a while, but I'm not sure if I'll be sticking with this game for a long time as I hoped I would be.


Ever get the feeling... you're standing on plastic?
