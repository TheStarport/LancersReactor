Just off the top of my head, on my first play-through, I noticed:

Trade-lanes are strongly reminiscent of Cowboy Bebop's hyperspace lanes.
The Nomads themselves (when they manifest in a host, and/or take a new one) look a fair bit like Stargate's Goa'uld.
Orillion might as well have a "NOT MORPHEUS. REALLY." sign around his neck.

And while it's not actually in the game, I really wanted Trent to pause on the landing pad on his first visit to the Texas system and observe, unimpressed, "So this is planet Houston."

Just a few examples of how Freelancer is a fun, but very silly and rather derivative game - just like its predecessor's refighting of the Cold War in space. (It's my theory that Chris Roberts wants to be George Lucas, in the worst way. And judging from the Wing Commander movie, he got his wish.)
