You still have not adequately answered the biggest question arrising from you suggestion. Who is to decide what server stays, and what server goes away? and who is it that can FORCE those who do not want to close ther servers to do so.

Your idea is heartfelt, but trying to get people to close the smaller servers is only going to bring a more swift end to the Freelancer community. I for one know my server is small, and inadequate for a large number. But I get satisfaction out of having it, and keeping it alive at all. If I was "Forced" to close my server I might be insulted enough to leave Freelancer all together. Im sure I am not the only one who feels this way. (Which of course means you arent going to get us to close our servrs in the first place).

Back off your point for a second and look at it from my side of things. Assume you've poured time into your server, youve gotten tied to it emotionally, you love to do it.. But your numbers are down because FL numbers are down, are you seriously going to close it just because some bloke on TLR says it is best for the FL comunity to do so?? I doubt it, and even if you did, you would lose a part of what made you enjoy FL. Some would get bitter that their server had to go while others didnt, and quit playing all together. In the end, it would hurt the community not help.

Part of what has kept FL alive this long is the fact that there are so many choices, if I dont like the options, or the playing style on a server I can always go to another. If we did as you suggest those options would go away. The casual players who go to be part of a community would dry up as the thugs who get thrills out of blasting starfliers to bits with a decked out eagle would run rampant, ruining the experience for those of us who dont like certain servers that allow this behavior to go on. (Yes, I have left servers because of this.. MANY of them, its actually what prompted me to start my server in the first place)
