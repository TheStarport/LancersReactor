Tue Apr 25, 2006 12:47 pm by vorticaldwarf

Part of the problem is that players will tend to exploit every little loop-hole to get ahead. Trying to solve these problems once the game has already started is a huge headache, so I have tried to make these rules cover as many concievable issues as possible. The result of that is a very large set of rules that are confusing for those new to the game. In fact, thats the primary porpose of the more restrictive advancement system. Players will have time to get their feet wet with small simple factions before diving into the logistics nighmare of running a house government single handed. By winning awards, a player can prove that they have mastered a faction and are ready to move on.


The good news is that I'm almost done revising the rules. Once I finish we can work on a less initimdating set of basic rules to give players a basic idea of how to play.
