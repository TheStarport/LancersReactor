It's probably the OutCasts. They're allies of the Lane Hackers and you won't be able to dock with Mactan if they're hostile to you.
Once you hit level 10 you can start ignoring the calls to take story missions and do some exploring up in the northern border worlds. Stop at every freeport and eventually you'll find somebody to fix you up with the Outcasts. (also makes travel in the northern systems much easier) This will also raise your rep with the Hackers and lower it with police and military.
Once you're OK with the Hackers and the Outcasts you should be able to dock at Mactan and buy your ship.
That's a great ship. It got me through most of the single player game and went to most of the systems in the game. Hated when I had to trade down to a fighter.


If you want to make lots of cash while you're exploring, detour into Kusari space and pick up a Drone freighter. When you find a big money run the 175 cargo will really pay off.

Wasabe

Wed Jul 09, 2003 6:07 pm by Wasabe

Actually, I think his problem is not with the Hackers - he said he was neutral with them. The problem is that the Hackers are allied with the Outcasts, and so if you are within 5K of Mactan base and there are any Outcast ships around, then because the Hackers are allied with the Outcasts, they will turn hostile and fire on you. So the trick is to get to Mactan without any outcast ships around, then it will not be hostile and allow you to dock. You can kill all the outcast ships before heading to Mactan. If you enter Magellen system through the New York gate, you will have problems as most of the time the Outcasts will show up. However, if you enter through the Manchester gate, the outcasts usually don't show up and you can make it all the way to Mactan Base and it won't be hostile.
So just make sure there aren't any outcast ships within 5K of Mactan base and you'll be ok.
