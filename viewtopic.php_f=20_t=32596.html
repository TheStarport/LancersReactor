This is a shot in the dark...... Does your machine run normally after this problem happens? Do you have the DIVX Codec installed? The reason why I ask these two things is that it sounds like one of a number of things could be happening. Here they are from best to worst.
1. System overheating. If your machine runs fine after the problem happens, then that is not it.
2. Conflict with the DIVX codecs. These CODECS used in some internet video can cause conflicts with DirectX on some machines.
3. Check your AGP Arperture settings in your bios. It should be set for at least 128mb.
4. Is your system overclocked? If it is, step it down a couple of notches and try again.

kingrat

Mon Jul 05, 2004 3:49 pm by kingrat

How much RAM do you have in your system? You see, the AGP Arperature setting in your bios could be causing the problem. What this setting does is reserve an amount of your systems RAM to help your video card operate more efficiently when it is being taxed by a graphic intense program. So if you have your AGP setting at 256mb and you only have 256mb RAM for your system, the program will terminate because all of your system RAM is allocated to help your graphics card, and there is nothing there to run the program with.

Could you give me your system specs. It will help in determining a solution to the problem.
