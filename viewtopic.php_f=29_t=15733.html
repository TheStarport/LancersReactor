[Engine
nickname = ge_gf6_engine_01
ids_name = 263676
ids_info = 264676

linear_drag = 599 ;<= this is where the 600 above come from, handle with care
power_usage = 0 ;<= power it uses from your powerplant
reverse_fraction = 0.200000 ;<= how fast you go backwards with "X"
flame_effect = gf_li_smallengine01_fire

Tue Jun 03, 2003 8:00 am by Victor

You can do even better...

max_force = 48000

This is the engine force, it does NOT merely determine the max speed, it is also used to determing how the ship turns, accelerates and stops. Increasing this will make your ship move faster, turn faster, start quicker and stop in a shorter distance but it will not make your thruster produce more of an increase, in other words your stock thruster will give you 120 over whatever your new max speed is.

linear_drag = 599

This is the fun number to play with it not only affects your normal flying speed but it also changes the amount of speed gain you get from the thruster.

So, rather than using: desired speed * 600 = force
Try using: force / desired speed = drag (subtract 1 from the final drag if you actually want to reach the exact desired speed)

In my mod light fighters go faster, 120 m/s, and their afterburners are more effective (just over 300 m/s), on the opposite end of the spectrum freighters still go 80 m/s but the afterburners get them up to a mere 160 rather than 200. All possible even though they use the same thrusters.
