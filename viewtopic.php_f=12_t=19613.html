Chandrasekhar Limit

Wed Jun 18, 2003 8:04 am by Chandrasekhar Limit

Did you:

1) Try reverting to the original hardware config (if you added any new pieces of hardware that might have screwed things up)?

2) Checked your BIOS settings to make sure there were no changes that might screw up things like vid card settings or memory timings?

3) Try uninstalling then reinstalling the game?

4) Try reverting to an earlier version of DirectX?

5) Try reverting to an earlier version of your vid card driver?

6) Make sure things on your HD are not going corrupt by running Scandisk?

7) Consider that it might be time to "clean house" and reinstall your OS to get rid of all the crud that has accumulated in the registry and in the Windows directory?

Hope these hints give you some new options to explore.
