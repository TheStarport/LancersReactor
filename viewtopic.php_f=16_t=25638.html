The Junkers do have Xeno antipathy, but it's only a -0.1 multiplier (most antipathy relationships in the game are in the -0.25 to -0.35 range.) Compounding this is the fact that the Xenos themselves don't give much of a reputation change for killing one of them (only 0.08 box while most factions are around 0.30). Multiply those numbers together, and it takes a whopping 119 Xeno kills to improve your Junker reputation by one box. Compare that to 33 Liberty Police kills or about 20 Hogosha kills.

Of course, flying missions for the Junkers themselves against anyone is an easy way to get your reputation up from zero to friendly; you get about 0.65 box per mission plus antipathy from the targets you're killing. Once you get your rep up to neutral to accept missions, that's great, but other measures are necessary to get your reputation up from negative to around zero.

Tue Oct 28, 2003 11:28 pm by T-hawk

Frobozz, you are correct, but those numbers don't tell the whole story.

When you kill a Xeno, your *Xeno* reputation changes only a small amount, much less than killing a Liberty Police ship causes your Police rep to change. These numbers are the "Destroy object" values in empathy.ini. These numbers are multiplied by the cross-faction empathy multipliers. Net result: even though the Junkers have the same antipathy ratio (-0.1) for each faction, the absolute numbers are much less for the Xenos.

IOW, the Xenos value the lives of their own much less than the Police value the lives of _their_ own. The Junkers know this, and thus weight your Xeno actions less than the Police actions. You can definitely still kill Xenos to get in good with the Junkers, but it'll take four times as many Xeno kills as police kills, because the police value their own lives four times more than the Xenos do.

So the best way to get in good with the Junkers is most certainly to kill Hogosha, as they have a fairly high (-0.2) negative multiplier with the Xenos, and they don't place such low value on their own lives.

A more profitable route is to become friendly with other pirates with whom the Junkers are affiliated, and then fly missions for them out of Rogue or Junker bases.

E.G.: Killing Bounty Hunters is the most rep. friendly way (in terms of collateral rep. damage) to gain confidence with the Outcasts and Liberty Rogues. Once you are on good terms with them, you can will be able to fly missions for them out of Rochester or Buffalo. Many of these missions will involve the destruction of additional Bounty Hunter ships, and hence you will get rep. points with the Junkers for both the individual ships destroyed and for the missions completed for their pirate allies (making money the whole while!!).
