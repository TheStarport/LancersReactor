Megistos

Thu Oct 07, 2004 11:11 am by Megistos

I thought about this a bit the other day. I'm afraid I don't track this thread, so apologies for any duplications.

Jump Gates/Holes should be reduced to ship-based, but you can only use them around the edges of the systems and not in the middle. You could invent some sci-fi tat about gravity wells or such like to justify this. Now, you would be able to interrupt your jump whenever you wanted and just stop in the middle of space. Coupled with the ability to build your own bases, I can envisage that ability giving rise to a whole load of secret bases, that you'd only really be able to get to with either knowledge or extreme luck.

In online play, defined areas where PvP is allowed (if it's not allowed as a rule on the server). Killing players in this area can net you serious rewards, but if you get killed you lose everything and start over with a Starflier (think Runescape - wilderness).

Another lift from Runescape - the ability to arrange a duel. You'd get control over a flexible setup. For instance, you might have a Humpback duel with all Codenames. You could duel for money, equipment, or just for fun.

Tracking devices. Say someone shoots you up good. You're going to want revenge, aren't you? Get a tracking device on him before you die and he'll appear on your Nav Map and on your Important sensor display (if you're in the same system) until you catch up with him and kill him.

The ability to command AI ships to a limited extent. For example, say, you would order a ship to join formation with you, or come to you.

That's all for now.

EDIT: Ships should be more specialised. For example, pirate ships would have good weapons, cargo space and speed, but not suited to long battles. Bounty Hunter ships would have good speed, weapons and shields, suited to dogfighting. Civilian fighters would have excellent shielding and speed, but not much by way of weaponry
