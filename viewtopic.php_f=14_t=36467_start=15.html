First off... Pinger's remarks aside... this is not the "bash BP" thread. I came into this to argue for a change of policy which would help BP & E and TLR in general, not hurt them.

...and for the record, I don't mind if you vent, or if it's directed my way. I don't take any of this very personally. I wasn't out to hurt BP's feelings in the first place. I can't un-say what I've said, and quite frankly, I don't think that what I've said is inaccurate or unfair- and I didn't mention BP's name once- not even ONCE... in a way that suggested that he's not a hard-working person who's put his heart and soul into things.

But, as I pointed out, at great length... I think BP should do a lot less work , not more. And the website should be structured to make that possible.

And if he needs help to figure out how to do that... I was hoping that my suggested changes would get the ball rolling. I'm not here to re-fight the decision to show DS news... those of us who disagreed lost that battle (in most cases, before we were even aware that it was occuring- I was totally ignorant at the time). So, as that decision has been made, we should all live with those consequences, whether we like them or not. So... please... let's not get sidetracked by what happened in the past. Let's think about the future.

My first post was pointing out Reality, which is that the News is poorly-structured and wastes bandwidth like there aren't consequences. Which I think is beyond argument.

As our multiple attempts to measure the full bandwidth used have all shown, it's about 4 MB of data for today's News alone. For a FRONT END.

I get the News plenty fast, just like most other cable/T1 users- but not everybody's on cable. This isn't about whether or not I'd like to shave a second or two off of my view of the News. It's about DSL users, Dialup users... and bandwidth costs. Which even M$ has to worry about.

It's not "modern" to waste bandwidth like the front end currently does. It's bad policy.

You don't see Yahoo throwing out megabytes... or Google... or counter-strike.net (whose MB makes TLR's look teeny-tiny, in terms of traffic and userbase), or any of the Planet 'sites, or... do I need to go on? So we're talking about facts here, not opinions- good design principles remain good design principles. And wasting bandwidth is always bad design. It's not "modern" to load up 500KB of Flash to do the same job as a 5KB GIF file... it's stupid.

So at some point or another, the front-end needs to be recoded, and I think that the News editorial policies should be changed. I said that with full knowledge of what that means- I have read the other posts describing Bargib's somewhat organic coding techniques, and how the current 'site maintainers have had to wade through lots of code that they don't understand, just to make small changes. So it's probably time to start from scratch. If the current DB can be ported, great... if not, too bad. If the front end can be recoded in situ, then great... if not, too bad.

But for now... the answer is pretty simple. Screenies and other visuals should be restricted to the Download pages of the mods. BP should not quote the entirity of a mod's Blurb when they submit News- just a synopsis. The rest of it should be relegated to the Download page for the mod in question. And older versions of a Mod should be taken down when newer versions are put up... thus keeping the DB clean. It's just that simple. This set of reforms would cut bandwidth wastage to maybe a hundredth of what's going on now, be easier for BP to edit, and would keep the Download DB cleaner.... and this is controversial? Sigh.

I have never, ever said that I think that taking care of this would be easy, and I offered to help in the way that I'm qualified best to do so- as a glorified librarian, removing old junk from the download DB so that we only have the latest versions of X, Y and Z mods, among other things. My offer is, and will remain, open- I will happily take time away from my life and my mod to help out TLR. Because it needs doing. Because I care about the health of the 'site. I'm here a lot, too- and I help out where I can, and write tutorials, and distribute my mod. I'm not just a whiny leech who doesn't do anything... I've been trying to help the latest crop of newbies get to the point where they'll make mods... which in turn will help attract people to this 'site. So I'm not trying to hurt anybody here... just pointing out the facts, and offering my opinions (backed by clear examples) about what could be done.
