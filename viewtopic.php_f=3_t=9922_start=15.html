I guess my biggest problem is with any game, i try to see all there is to see first time through. For some of my favorite games like Deus Ex or Divine Divinity, it takes some of the replayablility out of it unless I wait a few months to play it through again. Other games like Arcanum or Fallout offer you choices and paths that branch all the time, so you can play the game twice, go to the same towns but talk to different people for different reasons or go to completely new locations.

I stopped once with the single player mode for FL when I got ramped into a series of missions in a freighter with no cash that just couldn't take the 20 rheinland cruisers beating it was getting. I stopped around rank 10 and mission 6 and I restarted. So far it's a good bit more smooth. I haven't tried over exploring at all, I've just been flying around, taking mission and working on faction even though I know it's all about to go to hell anyhow. The game can definately last longer if you want to and don't rush right through the main storyline. These games have branching quests for a reason, they have extra stuff for a reason.

Hell, every other rank the game even tells you to go off and do your own thing, forget about the main storyline for a while.

Closing the spoilers forum is a bad idea, just don't read it. Some people are just better at games than others or have enjoyment out of using spoilers as opposed to us perhaps.
