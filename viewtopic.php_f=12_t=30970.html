it's a good trilogy, I enjoyed it, dam sight more bite in it than that Harry Botter rubbish. Wife and daughter adore Pullman's work, but I wouldn't go that far, he's not that good. Frankly, it rambles on a bit and I think isn't too sure about it's audience at times, is it for kids or not? (no)

Pullman said one of his primary motivations in writing was NOT to produce an escapist fantasy, ironically the "fantasy" genre fans have taken his work to their hearts. When someone compared him to C S lewis, he was quite upset; he thinks that work like Lewis' is deceptive and cheating with it's magic and world shifting. citing the magic apple from Narnia that cured Digory's dying mother in The Magicians Nephew, Pullman said using such plot devices as miraculous solutions to tragedy was a cruel and foolish idea to put into a child's head. Possibly a bit harsh but I see where he's coming from.
