Argh

Wed Nov 24, 2004 1:33 am by Argh

Take it from me (and lord knows, I've been doing weird things to Systems for my mod, if you're not familiar with it)... back your work up constantly. Make copies of your System's INI file before any major edits, and save them somewhere. Test things one part at a time... don't open up FLE and do 50 things at once... you'll be totally hosed, believe me!

And get super-familiar wth hand-editing systems- FLE is OK when it works (and the next version, which is in Beta, will be better still), but it's not 100% reliable, and it won't do everything. So be careful about editing, and get your hands dirty... and when something crashes... if you're not *sure* what you edited that might've caused the crash... go to backup...

warzog

Tue Nov 30, 2004 5:23 am by warzog

General Leo:
Besides the "pop thing" around the hole...
There's probably Patrol's leading to/from the hole...
(Which use the hole to leave the system through.)
Probably an exclusion zone that's affecting an asteroid field or nebula.
(Which will be in the Solar/Asteroids or solar/Nebula folders.)
Not to mention a zone that connects to a base or tradelane.
(pop_type = Faction, Wormhole, single_base_Unlaw)
(pop_type = major_tradelane)
Or connects to another hole, or even, a jumpgate.
(pop_type = wormhole)

I just did a quick scan of the file...
My guess would be an exclusion zone.
(The system's full of 'em!)
Check these files:
file = solar\asteroids\Li03_Silverton_rock_asteroid_field.ini
file = solar\asteroids\Li03_Copperton_rock_asteroid_field.ini
file = solar\asteroids\Li03_Alamosa_rock_asteroid_field.ini
file = solar\asteroids\Li03_Cheyenne_rock_asteroid_field.ini

Make sure that any "pop thingy" that you changed is changed in the appropriate asteroid field ini file, too.
Look particularly at any exclusions zones in those files.
If you've deleted/renamed a zone, delete/change it in the appropriate ini, too.

Watch your 6!
