Ether Dragon

Mon Jun 02, 2003 4:47 pm by Ether Dragon

Engine kill is extremely useful, and I recommend it highly. I recommend spending some time practicing using it outside of combat until you get comfortable with the difference in flight mechanics. In particular, you have to break any habit of using your normal engine power.

The pay off is increased speed in combat (or retreating from it,) some handy dogfighting applications, and even just to get from one trade lane to the next faster. I use my normal engines more for braking then anything else anymore. The only thing I can really tell you is to play with it. I don't fly anymore, I drift everywhere.

Tempest8008

Tue Jun 03, 2003 5:57 pm by Tempest8008

Engine kill's usefullness canNOT be overstated.

If you ever take a "kill a station" mission, it is so useful it makes it almost too easy.

First, boost so you're going to just miss the base.
Kill your engines and strafe fire all along the side of the base as you pass.
You'll be moving at about 170 when you're done, you've avoided everyone shooting at you, and you're past the base.
Then just line up and do it again.
Two or three passes like this, even in a basic Starfiler with 3 Lavablades, and the base is toast.

Like the others' said too, you can stay at 199 speed pretty much all the time with your engines killed, and just boosting occasionally.

You can even drop from cruise to engine kill, and start at 300 and slowly drop, and all that while you can turn and fire, and drop mines, and shoot missiles, etc etc.

It's the one piece of physics in this game that make it playable for me.
