Fulcrum

Wed May 28, 2003 8:20 am by Fulcrum

What I meant is that many former Freelancer players have set up shop inside EvE and have built and named corporations and companies in honor of Freelancer.

Having said that, I don't consider Freelancer as being able to do a "lot" in a ship. It's a mouse driven point and clicker third person shooter, once you're into combat, and it feels very dumb down for a person who also came from playing Independence War 2: Edge of Chaos, where the ships are joystick controlled and literally governed by inertia (try doing the 180 degree loop manuever, literally flying backwards shooting at a pursuing opponent.)

but FL is fun, much more expansive, and lacks EoC's innane mission design, although I much favor EOC's flight and combat system as still the best ever I've seen in a space game. EOC's universe, however like Tachyon the Fringe, does not extend beyond the SP campaign or to multiplayer, and like TTF, only offers deathmatches and team arenas. That's where FL rules.

But after thoroughly exploring just every system, created level 38 characters multiple times, and tried every ship in FL, I do think it's too easy and offers little now except variations with mods. I'm certainly not alone: inside EvE, I often meet and chat with other players that had previously owned and played Freelancer. Not only do they honor FL within EvE by naming corporations after FL factions, but also even use FL names like Trent or Juni.

Don't take it wrong: Freelancer is a deep game. But I think I've drank every last drop of it. The people whose appetites that were wetted with Freelancer now wanted more than what FL can offer. They want more universe interaction. They want a dynamic economy. They want to set up corporations as members and a space base of your own where you can call an HQ. They want to explore literally hundreds of star systems, and I do mean one heck of a lot. They want to build factories that build and sell ships. They want to fly big transports, cruisers and battleships and engage in massive fleet combat. They want to fly into huge gigantic space bases. They want a customizable character who is not a clone of Trent. They want to be in the same universe, where thousands of other players are simultaneously playing, when most of the time you meet another ship, it's really another live player, not an NPC. If you're ready for all of that, welcome to EvE.
