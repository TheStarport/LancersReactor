I've tested this business of firing one shot of all your weapons and one weapon on the wrecks and I've drawn the following conclusion>
It doesn't matter if you fire one gun/ten at once and they all have a different rate of fire/projectile speed. As long as you fire one shot to open the loot everything has always remained intact for me. Same goes for finishing off fighters/nomads and anyone else with anything lootable imho. One shot will get your loot, anything more may mean dmg to the cargo. I've tested it in a million different places and completely mixed up weapon loadouts with really slow moving/fast moving projectiles and my loot always stays where it should be.............floating around the wreck awaiting my precious tractor beam.

esun

Thu May 01, 2003 12:40 am by esun

I just worked on getting nomad weapons today (first time in the unknown from Omicron Gamma) and found it pretty easy (Eagle w/ Salamancas, Blue Blazes, and some similar lvl 10 weapons). I docked on Planet Primus, then launched, killed 4 nomads, re-docked, launched, killed 4 nomads, etc. That's the fastest way to get them, plus if you are damaged (but if you're good you should be able to avoid that) you can get repaired. From my experience, they will always attack when you launch from Planet Primus (I don't know about Gammu, since I only docked there once to find nothing useful).

When I was in the Unknown System, I got one nomad weapon (four cannons and a blaster) every four kills (only came in waves of four for me). But when I went back into Omicron Gamma, four nomads were waiting for me and I killed them and got two cannons from those four only, so I guess it depends.

EDIT: Also, I don't think using just one weapon to kill the fighter will matter. I used all six weapons the entire time and very rarely (maybe two or three times) did I get nothing from fighting the nomads.
