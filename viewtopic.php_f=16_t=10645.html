Minefields: What''s the trick to staying alive?

There's nothing I fear more in this game than a mine field. I'm not talking about the mines you drop out of your ship, but those huge mines that look like "Sputnik on steroids." In the few times when I've braved a minefield, I've almost always died. On those occassions where I didn't, I was probably down to my last nano-bot. I'm not exactly blundering through these things like a bull in a china shop, either. I've tried a couple different tacts.

First, I tried creeping through. I chose a nice slow speed, and edged into the mine/asteroid field. I did my best to steer well clear of any mines. Bam. I never saw the mine(s) that killed me, I was just dead. That made me suspect that they had some homing ability, and I needed to go through faster. I kicked in the cruise engine and zipped through. Even at this speed, the minefield I was in didn't seem particularly cluttered (Dublin system.) Bam. Never saw it.

The problem is, if I'm going slow, I can't tell if a mine is racing up behind me. If I'm going fast, I can't be sure that I didn't somehow skim too close to a mine. I tried one other approach, I used the "goto" command and let the ship choose it's own course around obstacles. While the AI did try to avoid mines, it didn't always get far enough away, and I was dead again.

So, what's going on? Are these mines homing in on passing ships? Are they magically appearing as I pass? Am I getting tunnel vision on the one on the left, and hitting the one on the right? What?
