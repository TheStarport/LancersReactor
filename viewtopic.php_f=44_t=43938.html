Cold_Void

Fri Jun 23, 2006 11:35 am by Cold_Void

your textures should all be on the same material if you want to save memory overhead - in the case of FL's original ships they use 1 main texture and then a couple accessory textures for hardpoints and engine grills and glass - but if you can fit all your textures on one material without sacrificing resolution that's the better way

there's no need to regroup your groups(and if you regroup two groups with different materials it won't show two different textures), just export the cmp

p.s there are texturing tutorials available, and really any tutorial for texturing ms3d objects will do - just don't try to do the texture mapping in milkshape, its editor is horrible
