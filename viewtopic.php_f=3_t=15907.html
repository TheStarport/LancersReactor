thefinn

Wed May 07, 2003 2:21 pm by thefinn

The Germanic and Norse languages represent compounded concepts with equally compounded words, the length of which is in principle unlimited. According to my old (1989) Guinness Book of Records, the "longest" German word is "Donaudampfschiffarhtselektrizitätenhauptbetriebswerkbauunterbeamtengesellschaft". By comparison, the longest word in Danish (a Norse language) is "Landsbyskolelærerenkealderdomsforsørgelseskasseselskabsbestyrelsesnæstformandssuppleant". The longest Swedish word is another 16 letters longer.

Oh, yes. Note that "Schiffart" is the old spelling. The recent spelling reforms in Germany have changed it to "Schifffart".
