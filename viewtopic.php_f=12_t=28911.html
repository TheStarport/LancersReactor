I would advise flashing the BIOS, which is always a good idea, but I think that the problem is that your MB battery is getting old. Try replacing the motherboard battery, because if it is not maintaining enough charge, the BIOS will not save information properly. They should only cost a few dollars so, even if it doesn't work, you won't be out of pocket much. You can get them at computer shops, or even at watch repair shops, etc. Make sure that it is the right voltage.

drivers definitley and a possible application/hardware conflict. its got squit all to do with yer motherboard. You have too many sound applications all loaded at once. Musicmatch jukebox is great, but how many others arethere, you've almost certainly got Media Player, Realplayer, Quicktime then there's all the others inc the ones you got with your sound device. I'd uninstall ALL multimedia players, ALL sound drivers, remove the sound device from device manager, run a registry checker/editor, then restart and allow Windows to redetct it and install with the latest drivers from the manufacturer (which you previously downloaded)
