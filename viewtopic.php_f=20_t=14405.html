Nog

Fri Apr 18, 2003 5:40 pm by Nog

You don't notice the difference because of the Nvidia chipset. Don't worry, this is a good thing. The GeForce chipsets are so good at interpolating colors these days that 16 bit and 32 bit resolutions have very minor differences when it comes to actual display on the monitor end of things. If you want to see the difference between the two, go to a system where the background textures contain lots of different colors that are shaded gradually in rainbow fashion. In 16 bit there will be a slightly noticible line between the shades of color that is not there in 32 bit resolutions. Even in 16 bit mode that line is virtually unnoticeable unles
s you know exactly what to look for and are actively searching for it.
