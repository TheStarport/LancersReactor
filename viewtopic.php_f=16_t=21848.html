Sherlog

Mon Jul 28, 2003 3:09 pm by Sherlog

You'll probably get bored with a Hammerhead in Omicron Theta (except in MP mode when missions with highest difficulty level are available), same as in Omega-41. BTW, in my earlier post I confused Omega-41 and Omicron Theta - it is Omega-41 where you can get $90k missions in SP mode; in Omicron Theta there are only the $50k Bounty Hunter jobs.

I think a good rule of thumb for optimal mission difficulty is ship class times 5, times 6 for very good ships (Anubis ...), and up to times 7 for truly outstanding ships like Dagger and Drake. This ensures that the missions are challenging enough to be interesting but not so hard as to become frustrating. I.e. you have a fair chance to get killed when you are sloppy but otherwise there is no doubt that you will come back with your ship completely intact (perhaps one fireball in 50 missions). Adjust the factor downwards if you have sub-optimal equipment.

Of course, in SP mode the problem is to find places with good missions for class 6 ships and higher ...

Sherlog

Mon Jul 28, 2003 5:33 pm by Sherlog

Nukenin, the FA drop those guns so you don't need to get friendly with them.

Incidentally, the FA base was the only one in Kusari that refused to give me missions at level 9. There is a chance that the FA missions are a bit less boring than other jobs in Kusari (best is difficulty 15 in Hokkaido which is tamer than the local fauna there ).

I do not like weapon groups very much either (cumbersome, glitchy) but against high-level enemies it is a big advantage to have a shield buster because it helps to destroy enemies faster while using less energy. The difference is especially noticable if you fight against groups of VHF in a Drake ... Pity that there is so little choice of shield busters below class 7; the Corsair thingy is a bit weakish for difficulty 35+ missions and it forces you to use slower main guns (or accept mismatched projectile speed but that negates the advantages of a light fighter).

If you have a single shield buster gun then you can configure the caps lock key to toggle it on and off. Works a treat, especially if you synchronize the weapon state with the caps lock LED on your keyboard before heading into battle.
