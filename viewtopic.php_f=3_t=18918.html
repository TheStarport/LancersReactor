Ether Dragon

Mon Jun 09, 2003 4:41 pm by Ether Dragon

I don't have just one favorite. The minute I choose one ship, I immediately start craving another one.

I like the Legionaire for it's graceful wings, but hate it's cockpit design. I can only fly it until I have to use cockpit view.

I like the Drake for it's high maneuverability. I think it's the most capable fighter in the game. While I like the cockpit and weapon mounts, something about it (the lack of real wings?) just throws me off.

I like the Mule for it's sheer ugliness. It's stained and paint-chipped exterior reminds me of Star Wars ships and the general seediness of the pirates that use the ship. It's slapped together and does away with form in favor of functionality. This ship should've been the starter ship for Trent, IMHO.

I like the Barracuda/Hammerhead for it's narrow, vertical appearance. I thought it was a pretty unique look until I saw similarities in "Lost in Space" and the B-Wing of "Star Wars." Even so, I feel that this ship has the most menacing appearance of any ship within Freelancer.

I like the Crusader...and I don't know why.

Every so often I fly a Hawk/Falcon/Eagle for their sleek look. Unfortunately, they also have a very generic aspect, that soon always leaves them as a stepping stone to something "better."

I like the Dagger, but can't stand it's heavy fighter or VHF iterations. The Dagger has a sleek space fighter look about it. Something about that lower boom on the larger variants just don't work. Ironically, they were the ships I longed for before the game was released and I was merely looking at screenshots/cover art.

I like the Clydesdale the most among the freighters. It's sleek and powerful looking, and I love the cockpit design. I think the thing that I enjoy the most about the Bretonian ships is the sleek intake grills worked into the body.
