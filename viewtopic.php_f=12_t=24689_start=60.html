Sorry to dig it up, but I just found the answer to my brain teaser about those two professors.
So, hint one:
if we multiply their ages, we get 36. We have 8 options: 1-1-36, 1-2-18, 1-3-12, 1-4-9, 1-6-6, 2-2-9, 2-3-6, 3-4-3
Hint two: the sum of their ages equals to the number of windows in the house in front of the two profs. We don't know the number of windows, but THEY do. So, we add up all 8 cases: 38, 21, 16, 14, 13, 13, 11, 10. All are different except the two ones with 13. It must be the number of the windows, since otherwise the third hint would be irrelevant.

Hint three: the name of the eldest son is Robert (or whatever, does not matter).
In those two cases with 13, one is 1-6-6, the other is 2-2-9. The first one doesn't fit, 'cause there are TWO elder sons, so the only logical option is 2-2-9.
