Very handy when you're running away with a freighter. Plus they do hit fairly often, at least the rippers do (just make sure you don't hit them yourself while using the rear turrets).

Meatboy Dogfood

Wed May 14, 2003 4:21 am by Meatboy Dogfood

Mines work as part of the package. Like they say in other post drop them right about the time a ship is passing you. If you thrust towards your target you can drop one extra as you close. The way that works is the mine you drop first will follow you coasting on the speed of your thrusters. When you drop the second mine as you pass, the foe will hit it losing shieds and a fair amount of the time they will ram the second mine following you being severely damaged if not outright destroyed. The chaces for a kil go up greatly if you were able to soften them up with weaon fire as they closed.

I like to use seeker mines {and yes the the Ripper is a seeker} But be aware Seeker mine are not smart mines.Iif there are no ships around you the mines will home back on you. The non seekers while doing more damage tend to hit less often.


Using mines while fleeing is a nasty way to say good bye. My fav tatic is.

1.Fly at normal speed jinking till everyone is behind you. {Use the V key to look back or go into turret mode}

2.Drop a mine then thrust up to 200m.

3. Use the Z to kill your engines and go hyperbolic, drop a mine.

4. Roll over to face your pack O hounds and Merrily blast away at them till you are out of range.
