mustang

Tue Feb 10, 2004 4:59 am by mustang

Bah, I hate fruity loops it's got every half assed computer user out there thinking they are a fookin ace musician / mixer.
I'm a fan of Cool Edit Pro 2 though, providing you've got a decent sound card you can plug your axe straight into your pc and record away. It's lacking a drum machine though and the stock effects suck, I still use external effects with it. However you can lay down as many tracks as your heart desires and do some fairly heavy hacking and slashing to remove those not so nice bits where you ballsed it up.

Major gripe with it is that the play back of tracks is at a significantly lower volume than what you recorded it at. So when recording another track over the top it's fairly tricky getting your timing right because you can't hear the play back track over what your playing.

Still for all it's bad points it's a pretty cool utility for messing around in, but your not going to get a studio quality production with it.
