Options Are Nice

It's not a MMOG, but I would pay a subscription fee - and I think some others would too - for the experience of playing on a persistent, well-maintained, high-ping 120-something-player server.

Some people feel crowded on a server with upwards of 30 players. That's fine. They should have options (as they do). However, some of us come from MMORPGS and are actually *more* comfortable in worlds populated simultaneously by thousands of players. There's no right or wrong; these are only preferences. But to us, 128 players would seem a small thing to ask.

Would there be legal tie-ups? I don't know. It's likely Microsoft or Digital Anvil wouldn't care if someone was out there charging subscription fees to maintain awesome servers, because they could have chose this model and they didn't.

The server would have to be rewritten from what I understand, but it's an idea.

Proteus, I just felt the need to point something out. You said you desired a server with a high ping, but a good server is one with a LOW ping. It might have been a typo on your part...but incase it wasn't, I provided a little explanation. The number you get is how long (in milliseconds) it takes for information to get to the other computer and back. So basically, the higher the number, the slower it'll be.

Anyway I do agree, I would really like a stable server that has an average of 100+ players on at one time. With that number of players, someone could arrange a place for dueling.
