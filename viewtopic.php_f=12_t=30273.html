Technical Question About Caching DLLs

My PC magazine wanderings led me to a longtime favorite the other day, CPU (magazine).

They had a little Registry tweak good for all versions of windows so that your system would force unload unusued but cached DLLs.

Question: Aren't some DLLs that are cached used commonly by other programs? If you force your cache to unload all DLLs used by, FL, let's say (just as hypothetical example), and this also unloads DLLs cached by other running programs, will those DLLs be re-cached by the other in-use application?

Esquilax

Sat Apr 10, 2004 5:57 am by Esquilax

Yes. If you have programs that are accessing the same DLL files that have just been unloaded, they will be re-cached when the program runs. This does of course mean that they will be cleared from the cache after the latter program ends. You CAN save RAM this way, but I don't really think that it is worth it, unless you need every least MB, and/or have only a small amount of RAM.
