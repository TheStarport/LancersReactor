WatercoolerWarrior

Wed Dec 15, 2004 5:19 am by WatercoolerWarrior

Two comments, on the difficulty of beating Hovis and a simple hack for the race that I haven't seen mentioned.

Difficulty: skill, reflexes, and ship all are part of this. I think the machine performance comments are dead on as the reason why many people who have tried repeatedly can't beat him, though.

I play FL on a P3-600/384 MiB RAM with a 32MiB graphics card and if I use high detail settings and resolution, I lose every time unless I cut off cruise in the last half - I miss rings then because of the lag. If I drop to low resolution, low quality, I don't have a problem.

For easy hacks... well, just give Hovis an extra advantage. ))

In constants.ini, under EngineEquipConsts, bump up cruise speed. A ridiculous amount like in the "translightengine mod" is
<pre><font size=1 face=Courier>CRUISING_SPEED = 300000 </font></pre>
In the same file under PhySysConsts you also need to set
<pre><font size=1 face=Courier>ANOM_LIMITS_MAX_VELOCITY = 300000 </font></pre>

Then when you race, DON'T go on cruise. The AI doesn't adapt to this very well; Hovis ends up way out there (30k+) and keeps trying to tack back and forth on cruise. After the race, drop the changes and go back to normal.

Off-topic, the high speed hack does give you some cool rear views if you just take off in cruise; you can see the entire system shrink behind you. Go "up" or "down" and you get a birds-eye view of the system that lets you see large scale structures like clouds that are on the map.
