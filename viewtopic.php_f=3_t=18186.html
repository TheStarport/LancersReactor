Fulcrum

Sat May 31, 2003 3:06 am by Fulcrum

to add to that, you must not take MISSIONs from anyone. You have to kill Xenos on your accord and live on the junk you salvage or the silver from some of the asteroids you can shoot at. When you take a mission from a corporation, police or military, your reputation becomes affected. i did that to get neutral with the Rogues to buy a Dagger. It was worth it because the Dagger is the best looking ship in the game. and seeing one in the hanger closeup proves it.

Then I made an interesting career path. With the Dagger, I only accepted military and police missions, so I can use the Dagger to kill pirates. But I don't accept taking missions from corporations. If you accept missions from corporations your rep with the Junkers will sour. Keeping myself neutral with the Junkers was intentional because I intended to get a Legionaire, which is on a Junkers base.

After I got the Legionaire, I take only missions from IMG bases against Corsairs. Keeping the enemy only as the Corsairs, it keeps the Bundswehr and the Hessions neutral to slight green. When I need a new ship, I go to Rhineland and pick up Stilleto from the Bund, and continue fighting only the Corsairs, and when the money is right, pick up a Sabre from the Hessians.

When you're rep is 2 squares less than neutral or better you can get missions for Junkers. Your rep with them, rogues, corsairs and outcasts will all go up a lot and police only go down a bit, or none at all if you do xenos killing missions.

I got the Dagger this way after five or six missions from rochester. The only faction who won't like you are Hogosha, but they are enemies with xenos, so it can all work out in the end. Using this method I got neutral to everyone (except xenos) throughout single player and multi as well. Bundschuch like xenos though but you can get everyone neutral before they go red.
