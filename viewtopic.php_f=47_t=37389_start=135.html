Tue Jul 05, 2005 7:57 am by Codename

Hm. Novel. Let's see where this goes.

Two quick points though; Dominic should be over 800 years old (chronologically, of course) since the events in Freelancer - which lead up to our game - take place around 800 AS, iirc. (I'm assuming Dom left around the same time as the sleeper ships but didn't have the benefit of any sort of FTL drive.) Also, I believe the enemies of the Alliance were known as the Coalition, not the Confederation.

Edit:
Okay, so I've read your first post. It's quite good. I don't think it does the whole "fish out of water" theme justice though. I mean, here the guy is, in a ship no-one but the most avid history buff will recognize showing up out of nowhere. He won't have any faction tags or a Neural Net or anything like that. It's quite possible that he'd have no way to communicate at all with the denizens of Sirius - at least, not while he's still on his ship.

He knows about the Sleeper ships, so he'll be expecting the dominant forces to be Bretonia, Hispania, Kusari, Liberty and Rheinland but he won't know where they are. He certainly won't know where he is, nor will he have any way of figuring that out until he lands somewhere, imo. He won't know about docking rings since they are Ageira inventions. So he'll probably just angle to the nearest inhabited planet and land there - much to everyone's great surprise - the old-fashioned way.

There's also the issue of where he'll actually pitch up. It seems most plausible that the AI will wake our slumbering voyager at first contact with an inhabited planet. Now, I don't know where Sirius (not the star, the sector) is in relation to Sol but I find it hard to believe that he'll wind up all the way in the Texas system. It seems more likely that he'd wake in one of the Edge/Border Worlds, or maybe Edinburgh or Dublin. Look at the FL universe map and pick a system of your choice which is on the "circumference" rather than in the interior.

Um. That's all I can think of right now. Consider what I've written and make any changes you'd like. The idea definitely has a lot of potential. Make the most of it.

If you have any questions, e-mail me. Address is in my profile and I check it often.
