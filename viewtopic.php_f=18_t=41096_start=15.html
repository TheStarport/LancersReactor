Sun Oct 02, 2005 2:04 am by Argh

@5i1:

You're mis-understanding. What I'm saying is... that the "Cargo Upgrade" will be the Armor downgrade. IOW, it's a reverse of what you're talking about- to get more Cargo, people will have lose Armor. Voila. It still works... it's logically consistant... it's just not in the order you want. Which is tough, but it's not our fault that FL's designers didn't let us use negative values for a lot of numbers...

From a code perspective... we're talking about a Powerplant, a Scanner, or whatever. It has to be a Powerplant or some other Internal hardware, because those are the only things that take up Cargo when mounted. Doesn't really matter- just go steal the Armor Upgrade code from some mod that already has this concept, and you're good to go.

So... write up a description like this:

Armor

Armor panels are composed of (make up some pseudoscience garbage here), and are initially mounted onto all ships sold in the Sirius System, with the assumption being that the fighter will be used primarily for escort/security duties. Sell your Armor panels to gain more Cargo space.

Then put a low monetary value on the Armor Upgrades, so that you're not handing people a lot of "free money"...and voila! Problem solved. Again... you're solving the problem... just... backwards. Same result, different method... and it's reversible! If people add the Armor panels, then they'll take up Cargo space!

All of that said... and please, don't take this personally, because this isn't meant to disrespect you sharing this with use... I think that, from a game-balance standpoint... armor and cargo upgrades are totally junk ideas. Why? Because in both cases, they cannot effect Mass. No Equipment, even the internal stuff, has any effect on the total Mass of a ship, period. Which means that Armor and/or Cargo upgrades are essentially "free" of any negative consequences, unless you've put in a generic value and planned out the add-on veeeeeery carefully.
