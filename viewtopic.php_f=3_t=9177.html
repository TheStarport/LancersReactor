Whats it mean when an item is perishable?(I know what perishable means, but does it act as if, i got shot alot or i bounced off rocks the item would be destroyed in my cargo?)

Actually, that's what "fragile" means. Perishable means it spoils over time. In Freelancer, this means that while you carry this cargo it is slowly decaying. Look at your inventory window while in flight and you'll see a progress bar under the item's icon that is slowly moving. When it goes to zero, one item will be removed from your inventory and the timer will start again. So figure you will lose some percentage of the cargo units you buy as you travel to your destination. How much is something you'll have to discover on your own. Buy a few units and see how long it takes to decay before you commit a bunch of money on perishable or highly perishable goods.

Lastly, I saw a post about a guy doing ROGUE jobs? if possible where can i get these jobs? and while playing a guy told me he was going to get a rogue viehical etc, how is this possible

Some outlaw bases do sell ships. These bases are well off the normal trade lanes and you'll have to be neutral or better with the base owner's faction before they'll let you land. If you're friendly enough, they'll even let you pick up missions in the bar. Keep in mind though that doing missions for Rogues will lower factions with all of their enemies... including the police, navy, and pretty much all the law-abiding citizens of Liberty.

Zem

Tue Mar 11, 2003 2:32 am by Zem

Selling drugs has no effect whatsoever on your faction with anyone. A good question though, is whether or not you lose faction with police when you refuse to drop that cargo after being scanned. The police will attack of course, but I'm not sure the mere act of them going temporarily hostile on you has any permanent effect on that faction. Not that I care too much. It only happens rarely.

And no, you likely will not be able to dock at Manhattan. I think that's nominally under the control of the Navy. In general, you cannot dock at hostile bases. Bases are hostile if the owning faction is hostile. Also, a base can turn hostile temporarily if you run across an enemy near it that is allied to the base owner. Example: I was friendly with Lane Hackers but hostile to Outcasts. I approached a Lane Hacker base recently and on approach it was green (friendly) until I ran into an Outcast. Then everything in the area went red and I left with my butt on fire. Another example would be getting hostile to a Liberty corporation because you keep blowing up their freighters. You might be neutral to the police, but if you show up outside Manhattan at the same time a freighter convoy from that particular corporation does, everyone will come after you and you won't be able to dock.

Trade lanes and jump gates seem to be an exception. Not sure if it's working as intended, but it seems you can use lanes and jump gates even if they are red on your screen and firing on you. Heck, I didn't even notice gates had guns on them until I took up a life of crime
