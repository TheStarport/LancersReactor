warzog

Wed Jun 01, 2005 3:53 am by warzog

The missions were removed when the Gemini Sector was created.
I tried to modify the missions to work with Gemini, but they wouldn't.

As to which versions did what:
Versions 1.## Added ships/weapons to the standard Freelancer game.
Versions 2.## Added Privateer Commodities to the standard Freelancer game.
Versions 3.## Modified Freelancer's Universe to match the Gemini Sector.
Versions 4.## Added the Gemini Sector, and removed the missions.

There's also a ship pack that will add several ships to the standard Freelancer Game.

If you have one of the 4.## versions, and have the missions, you've got a corrupt download, or a bad installation of the mod.

Every version should still be available here at LR.
Simply do a search for Reckoning, or Warzog.

NOTE:
Due to an unknown problem with my current computer, there are corrupt files in the last few mods.
(Part of the reason I stopped working on it.)
The first few mods have bugs that were due to my inexperience, at the time.

Sir Spectre

Mon Jun 27, 2005 4:44 pm by Sir Spectre

@Warzog, well I've finally been playing the mod and reimmersing myself back into Privateer. I have to say this mod is excellently done. I actually got a couple of those old thrills back as I afterburned into the jumpholes hoping my ship jumped before any pirates shot down my shields. Ah, the good old days of Privateer are back! There are a heck of a lot more pirates and retros than I remember, but that's a good thing.

There are a few commodity problems, mostly to do with improper pricing or not providing enough of the commodities usually found at each station in the original game. But nothing too extraordinarily glaring, except I can't seem to find any place that sells Uranium, I see Plutonium from time to time, but so far no Uranium. I somehow picked up a few Freelancer commodities, which is no big deal and actually adds to the immensity of the sector.

The music is a great addition! In fact, I love the music much more than the FL music. I even remember one planet that had a terrific piano score. The mining bases, Perry & New Constantinople are superb model creations for the game. I am mightily impressed.

One thing that is annoying, but not bad at all, is that there are so many other ships on similar or intersecting flight paths that on GOTO I often see my ship running into and hitting those other ships. If I had to pay insurance for space collisions, I'd be paying astronomical rates or I would bankrupt the insurance company who had to pay my claims.

Anyway, I finally can give you a sincere congrats on your hard work. It came out marvelously and so far very few CTDs for me. The combat is fun, I think I remember the Centurion and Orion being a little stronger, but I'm having a lot of exciting dogfights using the Galaxy.

Great work. It's a shame your computer kept messing up the files and you had to end your modding.

Thanks Warzog, and thank you Bob for starting it and continuing it.
