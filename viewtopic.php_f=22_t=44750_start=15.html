Very few things strike me as unsportsman like than camping out near a docking ring or a station and blowing the bejesus out of anybody coming out or trying to go in. IMHO, this activity is neither RP or PVP, it's simple harassment.
I actually have a solution for that....
NOMADS and lots of them!!
What I've done in my mod is enable nomads throughout most systems. This also means you'll likely encounter them as soon as you launch off of Manhattan. BUT...this also prevents anyone from camping nearby because the nomads will take it upon themselves to "light 'em up" . Now, anyone launching from Manhattan or most other planets or bases will most likely encounter them, but at least they have a better chance of escaping than they would with some uscrupelous camper.

These may be only lvl 10 nomads, BUT..their laser refire rates will certainly kill anyone (including anyone in capital ships) who just sit there. With a refire rate of 4 per laser at a damage of over 1000 per shot, then you have a group of 12 show up . Even my best capital ships have taken a severe beating by them.

Then there are the upper lvl nomads...these fellas have cruise disruptors and they are in the "Punitive" system as well as the "outworld" systems. Very tough hombres.
The "Punitive" system I created is called "Antartica" and just like the Original Alaska is a dead end system. One way in, One way out. But....
To send a player to this system is "punishment" where they are stripped of their pride and joy ship and given a choice of basic ships. Then they must travel throughout this system to "earn" a given level of credits for a "Pardon" out of the system. Meanwhile, since they are "stuck" in this system, the upper lvl nomads and bounty hunters will be after them making their stay in Antartica not such a pleasant experience.
Then there is the various base characters who have something to say to the offender when they go check on the rumors. (Some of it quite humorous I might add)

So, enabling nomads throughout most systems (including new systems) will basically take care of the unsportsman problem for itself.
