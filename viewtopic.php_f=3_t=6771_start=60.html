INVERTED MOUSE CONTROL IS NOT IMPOSSIBLE.

Ok, now that the thread is back on topic, let me explain how it CAN work.

Give Freelancer two options: one for standard mouse control, one for inverted.
For inverted mode: When NOT piloting the ship, the mouse behaves as a standard arrow for targeting, etc. When piloting the ship, the arrow DISAPPEARS. In its place is a targeting reticle. You move right, the reticle goes right. You move left, the reticle goes left. You move up, the reticle goes down. You move down, the reticle goes up. INVERTED MOUSE CONTROL. The reticle is NOT fixed; it is simply inverted on the Y-axis. There is nothing hard about it at all. If you're saying it's impossible you're assuming that turret controls aren't inverted as well. If you put in a floating reticle to replace the arrow, the turrets become aligned with the flight controls.

Sorry to resort to shouting but inverted control is easily implemented, despite what others may say.
