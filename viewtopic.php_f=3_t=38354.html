Boscoe

Tue Mar 29, 2005 2:31 pm by Boscoe

He was there for a while in the original builds. I was a beta tester for FL (not bragging, it was a true honor), and there was so much of this game left on the cutting room floor. If one caught Santiago, and got the million, their game is basically over. It was taken out because it was too easy a way to get that many credits, and the game would lose it's main purpose, which was to explore as much as possible and find ways to make your money. Much of the beta testing was about how much money you could make doing different things. One build let you go to Colorado and mine silver so easily that one load brought to California Minor would put you immediately to level 38. So, there are still remnants of the things we all tested, but they don't always lead to fortunes. Simple rule: If you're playing the original unmodded game, if it sounds too good to be true, it is. We found a cargo loop that's still in the game which will get you lots of money very quickly, but I'm not telling you about it here! Email me if you want it. (It's not a mod, just a way we found to make money quick).

Boscoe

Sat Apr 02, 2005 9:55 am by Boscoe

From what I remember about the early builds, there was a "secret" mission where if you caught Santiago you'd get a million. I'm not 100% clear on this, but I remember something about it, but we found it so quickly it was too easy. The prices and difficulties of mining, missions, sizes of cargo holds, was much of what we worked on during the later betas. It took a lot of changes and tweaking on DA's part to get the game balanced, they worked almost constantly on it for months, gave us tasks on how much money we'd make doing so and so, and they modified accordingly. It was a fun time. However, the name Santiago Llorens is still in there, since it really didn't matter about the name.
