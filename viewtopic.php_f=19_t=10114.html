LOL, good advice, Apocalypse!

And I do try to talk to folks. I say hi when I log in, and whenever I'm in a system with other people. But most of the time I get no answer, and I only occasionally see people working together. I may just have logged in on a few off days for the server, but it does seem a fairly limited multiplayer game.

I don't fault the other players for that, btw. I fault Microsoft. They could have made this a much more engaging multiplayer game, they could even have turned it into an MMORPG, but instead they did this weird cross between a Quake-style multiplayer game and an MMORPG. The limited number of players per server, combined with the problem of rebuilding a character on each server, has limited the game far too much.

Microsoft wouldn't even have had to invest as much money as in a full-scale MMORPG to make it work, that's the sad thing. Provide stable 24-hour servers that can accept 500 players at a time, and they could even charge a small monthly fee and get away with it. It should be within the realm of possibility.

In case you can't tell, I'm a bit disappointed in the game. :
