Ut

Sun May 11, 2003 9:11 pm by Ut

I'm not sure I understand the laughter.
For $500 CND I built myself an Athlon XP 2000+ with 512 MB of RAM (sans monitor, video, and sound card).
That was 8 months ago, when the Canadian dollar was all of 64 cents US (making it all of $320 USD).

Therefore, a 1000 MHz system should be as costly as a pile of dirt at this point in time.

The real key is to spend a little bit on a halfway decent video card, and buy a used system between 700 and 1000 MHz. If you actually have a computer (I'm assuming you do, due to the post, but I've been wrong before), then you should be able to get the processor and mobo rather cheaply (plus, you save on a costly monitor). The video card and RAM are the keys, though, as my parents computer, with its GeForce 2 MMX and its 128 MB PC-133 simply can't handle the game.

If you shop around, you'll find exactly what you need right around the price you're looking at ($250ish).
