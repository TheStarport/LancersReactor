it will affect "normal" people although not straight away, but by Christmas you won't be able to get jack else; that's how the production schedule works. There's normally a 6 month tail off period while the new stuff gradually gets stocked and the old stuff gets run down.

By the time the spending season starts in earnest, te stores will be full of the new stuff. However those with an eye for a bargain on a ready-made should come to Uncle Taw's errm I mean go to the January sales and get the P4 Socket 478 stuff that'll be sold as clearance.

however current stuff will keep you going happily for another year to 18months before you should really think about upgrading, and then only if you want to play new games.

this may seem all mindboggling for those without the technical experience, but the truth is that the PCI (Peripheral Component Integration) system bus is ancient, it goes back at least a decade. It has been a boon in that it allowed proper plug and play; whereas before with the ISA (industry Standard Architecture) bus the card had to have its resource allocation set by jumpers, with PCI the system allocates resources itself. However the bus speed of PCI has been bottlenecking computers for years, while the components ie cpus, ram, video adaptors etc have been racing ahead in terms of speed and bandwidth the system bus hasn't kept pace at all. PCI-Xpress will resolve that issue but anything old PCI will not go into PCI-Xpress, it's too fast for the old stuff.

as to those 2.88mb floppys, don't worry about that, that's a specific use that I can gaurantee none of us will ever have to use, in all probability.
