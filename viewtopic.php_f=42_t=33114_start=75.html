As to the Starscapes...
I used the same one over, and over, just to speed things up.
Besides, I wasn't sure that the whole idea would work.
I'd love to have some Unique Starscapes.
But someone's gonna have to make them, and create 3DF & MAT files for each one.

As to the green suns...
Look at the Gemini Sector map...
The one with the systems, and jumplanes on it.
What color(s) are the stars?
I tried to get the suns in each system to match the ones on the map.
There's Green, Red, Blue, Yellow, Orange, White, etc.
Well...
Anyway...
That's the reason.

"LOTS of debris! Sometimes too much."
When I started making this version, I noticed that without Freelancer's tradelanes, there was no real feeling of movement.
It was like you just sat there, and if you waited long enough, eventually things got bigger.
So...
In the first few systems I did, I added debris.
(Mainly the Clarke and Potter Quadrants.)
Then I noticed that it was becoming a bit much...
So, I switched to using dust(s) to convey movement.
About 90% of the Debris-Filled systems are in the Clarke Quadrant.
(To show the Kilrathi's constant attacks in the Sector.)
There are 7 in Clarke, 2 in the Potter Quadrant, 1 in Humboldt, and there aren't any in the Fariss Quadrant.

The Retros have bases in the 17-AR, Famine, Varnus, Gemini, ND-57, and DN-N1912 systems.
That's where you'll find them concentrated.
If you hang around long enough, they eventually show up in most of the systems.

The Pirates are setup fairly closely to the way they were in Privateer.
(I just added them here and there to add life to otherwise dull systems.)

The Steltek are only in the Fariss Quadrant, and the Kilrathi are only in the Clarke Quadrant.
Ooops!
Almost forgot...
Both are in one of the Potter Pleasure Planet systems.
I think I put them in the Shangri La or DN-N1912 System, for the tourists to go up against.
(Fairly weak versions, but still tough.)
