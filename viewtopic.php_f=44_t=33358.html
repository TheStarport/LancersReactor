black_lightning

Thu Jul 29, 2004 1:32 pm by black_lightning

well keep in mind if you use the mat exporter and you have the word glass anywhere in your texture name, it rather then using your texture will have a completely made up ingame freelancer one. Aka, it doesn't set the opacity for your models texture, it doesn't even load that texture into the mat, rather it uses a kinda default freelancer glass... so in other words never do this to your entire ship, only for cockpit glass, and only if your cockpit glass is a stand alone texture without anything else in it (differing opacities).

Another thing which occasionally happens when dealing with transparencies used in .dds or targa format which I've seen a number of times, is it causes a kinda peep hole where you can look through your entire ship when looking through that glass texture. An odd thing, not entirely sure why it happens but I do know how to fix it when it happens, I always just select the cockpit's group in milkshape and just press regroup, then reweld, re export, and no longer have the effect caused by looking through a transparent texture to look through the rest of the ships mesh (maybe something to do with the order it reads in the meshs). I've only seen this happen occasionally with cockpits, which are a small part of the ship, so has never induced the entire ship to be invisible, but it could still be possible in regards to your model if your entire ship is trying to be transparent.

Make sure all your textures are random names which you have never used before, make sure none contain the word glass in them, and regroup the parts of your ship which are to be transparent, re export everything and see what happens.
