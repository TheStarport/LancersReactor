Ein-7919

Thu Apr 24, 2003 3:53 pm by Ein-7919

Remember folks, the Apache was designed for tank-busting operations. The Longbow epitomizes this seeing as how it can rail-fire 16 hellfires and guide them to unique targets. The Longbow FCR really opened up the AH-64D's capabilities in taking out entrenched armor. While the RAH-66 is nice and all (what being a stealth helicopter in flight, if not during fire missions) it was designed as a scout/attack craft. Meaning, the Comanche is just not suited to the true attack aircraft role. It is a light recon craft, comparable to a heavily armed Kiowa Warrior. As you can tell, I am kind of biased towards the Longbow...but then again, any aircraft that can take several 30mm cannon rounds and still protect the pilots deserves respect (which is why I also hold the A-10 in high esteem as well). Now for a pro/con list of the AH-64D:

Pro:
Semi-stealthy (has an exhaust redirector to dispense the heat from the engine evenly across the area around it, and a "blackhole" radar jammer that jams radar in about a 45 degree angle on each side)
Can carry up to 16 Hellfire Mk2 laser/radar guided missiles as well as 4 Stinger/Sidewinder ATA missiles.
Rotors can withstand 12.7mm machine gun fire (it might have been 20mm...but I'm not too sure about that), the fuselage can take 30mm cannon fire, and the pilot compartment (calling it that to avoid using cockpit) can take 20mm rounds.
Longbow FCR can track 256 independant targets and can designate multiple PFZ's to attack up to 16 targets simultaneously and send off PFZ's to wing-mates

Cons:
Speed (limited to about 160 knots)
Lacks true stealth abilities
maneuverability could be better (but this is restricted because of the overall mass of the aircraft...the Apache ain't a Comanche/Kiowa Warrior)

I think that's about it. Dang, this post is long. Signing off. Good hunting...out!
