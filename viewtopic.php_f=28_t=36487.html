Argh

Fri Dec 17, 2004 10:44 am by Argh

You're much more likely to get a response to people if you can:

A. Express a very vivid image of your game design, including the world it's set in, the gameplay enhancements you're bringing to the table, and the general "look and feel".

B. Provide an early demo or three. It took 3 Alpha releases of Warriors of the Sky before I started seeing a large number of 3D content creators coming to my assistence, and I had to do the modeling/skinning for the first 3 Alphas entirely on my own. Even if you're barely able to model/skin... it doesn't matter very much, if you've got clever gameplay ideas. Show people what you've got, even if you have to re-use FL models for your ships.

In short, people won't give you their free time unless they see that you're doing the hard work necessary to see things through, and you need a really special concept to get the attention you need. There aren't that many people who can make good 3D content for your game out there, and you have to attract them by being organized and by showing everybody that you can put the work where your dreams are
