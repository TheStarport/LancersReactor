I'm flying a falcon at the moment, I have a good weapon loadout, and I can put up a good fight against most VHF's by avoiding fire and being able to just turn quicker and get them more then they can me.

I'm currently looking at the centurion, mainly because it can mount some level 9 guns, but I'm pretty sure it flys worse than my falcon, meaning people who I'm fighting will hit me more than before, which isn't desirable when they're packing class 10's.

So, is the extra firepower and shielding worth the loss of maneuverability? Or should I keep my falcon and be safe in the knowledge I can avoid fire, but take longer to wear them down.

Ether Dragon

Mon Jun 02, 2003 4:39 pm by Ether Dragon

My personal favorite (and I'm not a VHF fan either) would be the Drake. It's probably the most manueverable ship in the game, as well as being a very small target. Let's not forget it's stylish good looks. I've fought off waves of Titans and Sabres with the little guy, personally.

The biggest concern pilots might have when looking at the ship is it's light shielding and weaponry. The answer, however, is in it's superior maneuverability. VHF's simply can't keep up with it's agility, so your shields are actually protecting you from those instances where you accidently run through the random spray generated by a dogfight. That same manueverability lets you doggedly stay on your target. Where a VHF (or heavy fighter, for that matter) relies on it's firepower to knock down a shield in one or two hits when the opportunity presents itself, the Drake can stay on target and rain continuous damage on it. Thus, your weapons might do less damage per shot, but you'll hit more often than a VHF.

The Drake lives for the dogfight. You want to close with your enemies quickly, get amongst them, and then chew them up while they wheel about in frustration. Avoid that first encounter where everyone usually just exchanges shots while closing. Just dodge their fire until they close with you, then try to avoid laughing while you tear them apart - it'll mess up your aim.
