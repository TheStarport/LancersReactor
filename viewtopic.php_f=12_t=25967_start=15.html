I usually ran across when the Radeon 9800 Pro came out was that it needed more than 128Mb of video memory. Double the volume to 256Mb was a common suggestion. Again, however, that was for the 9800 Pro. Can it make a difference? Like say for HL2 if it ever comes out?

Lets put it this way: Imagine you have a texture 128x128 pixels in size, 32-bit pallete, 32bit texture depth. You would need *deep breath* (128x32)+(128x32)+(128x32)x32x8x8 (x,y,z,color,T&L,bump mapping) bits = 24MB for storing and processing that texture in one cycle. Original HL has an average texture size 64x64 and in some cases 96x96, and assuming that HL2 will use 128x128-256x256, yes, the quantity of memory COULD make a difference, but with high speed DDR (4 ns and faster) that difference between 128 and 256MB of mem shouldn't be more than, say 5-15%, depending on the vid card.
