WatercoolerWarrior

Mon Dec 06, 2004 1:18 pm by WatercoolerWarrior

Some miscellaneous comments after running through 2.71. This is apparently not the most recent version, and I'm not sure about the exact release "letter" since it isn't shown in script.xml, but the download was epsilon271.zip.flmod and script.xml is dated 2004-09-04.

In general, this is an excellent mod in my opinion. I haven't felt this terrified leaving the Manhattan docking ring since my first time playing Freelancer.

Possible bug/tweak items below.

+ Error loading epsilon1.dll
I always get errors in my flspew for epsilon1.dll:
Couldn't load epsilon1.dll! Error code 998
In case it might be a file corruption error, the file is dated 2004-08-32, size is 98,304 bytes, and the CRC32 checksum I get for it is 443437e4.

+ Gameplay on slower-than-recommended systems
It actually works OK on my P3-600 (384MiB RAM, 32MiB nVidia video card). Some miscellaneous crashes on new systems where I'm seeing odd things with rumors and names, so it may be related to the epsilon1.dll error above.

+ Taipan
The Taipan rides a little high in dogfights, enough so that the upper wing plane tends to obscure targets ahead. In my version, the f_taipan model's camera_offset data in shiparch.ini is
camera_offset = 9, 38
After some experimenting, it looks like changing this to 18, 38 is definitely too much, but values in the range of 12-15 generally seem to work well - a decent view of the ship under cruise but the targets don't get obscured by the wing.

Longstorm

Mon Jan 31, 2005 5:36 am by Longstorm

I am writing just to thank you and the many others in the team who created the Epsilon MOD for Freelancer. When I first read the reviews of the original game, and having been a big Elite fan years ago, looked forward to playing the Freelancer programme. Having plaid it to its conclusion (story wise), and for several days after, reaching 14 million credits and over 2600 kills, I started to search the web for any MODs that would extend the game and enhance the experience of Freelancer.

I then discovered the Lancersreactor website and several MODs. The predicable and very enjoyable Star Wars MOD, a must for all space fans. And then your Epsilon MOD. I liked the look of a lot of your ships and couldn’t wait to download the MOD and try hen out. You said players new to the MOD would die several times in Liberty alone, and I did just that. Those Rogues are something else ! I did however come up with a few new tactics that outsmarted them and allowed me to get to Planet Huston. There I bought the Warlord fighter (after a while of collecting mission bounties, and running Cademine to Planet Manhattan), and have not looked back since.

Thank you very much indeed for your MOD. I was tinkering with adjustments to my own MOD, with pilots being ejected from blown fighters and transporting them back for a bounty. (You thought of that one too). And transferring prisoners from one planet’s jail to another’s. I also had the idea of transporting three kinds of people. Travellers split into the very wealthy (high cost), who could be sold to pirate bases for a ransom; the middle class passengers (medium cost), looking to pay passage from one planet top another; and refugees (low cost), who are just keen to get out from one place to another.

It doesn’t seem right that the only people flying from place to place in the systems are traders and fighter pilots. There should be passenger liners too, which could be attacked and life pods transported to the fighter’s hold, and then you get paid for delivering them to a safe planet or station. (They don’t have to know that you were the one who destroyed their passenger liner in the first place….

Please pass on my thanks to all concerned, and though I didn’t know him, I think you have honoured the memory of your friend Worth Whitcomb with the Warlord fighter. I think of him as I’m flying the great craft through the space lanes, and I never met the chap.

Hope you keep up the good work. Its right on the nail.

Best wishes,

Longstorm
