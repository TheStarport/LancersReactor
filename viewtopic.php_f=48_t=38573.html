The trouble you will have with trying to get people to make new mods now is that everyone and their dog has an idea for what would be a 'cool' mod. However, most of the people who know how to mod will either A) be working on Mod's of their own with their own ideas or be working on one of the bigger mods.

Simply put, if you have an idea for a mod, the best way to get it done is to start with it small and make it yourself with perhaps one or two friends if you want assistance. Indeed, most of the mods in Lancers started this way with just one or two people working on it. Then, as you release it and start getting it 'out there' people will start to pay more attention and those who like what you have done as a starter will be more willing to come forward and help, enabling the mod to grow and develop further. This is a more guaranteed way of getting yourself noticed and getting people to help then posts like these where people go 'hey! I have an idea, lets do x, y and z and oh, we will need people to cover the 20 odd areas that I don't know.' If I read something like this, I'm less likely to want to get involved then say in a mod where I have seen it has been successful and is popular and people have already put a lot of work into it or a mod based on my own ideas and story lines.

Simply put, start small and build from that. As you build and show people that you are willing to put the work in to make this the mod you want, more people will be drawn to the mod.

Hi pwn4ge!

I think you expect too much. As Aldebaran28 has said already, most of the modders are already working on a mod and therefore do not have the time to mod. I'm also working on my own mod (The Monkeys) and I started it alone and I'm still the only one working on it. Of course I'm therefore quite slow and cannot include everything (e.g.: I have to get models of others, as I'm only good at ini-coding and cannot model any new ships or stations). But on the other hand I can make the mod totally in the way I want it and do not have to be in time or something like this, as the whole mod is finished when I'm finished. So, there aren't only bad things in creating a mod without a team. At least at the beginning this is really good, as you need to learn everything and cannot just learn one thing like system building without knowing anything about implementing new ships into the mod. I learned almost everything about ini coding and can do most of the things I want to have in my mod now. Sometimes I'm even able to add totally new ideas of my own and this is only, because I started all of this alone.

For your last post: Of course it would be nice if someone like me would just post something like: "I really like your ideas and hope you will find some team members. Unfortunately I'm already working on a mod and therefore cannot help you. Good luck anyway!"
Of course this would be nice, but I once did this and the guy asking for help just posted back.
Thanks, but please do not post if you cannot really help with the mod. Therefore I do not post things like that anymore. Only sometimes, if there is a new unique mod idea (like Warriors of the Sky) I post this just to show, that I really like ones ideas. And of course the mod makers are quite busy normally and most times do not even have enough time to work on their own projects. Therefore they just answer the questions in the General editing forum, as they can do this quite fast and can also really help others with this and not just tell them that they like their ideas.

I hope you may understand now, why it is so hard to find someone, who can help you. I also got a thread here in the forum and I would not have found someone, if I did not help someone else some time ago, who is now helping me.

Good luck anyway and just try to start. Maybe you will find someone soon, or after you released a first version of your mod (It does not have to be so large and perfect)!!!
