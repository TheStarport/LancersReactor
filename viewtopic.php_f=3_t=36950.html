Boscoe

Sat Jan 01, 2005 4:13 am by Boscoe

Play it like a strategy game building your reputation with differing factions. Try all the mods. Explore systems way outside of the trade lanes, you'll never know what you'll find. (use the map's "patrol route" and check all three types, friendly, neutral, and hostile. The expand when you hit waypoints and bring you to places and stations and jump holes that you didn't know existed. Get a freighter and try making through hostile territory. Run Cardamime and ditch the police when they scan your cargo bay.
I could go on, the game is all up to your imagination. Every game will eventually get to the point where you've "seen it all", but there's a long way to go with this one before you get there.
Mod suggestions: Tie Universe, TNG, WTS Worlds (multiplayer only), Rebalance Mod, Massive Mod, All Good Things. Check our downloads section on the front page and set it to show "type" of file, you'll find the mods. If you need help with any, or how to install them, go to the "Using Mods Help forum" or the "Mod Announcement forum". The mods will increase your playablility interest, add new ships, worlds, make ships faster, and increase the difficulty of the game at times.
I always consider the end of the storyline missions the "beginning" of the game.

Boscoe

Sun Jan 02, 2005 5:02 pm by Boscoe

JJ, that's a massive request, and would mean that one would have to create animations and additional artwork for the game. Basically, don't expect anyone to take on a project like that, even if they could do it.
The only time I even play FL now is to check out a new mod, or help Reynen with the TNG testing. All games have their limits, just that FL has a much more open ended structure and leaves the gameplay after the storyline up to the individual player. Think of how many games have their storyline missions, and when they're done, you're done. This is what they were avoiding and was Chris Roberts original idea in the development of the game. But, alas, even FL can get redundant after a whole hell of a lot of hours (assuming you've really tried to explore, trade, take missions, change factions etc). It took a very long time for me to get to the point in the unmodded game to feel "I'd seen it all". Thank God for the Mod Makers and their hard work to keep the game fresh.
