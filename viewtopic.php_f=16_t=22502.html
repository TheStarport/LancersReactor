Competition: FLDQ - Freelancer Done Quick

In honour of T-Hawk releasing his mod Campaign Cut Scenes Disabled and perpetuating the tradition of QDQ (Quake Done Quick) I propose an ongoing competition for doing the campaign as fast as possible. In order not to penalize people who don't like mods I propose to do this in two classes: (U) for Unmodded game and (T) for T-Hawk's quick mode.

And in order to make things more interesting and to put participants in a nice quandary I propose to keep separate score as per number of battleships killed. It is one thing to go through the campaign quickly but it is quite a different thing to kill cruisers and battleships with a Starflier and a couple Justice Mk I on a budget of $4 ...

I would like contestants to post a screenie with the player info screen from after the end of the campaign, i.e. after the talk with the guy who tells you to enjoy your vacation. Please post your screenies as JPEGs with less than 100 KBytes and at most 600x450 pixels dimension, in order to keep this thread readable & enjoyable for all. I will keep scores per category in this post. However, in order to keep the volume on manageable levels, people will be listed at most once in each category and they must beat their own entries in the same and higher categories by at least 5 minutes (i.e., if you have an entry with 4:15 in category T4 then new entries for T0..T4 must be 4:10 or faster).

Also, any discussion of insights gained, newsworthy experiences and plain whacky stuff related to the blitz campaigns are most welcome. And applicants for a title must divulge their secrets and tricks if asked, in order to level the playing field. Of course, they may do so on their own volition ...

For background information and helpful (or at least interesting (or at least outlandish)) tips, please refer to the following threads:
Level 7
Battleship kills that do get counted

Starflier campaign
Rhino Campaign

I have also created a topic where detailed plans ('you need two pieces of scrap metal, a burnt match ...') can be discussed, because posting them here could spoil the fun for people who want to come up with their own plans:
Freelancer Done Quick - hints and partial walk-through
