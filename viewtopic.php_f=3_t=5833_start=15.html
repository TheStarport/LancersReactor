Bhurak Starkiller

Wed Jan 29, 2003 12:00 am by Bhurak Starkiller

Here's the basic problem with many of the new people coming here:

Many of you are skeptical about Freelancer. This is understandable. I, too, was a major skeptic and spent much time arguing, for instance, why the game should include the option for joystick, among other things.

Thing is, it's unreasonable for so many of you to come here without having done about as much research as you can, reading many of the previews available, etc., and then demand that we compile all the information for you, or try to show you why your concerns are unjustified. We've done this many times for many people, and more important, we've successfully convinced most or all of the skeptics who've come here, myself included.

Perhaps what we should do is compile a FAQ addressing all the major concerns over Freelancer, but in the meantime, as Freelancer gets closer to launch, many more people are going to come here and start wailing their concerns. As much as I or the rest of us would like to help people out, I'm simply not going to continue doing this, particularly on a per-person basis, and I'm definitely not going to do it for people who get their information wrong and start yelling at us for it (none of us support the exclusion of 3-D cockpits, as Tach pointed out).

Yes, threads do get closed here, and I don't think they always get closed for a good reason. Nevertheless, pining over joystick support has not been well tolerated because, frankly, it just ain't gonna happen. Joystick fanatics represent too small a community, and the game is so close to completion as to make any talk of putting in joystick support futile. If you really need to see some comments about it, search the forums. The most recent thread was especially good in terms of laying down that issue.

But really, the most I can say to anyone who's skeptical of Freelancer is, just keep an open mind. All of us have been waiting a long time to see what DA has come up with, why not have a good (or at least civil) time doing it?
