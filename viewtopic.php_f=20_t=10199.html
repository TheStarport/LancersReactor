Marco

Sun Mar 16, 2003 6:13 am by Marco

My Freelancer crashed inexplicably, and very harshly (forced to hard-reset), for a while, until I realized that my virtual memory was eating up all my space on my XP partition. So I freed up a ton of space and moved the paging file to another drive, and now it never crashes.

So if you notice you're low on disk space, then try freeing some up. If you still get crashes, you'll have to let us know the rest of your system's specs to better diagnose. Do you have the latest video drivers for your video card?

guptasa1

Sun Mar 16, 2003 8:43 am by guptasa1

This may be overly obvious, but I had one crash so far when I had other programs running in the background and was just messing around in the universe. Besides that, not a single crash, and I just follow a simple rule of thumb - every time I'm going to seriously play (running XP), I reboot my machine. As soon as it boots up, the only thing I do is disable my virus scanner (for better performance) and then jump right in again. I also don't have many programs starting up with my system. Not trying to be overly simplistic here, but if you haven't tried that, give it a shot. I do think it can make a big difference (from even shutting down programs and THEN running the game). Also make sure you have the latest drivers for all your hardware, DirectX 9, and all that business. Good luck!
