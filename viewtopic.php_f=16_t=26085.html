T-hawk

Wed Nov 19, 2003 8:25 pm by T-hawk

"Anubi" makes no sense. "-us", not "-is", pluralizes to "-i".

"Anubii" makes absolutely no sense unless the original word is "Anubius". You can't get an "-ii" unless there's already an additional "i" in the word (as in "radius" ).

It would be "Anubes", pronounced "a new bees". This is if one insists on applying a Roman Latin pluralization rule to an Egyptian proper name used in an English context, which any linguist would tell you is silly.

There is absolutely nothing wrong with "Anubises".
