Okee dokey.

I generally use a large monitor and the mouse set to move quickly, in so doing I pick up the mouse and skim it across the mouse mat. This gets me all confused as to where the cursor is on the screen and where the ship is going. I also don't move my hand much when moving the mouse in normal Windows mode and in the lazier games like RTS and RPGs, I just move the mouse with my fingers. If you have to move the mouse all over the screen for minutes on end chasing some bad guys then it becomes a real pain (and literally too).

If I desensitise the mouse it doesn't move around so much and I can't keep up with the dad guys (or good guys - depends on how I'm feeling).

In all WC and Privateer games I used to hang on the joystick and it's throttle constantly adjusting the speed. Without a throttle I can't work out where the velocity is set. I therefore keep bumbing in to things, like missiles!

The right mouse button feels so unnatural it's hard to keep thinking I have to press it to shoot.

After only a short while of frantic movement and shooting I can feel my wrist getting sore (I wonder if M$ will give me some compensation if I get RSI).

Why would it be such a big deal to have mouse and Joystick control? What possible advantage is there in leaving the joystick out. Fine, leave the mouse in and tell everyone it's a better control method but why on earth not have both?
