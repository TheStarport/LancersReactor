Chips

Mon Jan 10, 2005 10:00 am by Chips

I would ensure that Direct X is the newest stable one you can have (9.0b or c) first.

You mention you updated your graphics card - but don't say if it used to be stable before (with your old one)...which would give a large clue as to what the problem is...
Are the graphics drivers are the most modern possible, have you tried re-updating those, and then applying for some help from them too? What graphics card are you running etc...as strange issues like this can be from graphics cards/drivers issues... If its a radeon card, they have a fairly comprehensive sort of help thing going on - where they will email you with issues to try to fix it (although this appears to be a generic email for any problem involving their cards and games!).

Actually - what card are you running, and also how powerful is your PSU (power supply unit). It could be that you don't have enough ampage on the '12v rail' as its called, causing it to crash. Have you also checked out the graphics card manufacturers site for support on their forums? Although you may not get employed 'techies' helping out, you do get some exceesively knowledgeable people who will give suggestions.
