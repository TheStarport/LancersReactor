depends on what you're after. IMO the best route to go is to have the highest damage output in you highest class slots, and fill in the rest with a nice complimentary weapon (same speed, maybe refire).

"The List" is coming along, which will include every gun and turret, and hopefully all the purchase locations. Shields may get added, but not likely till after the initial framework is completed. I don't see the point in listing shields just to compare capacity/recharge, for the same reason I don't plan on doing mines and missiles.. they're universal (to factions), and have a linear class progression.. it's not hard to see that a 4,000 credit mine does less damage than a 7,000 credit mine (ammo). There's not going to be a level 4 mine that works better than a lvl 5. The same applies to shields. The only differences I could lay out is the effectiveness of a Class 7 Graviton vs. a Class 7 Positron against a certain faction.. which I will admit might be handy, but the game tends to balance that on it's own.. e.g. The Corsair level 10 is tuned to take less damage from Outcast weapons, and to get the Corsair 10, there's a good chance you're not worried about Corsair weapons fire (which is effective vs. Graviton shields), and that you've likely not played nice with the Outcasts. So basically, you almost automatically get the best shield for your situation.

Im speaking in broad terms here, so before someone goes fLaming me over specific situations where my logic may not apply, assume that I already understand. And I may have gotten the shield types backwards, feel free to fLame me on that if I did (I should know better).

However, as I said, the Shields will likely be added, but only for the sake of the purchase locations. And you'll more than likely be able to view them sorted by capacity and whatnot, so I assume my arguement is only for the sake of order.. In other words, you'll see the Guns/Turrets Full listing before shields or mines. But once that's accomplished, it should be pretty simple to add all the other stuff.

I agree with the Sadist. Damage per second does not indicate effectiveness. Ever try hitting something with a bunch of lavablades? When you do, the effects are gratifying, as a big chunk of hull disappears -- but try hitting Kusari Drakes or other quick ships with them and you will be cursing loudly.

Which brings me to another point. Once you get below 700 m/s, there are few guns to match with. Only the Barrager and a few others have a quick refire at these sluggish speeds, and accuracy beyond 200 meters is, well, non-existent. At 500 m/s you're projectile takes more than a full SECOND to reach maximum range.

It makes me very sad that there are no 8.33 guns that I can match with my specials or nomads. Sometimes when the enemy has but a sliver of hull left, I am frustrated at firing huge, slow cannons after him hoping to get that last hit... if I could mount a Skyblast with my nomads and still be accurate this problem would be solved... but then I end up missing with the nomads...

I believe there is a class 6 Kusari gun that has 8.33 at 600 m/s -- but wasting a class 9 or 10 slot on that pea-shooter seems like a losing proposition.
