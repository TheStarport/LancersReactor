Browclops

Thu Jul 10, 2003 10:39 pm by Browclops

Wow. And to think, 3 months ago, as I gazed dreamily at the statistics 1/30 and flew alone, I wondered will anyone ever show up on my server? It seems I need to say something, anything . . . Freakzilla is right, I have giggled, even cackled once or twice as I read these. The passion is fascinating, and understandable. Some of you folks have been on Browclops for literally months, working on the same character or characters without interruption. The player behind Bits has, through time and effort, proven himself to be dependable and trustworthy, and willing to put in the effort regarding the stability of the server. He was a bit of a tight ass to begin with (I got nuthin' but love for ya Bits) but has loosened up considerably over time. His 'server police' character is just that, financed originally by myself and later on his own. He is the only person I have directly asked to monitor the server, if he is inclined, when I am not there. Regulator is correct in my selecting him as another person to monitor the server in the same regard, but I have yet to talk directly with him and finalize this. However, I am also in close communication with Argonus of the {AR} clan, and I have no doubt that their presence also helps to deter cheaters, modders and/or clans that are not interested in roleplay, but rather band together simply to control some aspect of the game. I have always been purposely ambiguous in server rules, and this has served my purposes well for the most part. I have welcomed anyone, clan or otherwise, to visit Browclops and stay, if it suits them. I have also purposely stayed out of the clan agendas, meetings, etc., for I do not see that as a role I need to take on. My job has always been to provide the most stable server I can, with a little extra something. Thus were the events born, one at a time, through trial and error. Some worked well, some didn't. But the successes have been noted, and built upon by others. It is true that I personally do not lead as many events as I used to, and the reasoning is two-fold. 1) I actually do work for a living, and employment is something I wish to retain for the time being and 2) others have taken up the mantle and are providing events on their own. Now I'm losing my train of thought . . .
I will not deny that I enjoy some upheaval, the quote of me was true and exact. Months ago I created a character (since deleted) that claimed all of Kusari space for himself, and attacked anyone who entered. It created quite a stir, this madman in a Dragon who died quite often. There was some threat to others, but I died more often than not, and I think the excitement was well worth it. It is within this framework that I see the clans forming now. I certainly hope so, at least. If it turns out to be a negative factor, then adjustments will be made. In any event, this game has held my attention far longer than I expected, and in a year no one will even remember this server existed. The only demand I make of anyone on the server is to have fun, and keep it reasonable.

Browclops
