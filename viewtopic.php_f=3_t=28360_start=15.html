Yumiko

Sun Feb 22, 2004 12:30 am by Yumiko

Weapons that hurt that type of shield the most do 20% MORE damage, and weapons that hurt that type of shield the least do 20% LESS


So the level 8 Adv. Protector shield (5890 HP, strong against laser/photon pulse) can be better than the level 10 Adv. Brigadine shield (8280 HP, weak against laser/photon/pulse).

If someone shot you with 7000 laser damage, the Protector would take 5600 damage and have 290 HP left.

But the Brigadine would take 8400 damage and would fail.

Also, since I think shield batteries restore a constant number of HP, your shield batteries will last longer if you're using a shield that's strong against your enemies.

So, it seems that if you're always fighting enemies who use laser/photon/pulse weapons (if you take the highest paying missions, you'd probably be fighting powerful Bounty Hunters VHFs, and they use those weapons), the level 8 Adv. Protector shield would be better.

Except that the C10 shield has a higher recharge rate then the C8 graviton shield.


The Adv. Brigadine H. F. Shield has 8280 capacity and 184 regeneration. 184/8280 = 2.22%

The Adv. Protector H. F. Shield has 5890 capacity and 130 regeneration. 130/5890 = 2.22%

Both of them have the same percent regeneration rate, so in the scenario I laid out above, the C10's higher recharge rate won't make it any better. Shield type advantages benefit the regeneration rate to the same proportion as they benefit the capacity.
