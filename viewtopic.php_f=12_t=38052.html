Try "HardWar" ... named game of the year back around 1999. I've see it offered on eBay for next to nothing now. Instead of in space, you are on one of the moons of Jupiter, where you're ancestors colonised but got cut off from earth centuries ago. There is a cool mystery, plus you fly different ships -- as a bounty hunter, trader, salvager or pirate. You make enough money to buy your own base too. You have missions to fly, as in Freelancer.

Unfortunately, there are not many games that give you freedom like FL around now. Most people just seem to liek the types where you are told waht to do ... and do it, nothing more.

p.s. There are a couple of German developed games of the FL type, but they are dreadfully slow. Takes forever to build up enough credits trading to get into the interesting things. Beautiful graphics though. Can't think of the name right now. somebody else remember?

Apart from some MMO's, theer are 2 games I reccomend:
X2: The Threat: Very open-ended, can get capitall ships, fly fighetrs, build corporations, purchase space stations. However, there are like 10000 hotkeys and its hard to get use to.
Nexus: The Jupiter Incident: It has an intriguing storyline, however it is strategy. You command a flagship, and later a couple of other ships. Before each mission, you outfit them with new weps., equipment, and fighter wings, but only if you have enough "resource points" to do so. Their crews will also level up. There are plenty of missions, a couple very frustrating (if you need help, just check the offical forums for that game), but mostly enjoyable. Memorabel charcters too. ALso, you don't build ships, you just use and upgrade them (if you lose 1, it will be replaced the msiion after the next mission), and you have some temp. ones.
Sorry about all this gushing, but Nexus: TJI is the only recent space sim I truly enjoy other than FL 9X2 had too amny hotkeys, but maybe I will get back to it).
