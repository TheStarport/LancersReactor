On the war rules thing, can you keep the discussion here, or in a new thread, I'm sure most people will have some input, and will be able to find flaws in some of your thinking.

Another alternative to the 5 minute rules, is:
If it is clearly obvious that the remaining player(s) for one side are evading more than they are attacking, it should be considered that they are fleeing the battle, and are thus no longer considered in determining the winner.

This would account for the situation where there are 2 or 3 light fighters taking on 2 or 3 heavy fighters, there may be no death for 5 minutes, but the outcome is not truely clear (so the battle should continue on past 5 minutes), I would wager on the light fighters if they co-ordinated their attacks. However if it was one or two light fighters against 4 or 5 heavy fighters, then the light fighters would be evading more than attacking, and I think the outcome would be obvious (99.7% of the time). And similarly if it was 4 or 5 light fighters against one or two heavy fighters.

Also, this rule encourages continuous confrontation.

Here's my suggestion for battle rules. If I repeat some of the one's previously mentioned, sorry, just consider it being your idea, I don't mind. We're all here for the fun of it.

1: There are two kinds of battles, Major Battles and Minor Battles. Major Battles can incorporate entire star systems as a disputed territory but require scheduling that the leaders of the involved parties can agree upon. Minor Battles can be declared on the spot with a Console message (or equivelant, such as informing the members of the other side currently on) for bases, Trade Lanes, areas, and Jump locations. A side can only declare three Minor Battles in one day. Major Battles do not have this limit.

2: Everything goes as far as fighting is concerned. Gang banging, sneak attacks, batts/nanos, etc. are all legal. Rules of engagment can be made if each side agrees upon them, but these are by no means enforced except by the two teams. Other rules, such as a limit to the amount of times players can die before being defeated, are left to the two initial sides.

3: Other factions and players can join a battle at any point. To do so, they must inform the involved sides of their involvment prior to participation and adhere by any special rules, such as limited deaths. Their motivations, or what side they take, need not be made readily apparent as you may choose any side currently involved or simply be your own side.

3: A victory is declared when all but one of the contenders admit defeat or lose all their lives. At this point, the remaining team need only secure the contested area for a span of Fifteen Minutes. If no one else arrives in the area to contest it, the battle is declared over and a victory for the current possessor.
