I defeated them all, single handedly, while also being ravaged by the radiation field. Best fighter in the game, I say. He died twice, didn't kill anyone. The bigger the ship, the easier my job gets for staying on their but and drilling through their hull. You might think it takes longer to kill a ship with fewer, weaker guns, but my ability to keep a target consistently in the crosshairs really evens the playing field.

Anik

Thu Mar 27, 2003 8:11 pm by Anik

I too, am fond of the drake. While I've enjoyed flying much larger ships, and sometimes delight in the awesome firepower of a heavier vessel, the drake's nimble performance, decent firepower and seductive good looks suit my play style more than anything else I've tried so far. I loved finishing SP in one, and as I continue to explore the level 38 universe, I continue to enjoy the edge-of-your-seat experience that the drake offers. Flying the drake is like dancing on the edge of a knife - always a mistake away from death, always in motion. Of course, the great thing about Freelancer is the fact that there are tons of 'best' ships out there - I still haven't tried out the Dromedary, and it's likely that I'll love that one too. Its refreshing that the game doesn't force people into a very few 'best' ship choices in order to complete the SP, or to stay competitive in higher level play.
