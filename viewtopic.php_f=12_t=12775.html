I'd probably stay the h3ll away from the past AND future and just live in the present. If time is linear, then if I change the past I won't that the event actually happened. If I didn't know that the event actually happened I wouldn't have gone into the past to change it. If I didn't change the past then the past would still be the past and I'd go back and change it...ad infinitum. You're stuck in a paradox. Okay, so onto the future. Again, if time is linear: you go into the future read up on some events that have occured (say a lotto number that x person won with). So now you know that person x won with numbers 6-43-23-15-3. If you go back to your own time and get those numbers then the knowledge that you had of person x having those numbers might get erased because you never saw those name/number pair. Because of that, you wouldn't know the numbers to begin with. Sure, you could go back to the past and plainly observe events as they happen (and I don't doubt that that would be most informative) but it would all be a gamble. If you were discovered it could be utterly tragic. Firstly, you would probably not have the social etiquette of the time period and so you would most likely not survive in the social environment. And the future...well, there's nothing worse than being teased with knowledge that you could never use. Of course, if time isn't linear then all of my arguments are inconsequential...but there is a 50% chance that it is linear (last I checked there were only those two options). I'll leave the future to the future, and the past to the past. Hope this post made sense. Cheers.
