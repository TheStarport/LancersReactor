I agree with regard to the wasted opportunity ... Just think back to the variety of missions in the Privateer and Wing Commander series.

And let's not forget mission objectives. In Privateer and Wing Commander, if you were told to protect a ship and you let yourself get lured away by a bogie by staying on his six then the other baddies would shred the freighter in the meantime and you failed the mission.

In Freelancer, if you get told to take out some gunboats you may actually do it because the loot tends to be good (i.e., you'd have done it anyway) but if you get told to take out some heavy bombers you target the nearest enemy fighter instead regardless of its type, because it doesn't make any difference.

In fact, you can solve many story missions just by being there and not dying; there are pretty few missions where you actually have to take out something.
