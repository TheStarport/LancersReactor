Wanderer

Wed Apr 14, 2004 2:27 am by Wanderer

Coming back here on a lark i saw this post. Maybe nobody remembers (since its been a long time past its release), but i've got the perfect base designed.

Name: Citadel
Q1: Massive central structure with 4 external substations connected by pylons and 1 ring connecting all substations. Substations each have 1 capital ship sized docking bay and docking bays for smaller craft on the sides. The central structure has docking bays for fighter sized craft only. Size wise, the station was so large it needed 5 sur files from asteroid bases for the central station and its 5 substations and its still not large enough.

Q2: With its capabilities, this station should have been totally self-sufficient. As such, i would have it placed somewhere very far out of the way. Something along the lines of hidden in a desolate system with no trade lanes leading to it. Mines or gas clouds would encircle the approach to the station making it only possible to reach by the most tenacious.

Q3: Security wise, this station was designed with enough firepower to smash entire fleets. A total of 8 asteroid base sized siege cannons (2 mounted on each substation 1 above and 1 on the underside) with a maximum range of 30 km and a splash radius of 2 kilometers ensures a hot welcome to any interloper. And if fast movers avoid the long range volleys of the siege cannons, over a 100 fast tracking weapons platforms firing AA, Torpedoes, high maneuverbility missiles and anti-capital ship energy bolts protect the immediate vincinity of the base.

Q4: Hidden behind such dangerous territory, patrols that pass that area are rare. However, there are regular outgoing patrols from the station itself and a support fleet of capital ships is permanently on standy near it.

Q5: Given the drastic departure of the station design from any existing house design, i figured that the station itself should have been built by the occupants of another sleeper ship that was never accounted for. The back story would have the ship being unable to find a habitable planet but instead landing in a resource rich system. Oxygen gas clouds and ice asteroids meant that the sleepers would have little worry about air and water. The resource rich asteroids there allowed for the construction of agricultural domes on the largest asteroid in that system which supplemented their dwindling food supplies.

As time went by, development continued on the asteroid until a time came when it was decided that an outer shell should be constructed over it and pressurised. This was the beginning of the concept behind the Citadel. Eventually, expansion reached a level that the Citadel of today came into being and the current barren state of the system was achieved. This particular house has never had a planetary hold and as such, the Citadel has come to be their capital.

Odd to think though, that hardly anyone has designed a custom station model in a long time as far as i know.
