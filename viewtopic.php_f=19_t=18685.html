Regardless of who they were, I thought that was pretty lame..

From henceforth, I kept myself aware of this, hoping it never happened to me. I plotted my trade runs and my exploration AROUND systems where people were congregating. I used the chat feature as a sort of "radar," effectively allowing me to avoid trouble. I am a level 20something, and probably ripe for the picking from people like you and your squad.

Anyway, back to my point.. Many of us are sick and tired of you... and your dock camping antics. Yes, I was being camped at magellan by one of the NEOs. No, i've never been camp killed before. Do I care if I'm on your hit list? No. Will I shoot at you if i see you? No. If you are NEOfied, maybe. But you know.. I'd rather just play this game and explore... I prefer getting a group of friends together and trading/charting new systems/doing missions.

Something I've done lately, that's a lot of fun... is giving newbies my own made-up missions.. whether it's trading or flying waypoints.. and then giving them gobs of money upon completion. This really adds a new dimension of play, making this game more of a freelancing kind of atmosphere than before. For example, I recently took a new player with me from Manhattan to Tau-29 as an "escort", and paid him, purchased new ships and equipment for him, and taught him a few tricks on the way. He went from level 1 to level 12 during this run which took about 30-45 minutes. Did I earn any cash for it ? nO. I was carrying cargo in my freighter, and was able to reimburse myself with the trade from that. Did I do this selfishly ? nah, I did it because I enjoy helping people. I like to make this game fun for myself and others. Here's an idea for some of you: making friends with the noobs will ensure your survival in the future, if some day this noob becomes an evil pirate.. he will remember you and spare your life. Playing nice helps secure your life down the line as well..

Well anyway, that's all for now.
