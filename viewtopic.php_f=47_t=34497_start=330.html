Story of the Battle of Leeds:
The battle opens with the BHG on the South side of Leeds, and the Hispanics on the North side. The House Forces are on the dark (Southeast) side of Leeds, building the RH base. The first volley is only between the Hispanics and the BHG. It is at long range. There are not a lot of hits, and the BHG gets more. Then, the RH Biowep is fired at the planet, and the House fleets join the BHG before the next salvo. The fleets are closing distance, and this one is at medium range. Fighters get into a dogfight in between the fleets. This salvo is disastrous for the Hispanics. The high powered Axis weapons tear through their capital ships. All Capital ships are destroyed for the HA. However, the Axis takes heavy losses as well. Their fleet is hit hard, with many, many battleships and warships getting destroyed. The fighter battle is very, very intense. However, the Axis fighters and cruisers manage to barely hold off the bombers, but losses are very high. The remaining Hispanic fighters flee back towards the remaining ships in their fleet. The cruisers and support ships charge the Axis fleet to assist the fighters, but by the time they arrive, the remaining HA fighters are retreating. The cruisers make a 180 degree turn, because they cannot fight the Axis capital ships. As they run, the Axis cap ships get three good salvos at them, and many of them are killed. The remaining HA fleet retreates to Tau-31.
