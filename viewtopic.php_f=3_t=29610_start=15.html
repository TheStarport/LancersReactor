Midaus

Tue Mar 23, 2004 10:44 pm by Midaus

I sometimes use the 1st person mode, but not usually in combat, but to just break up the monotany or get the cruise engine glare out of my eyes for awhile.
I really like the way the cockpit view draws you right into the combat and it's more intense with the lasers going at eye level and enemy ships streaking right by you flaming and losing parts...heh and going boom. The problem in first person mode is while it brings you into the battle from a certain point it also severely limits your depth perception and the sense of where you are in relation to the other ships. I think the third person mode gives you this sense closer to the reality of being in a ships cockpit because you have some idea of whats beside you. In a real cockpit all you'd have to do is turn your head or look over your shoulder. In the FL cockpits you can't do this. If they pulled the camera back into the ship a bit, just a bit and did better work on the cockpits I think that would work to give more of a genuine feeling. In the FL Cockpit mode I feel like I've got my nose pressed against the windshield, or I'm trying to watch television from about two inches away from the screen. BTW. I think the best looking cockpit is that Britonian Light Fighter, what is that called, the Cavalier?
