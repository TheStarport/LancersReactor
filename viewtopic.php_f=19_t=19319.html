Yes you can play MP behind a router. You may have to forward a few ports tho. Also i have heard about software firewalls (usually bundled with routers) causing problems so you may need to switch that off.

SP is good to get you into the game, hone your skills a bit. But it has a fairly short playtime. Once you've completed the story you've got 20-40 hrs max before you're totally bored. Although this can be greatly extended by soem of the mods on offer.

MP is better once you are tired of SP. You've gotta be pretty competent to survive at all and it is abotu 100x harder fighting decent humans instead of AI. The opportunities for co-op ing etc mean you can get totally hooked on MP - theres always soemthing to prove

Yes, it will work fine over a router. Some NAT routers need the DirectPlay ports opening or forwarding to the machine on which you want to play the game but most decent domestic routers are designed to cope with this kind of application and won't need any config.

SP is a good intro and lets you learn the game in a "safe" environment and you might enjoy the missions and storyline. Once you've completed them it gets boring very quickly tho. I much prefer MP - it's still not entirely open ended but the human interaction makes it more interesting.
