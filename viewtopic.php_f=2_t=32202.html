I'm afraid your very wrong there with your facts.
Before the advent of Nuclear Submarines, the Germans, British, Americans, Japanese all used countermeasures during World War 2. They were tiny strips of metal foil called 'Chaff' that were released by arial Bombers to confuse enemy ground Radar signal returns.
The British, who led the field at the time, also experimented in WW2 with Electronic Countermeasures (ECM) which consisted of electromagnetic pulses (EMP) to scramble ground Radar and Night interceptors target acqusition gear.
