Puke Barwalker

Wed Aug 20, 2003 1:28 am by Puke Barwalker

When somebody tells you where a wreck is, they give you a large sector ... e.g. 4C. You have to fly back and forth in that sector until the wreck shows up on your scanner. (It will appear as white, since it is neutral. Make sure you have "show all" clicked.) It also helps to look at the navmap. The wrecks show up as a red X on the map, if you have passed within scanner range of them. Sometimes it can be a pain in the butt finding them, especially when you hear that "warning -- scanner efficiency effected" message. Some people have posted that they like to fly an X pattern, others a Z or whatever. When you are told say 4C, the wreck might even be just barely within that sector ... right on the edge of it. Wrecks aren't so easy to find, but some can be quite profitable!

As a tip, wrecks are alway in asteroid belts, debris fields, ice clouds etc -- places they are NOT easy to see. Otherwise, someone would have found them before you. Worst of all are wrecks in radiation fields. Then you have to find them while the radiation is eating up our hull and you are using up all of your nanaobots.

Your searcg pattern isnt good enough to pick them up in your hud.

Set a waypoint at one of the corners of the sector that the wreck is in. Set your next waypoint at the corner diagonally opposite the one you just made. Set the third one on one of hte other corners, and a fourth at the corner diagonally opposite that, so your search pattern looks like an X.

Flip your hud display to show all contacts, and then start flying. You'll find em.

PS - also look on your map for a little red X that will mark the spot.
