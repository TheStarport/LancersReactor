Arms Dealer?

One thing that I like about MP Freelancer is the ability to trade cargo and equipment between players without going through a commodity trader. Some of the most lucrative deals that I have ever made have been in the sale of Level 10 weaponry to those who want it. I can usually get more than what the NPC salesmen will give me. Since I hate wreck-camping and waiting for a reboot, my opportunities for selling SILVER FIRE or DIAMONDBACK weapons are few, but Nomad cannons are in plentiful supply for the intrepid.

Which brings me to this question:
Is there enough demand for Nomad guns to justify making a career out of harvesting them or does everybody who wants them have them already? For example, I have 4 Nomad blasters that I put in my cargo bay when I switched to the cannons. Should I try to sell them on the player market or just settle for the paltry sum the NPC's will give me?

What I'm looking for here is economic tips. I haven't been playing for quite long enough to get a feel for the dynamics of the MP Freelancer universe. Arms dealer sounds like an interesting Freelancer career, but only if there is a demand for the product.

Wed May 14, 2003 12:42 am by rabidrobot

I haven't had any trouble selling guns on Elite. I sold two SILVER FIREs for $150k; more than I could get selling to NPCs, but less than they would cost if I tried to buy them back from NPC. I don't know what Holliday is talking about when he says "worth." Is this what NPCs will buy them for or sell them for? Or some average? And who does the server consider is cheating? The seller? The buyer? Both? In short, I don't think it is actually cheating. The problem with being a full time arms dealer is demand. Very few of the somewhat limited CAPNAME guns are more useful than the essentially unlimited Nomad guns, and anyone able to equip class 10 guns is capable of killing a few Nomads themselves. And there are no repeat customers. Once someone's got the weapons they want, they've got them. You may have better, if less profitable, luck selling midrange guns to developing players. For example, someone freighting goods doesn't feel safe going into hostile territory to get that better turret (or shields!), or maybe doesn't have a good enough rep to buy it themselves. You could charge a reasonable finders/delivery fee.
