Argh

Tue May 10, 2005 1:33 pm by Argh

Um... try exporting your ship from 3DS in different formats, and then importing into MS3D. OBJ is my preferred format, since if you're going to work with UVMapper Classic, that's what you need to be in anyhow.

And if anything's wrong with your model, MS3D may crash. It's a lot more sensitive about things like zero-length tris, concave objects, etc. than most modeling environments. This actually a good thing, because most game engines can't handle bad geometry either.

Superspider,

Are all your gruops in the correct order, largest group 1st and (in this case) named seraphim, followed by the groups in size descending order. Exporting a ship without hardpoints can cause milkshape to throw an error, even if using hardcmp I always export with Hp/Fixed/HpMount, as a minimum.

Though I am curious... What's so hard about creating hardpoints in milkshape? They are simply three vertex's arranged in a triangle and connected to make a face.. then for simplicity flattened on the 'y' axis. using that as a template copy, move into position then rename..best thought but not that important if your fixed hardpoints appear before the revolute... as thats how they will appear in the .cmp file.
