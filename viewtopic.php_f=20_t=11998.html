Nog

Sun Mar 30, 2003 9:13 pm by Nog

In Windows XP you can disable the midi in the Audio Devices panel located in the control panel window. or disable the driver directly in the system devices dialog (in all win9x OSes) Just check the box labled "Disable in this profile", or "disable in all profiles", under your driver properties.
Note that doing this will cause any games or apps that use the midi channel to crash so be sure to turn the midi back on if you plan to play any Doom.
Not a problem with most newer games as they use wav or mp3 for music now. No need for a midi channel.

DO NOT disable the Midi if you have Pasport Designs, Encore 4.0 installed. Doing so will cause Encore to rewrite the Midi Driver with it's own (Therby trashing your soundcard drivers)
In this case just change the default Midi output driver to the MPU401 Driver, it will serve the same purpose as disabling the Creative Midi Driver.

The article I linked to above contains detailed instruction on how to disable midi on all OSes if you need to. Refer to my origonal post for the link or the 88th post in the soundfix thread on top of the technical page.
