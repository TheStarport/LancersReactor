Sat Mar 01, 2003 9:02 am by Lemlestarn

The thing with hostiles ignoring other enemies when you come in sight is another remainder that this is more of an arcade shooter than a space sim. I'm not saying that's a bad thing, but you probably shouldn't expect "realistic" behavior from NPCs within an arcade shooter. It's basically designed to create a lot of encounters where you have to shoot things up.

IMHO, it would have been a bit more interesting if the police tried to stop all combat close to the bases, regardless of participants. Unless it was the base itself that was attacked, that is. Having swarms of ships doing combat right outside a base should be considered a danger to the base which the police, I'm guessing, are there to protect.

That way you could choose if you wanted to be a hero and combat the hostiles attacking the base, or be a greedy merchant and sneak of with your cargo while the hostiles are busy fighting the police.
