****************************************************
(Question 1) ------ "What is the Best Ship ?"
****************************************************

Since this is an objective review, I can only give you the facts as they are.Now, most people agree the best ship is one of the three top VHF Fighters. Here I will list the stats of each ship and let you decide for yourself.

Eagle ---- Pros: Fast Turning/Dodging Speed
Cons: Weak Armor, Medium Firepower

Sabre ----- Pros: High Firepower (Due to 360 Degree turret on top)
Cons: Medium Armor, Medium Turning Speed

Titan ------ Pros: High Armor (2000 Extra Shield Power)
Cons: Medium Firepower, Slow Turning Speed

Okay, people who support the Eagle say that it can beat the Titan by dodging it's shots and staying behind it, wearing it down over time. However, it's dodging speed IS lowered considerably when fighting in asteroid fields, but dodging is great in open space combat, and if it can get and stay behind a Titan , it will win.

Titan Supporters say that the Eagle can be taken down easily with a head-on weapons run, which is how most ships approach combat. Due to a lack of a back firing set of turrets however, means that if a Titan dosen't destroy it's target in the head-on run, giving time for the Eagle to get behind the ship, means the Titan is finished unless covered by another ship.

There have been no major arguments for the Sabre, as most people seem to like either of the Extremes (Titan or Eagle). Onto the next question .......

Okay ,Black_Leader,
HammerHead vs Centurion ...
Here are the stats :

Hammerhead

Class: VHF
Gun/Turret Mounts : 6/1
Armor: 7900
Cargo: 70
Max Nanobots/Batteries: 49/49
Max Weapon Class: 9

And the .......

Centurion

Class: Heavy Fighter
Gun/Turrets: 6/0
Armor: 9200
Cargo: 45
Max Nanobots/Batteries: 55/55
Max Weapon Class: 9

Now, these ships are quite similar in stats, but are different enough to warrant different playing styles.

The Hammerhead is a VHF, so it has a bigger cargo compartment. This would be a quite good ship if you were doing some trading off-side while searching for missions. However, since it is a VHF , it is slower turning than the centurion due to it's class, so you would have to watch your tailpipe. Which brings me nicely to my next point. The Hammerhead has an extra turret, which means that if someone is on your tailpipe, you can take a few shots to distract him, and then swing around and go in guns blazing. The turret would also compliment a frontal attack nicely as well , and one class-nine turret can make or break a battle. Onto the Centurion.

The Centurion is a heavy fighter, a class lower than the hammerhead, yet is is on par with it statistically. One thing to note, is that it has no turrents to defend it's tailpipe with, so if someone get's in there, you're finished. However, it has both the upper hand in Defence and Turning Speed, it is a nice ship in it's own right. This ship would be used in missions ,because of it's small cargo hold, it wouldn't be worthwile trying to trade because it would give such a small return (unless hauling diamonds , that is). If the Centurion had the suprise in a attack on a Hammerhead, it could shoot from the side, and be behind it, attacking the engines. However, you would have to be good at dodging turret shots to stay there.

The Hammerhead and Centurion are both fine ships in their own rights, but I would have to lean towards the Hammerhead as the superior ship.

Hope this helped

I'm going to fill in the blank in the arguments for and against the big three.

Most people think of the Sabre as being number 2 over all, as it is less heavily armoured than the Titan, and slower than the eagle, but it makes up for that by being more heavily armoured by the Eagle, and faster than the titan, not to mention packing more guns than either. let's look at it like this: each ship has a speed value, an armour value, and a firepower value, rated in three easy steps; low(1), medium (2), and high (3). these values are compared against the other ships in the "Big Three". it works out as follows

Eagle:
Speed: high (3)
Firepower: medium (2)
Armour: Low (1)

total: 6 points

Titan:
Speed: low (1)
Firepower: Medium (2)
Armour: high (3)

total: 6 points

Sabre:
Speed: medium (2)
Firepower: high (3)
Armour: medium (2)

total: 7 points

purely on the numbers, a sabre works out as the best ship, which is why I use it.

admittedly, the success or failure of a ship will depend on your ability to fly it. if you're a very skilled pilot, then agility is going to be more important to you as a survival tool than armour. if, on the other hand, you think dodging is for wimps, then the Titan is your beast. My personal philosophy is that the best defence is a strong attack. after all, destroyed ships can't shoot back.

in all, I guess the best argument is that the best ship is whichever one you think is best. I'm a staunch defender of the Sabre, but that's mostly preference (plus the fact that I think it looks damn cool).
