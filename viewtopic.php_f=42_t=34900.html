Mon Sep 20, 2004 6:24 am by warzog

Well, in Wing Commander Privateer, a few systems had starfields like Freelancer, but they were disabled because in the more complicated systems, like New Constantinople, the computers of the time slowed down to much, and the game became unplayable.
There was, originally, a way to purchase every ship in the game, and more weapons than in the official release.
Again, the computers of the time couldn't handle as much as was originally planned.

In Privateer 2, The Darkening, it was originally set up so that you could get some ships from Joe the Bartender.
(Like the "Unknown" Talon ship.)
IIRC, it was removed because it required an additional 1.5 CD's, and they wanted to keep costs down, and felt that players didn't want to be swapping disks so often.

IIRC, the Wing Commander Academy was originally supposed to be part of one of the other games, but time, costs, and other considerations required that it be released by itself.

In WCIII, The Heart of the Tiger, there were several missions planned for the base where you refuel on your way to destroy Kilrah.
IIRC, they were removed because they required about 1/5th of another CD, and they didn't want to spend the money on it.

In Wing Commander IV, The Price of Freedom, there was originally a series of missions against the genetically engineered Dragon pilot, but they were removed due to save on the number of CD's required, and that they added little to the game.

Of course, some of the above may just be rumors, but as far as I know, are correct.

One of the main problems with restoring those items to Privateer, and the reason for our mod, is that Privateer is a DOS game.
And, as such, it's already maxed out.
If you did add them, there's no machine in the world that could play it.
In order to restore them, you'd have to convert the entire game to Windows, and then it MIGHT work.
But then, you'd have to come up with some new graphics, and animations.
The Graphics for ship sales, and weapons, were removed.
As were the ship dealer's sales pitch animations.
Not to mention the fact that the voices were never even recorded for the sales pitches for the other ships.
