NukeIt

Thu Mar 29, 2007 11:28 am by NukeIt

This isn't actually going to present a problem for you- in fact, if you keep playing, you should be reaching level 20 very soon. What the game hasn't told you is that after the SP campaign is completed, you are no longer given your current worth to compare with the requirement for the next level- instead, you are shown your current worth and how much more money you need to make before you'll get the next level. The number you're seeing is the difference between where you are and where you want to be- the smaller that number is, the closer you are to reaching your next level.

The "mission" bit is, in fact, a bug, but it has no effect on your ability to level up. You can just ignore it, as it will go away after you've launched and docked once or twice.

NukeIt

Fri Mar 30, 2007 12:25 am by NukeIt

It isn't uncommon to end the SP game and jump a few levels right away- your net worth by the end of the last story mission could potentially be high enough to jump you straight to 38, if you've been busy enough making money during the campaign. It is impossible to end with a level lower than 18, as you are given 18 as part of your reward for completing the mission, but immediately after the last cutscene ends the level restriction that kept you from ascending beyond the SP cap (designed to keep the player using class 6 or lower equipment for the sake of difficulty) is gone as well- so you get everything that the game didn't let you get before defeating the Nomads.

I usually wind up in the mid-twenties after the end of the story missions just from looting any wrecks that I come across during the course of playing the missions or working levels in between missions. You'll only really end at 18 if you haven't done a whole lot besides what the game requires of you.
