Paul_Proteus

Sun Jun 22, 2003 7:16 pm by Paul_Proteus

I didn't realize that so many people were offended by the cloaking device, primarily because it's not my initial reaction - when I get a new toy - to begin coming up with ways to exploit the game with it. For good-natured fliers, the cloaking device provided a means to avoid dock-camping in areas where that is a nuisance. It also allowed players to stlealthily explore systems that would have otherwise been too dangerous for them to enter, which may *sound* like a cheat, but rationally, this has generally been the point of cloaking devices in science-fiction. I know that, in Earth and Beyond, it's an actual skill that's worked into the game world that certain races and classes can master.

I always thought that cloaking technology was probably one of the features that Microsoft dumbed out of the product that Digital Anvil had initially envisioned. It would take some well-thought mod-designing to make it less exploitable.
