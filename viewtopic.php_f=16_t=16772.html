Circumcisor

Tue May 13, 2003 6:34 pm by Circumcisor

Firing rate (8.33, 4 etc) does not affect the targetting reticule.

It's calculated by taking the average of the projectile speeds of your guns, because what's important is the time it takes for the projectile to travel to the target, not the number of projectiles fired per second.

If the turret is activated (green in the weapon list) then it will be included in the calculation. But with an Eagle there isn't much point having a turret anyway.

Crash-Drive

Tue May 13, 2003 11:01 pm by Crash-Drive

The turret projectile velocity is figured into the targetting indicator when using the turret on a Sabre to fire forward along with the other guns. It's ideal to have the turret's projectile speed match the guns for targetting accuracy, but not essential in most opinions I've heard. I suppose that the farther apart the velocities are, the more problems it will cause with the targetting. It's not easy to match the gun speeds with a turret on a Sabre, because most people prefer to use the c10 guns, which have a speed of 600m/s, but no turret has that speed.

So, I don't use c10 guns on a Sabre, I use 6 x Kraken Type 2 and a Salamanca or Adv. Debilitator turret. The Kraken Type 2 does more damage over time than the c10 guns except the Diamondback and Cerberus, with better efficiency, higher projectile speed (harder to dodge), and higher refire rate. This loadout has everything in sync: 700m/s speed, 700m range, 4.0 refire. I use the pulse turret instead of the neutron turret if taking shields down quickly is important. You do have to watch your power, unlike Nomad guns, but it kills very quickly.
