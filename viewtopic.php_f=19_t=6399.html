bRydeR

Thu May 08, 2003 2:57 pm by bRydeR

Human players have no effect on each others reps.

1. Factions will respond to each human player the same when you're grouped as they would when you're flying solo. If you meet a faction with whom you have hostile rep but your grouped buddy is friendly/neutral they will attack ONLY you and show up as red on your target list but green/white for your partner. If he attacks in some kind of foolish gesture of solidarity <g> he'll get the usual "cut it out/friendly fire" dialogue and then they'll turn hostile if he inflicts enough damage. The encounter will have the same effect on his rep as it would had he been flying alone.

2. If you attack another human player NPCs will not take sides, nor is your rep affected, irrespective of the rep of the player you attack.

I've always thought this aspect of MP was a bit pants, especially #2 above... the whole idea of the rep system is that your own actions dictate the way in which the universe responds to you. If you turn Rogue and attack a player who is Navy the whole of Liberty space should still come tumbling down on your head - as it is you can waste a Navy player right in front of a battleship swarming with patrol NPCs and they just turn a blind eye!
