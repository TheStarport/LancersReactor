Sheesh, this seems to have degenerated into another worthless thread, but since I'm a big fan of LOTR, and very un-racist, I need to come to its defense here.

Tolkien himself said that LOTR was meant as an ancient mythology for England, since it had none (Arthur and the knights of the round table is not ancient). Therefore, he made the geographic distribution of people the way it would have been 7-10 thousand years ago. So, people from the south and east would indeed have had darker skin. Tolkien himself was definitely not racist, and he even has a passage where Sam wonders about one of the fallen Easterlings (a "swarthy" man, btw) and wonders if he had a family, friends, and if he was evil, or just pressed into service by an evil ruler.
