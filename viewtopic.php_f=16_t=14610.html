You could change the value in one of the files ina certain game directory... if you look for a value listed as "DIFFICULTY_SCALE" and change it to something lower than it currently is (but not too low)... that might make it easier... don't know if that's what you want... You might consider that to be a cheat... but I totally understand how hard that mission is... it took me more than a few tries to get through it, I think I just got lucky one time and finally got through... make sure if you do use that method that you change it back or it will be easier throughout the rest of the game...

The file should be in this directory...

C:\Documents and Settings\Your Name Here\My Documents\My Games\Freelancer

and look for the file called PerfOptions...

Haenlomal

Wed Apr 23, 2003 4:27 pm by Haenlomal

I recommend taking out the battleship first...there is a rather huge blind spot on all Kusari battleships, and as an added bonus, that spot keeps you pretty well covered from outside shots too. I'll leave you the challenge of finding it. Once you've found the blind spot, you can pretty much just sit there and blast the battleship away to kingdom come. Keep your eyes open, though, just in the off-chance that an enemy fighter comes right into the blind spot looking for you.

The generators are pretty easy to take out after the battleship is gone.

You don't need to take down the battleship. it can barely hit anything anyway.

I used a Hawk, with Starkiller torps, advanced thruster, and the best shield available from Kyoto. My guns were advanced skyrails left over from Bretonia which I never bothered replacing.

I did a series of head-on runs towards each generator beginning at 1500m out and firing torps and guns all the way down to about 200m(at which point the visual slowdown got too bad and I pulled up to avoid crashing). Then I turboed out to 1500, and made a similar run on the next generator. I continued this pattern until the generators went down. In the end, I used a ton of shield batteries but used no nanos at all. The biggest expense was the 30-someodd torps I burned through.
