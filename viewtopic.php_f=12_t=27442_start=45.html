I'll lend you the Keep and Dust Devil if you want, both on PAL-VHS, gotta have em back though. Dust Devil is incredibly rare though. it is disturbing but looks amazing and it's very hypnotic. Suffers from aagin an unfocused script and weak dialogue, apart from Joe's chilling explanations of what the Demon is actually up to and what he is.
"he takes the souls of those who have no hope.. to build his power in the spirit world.." brrrrrrr!

you might also be interested in Alex Cox's film, Walker, which is also transfixing. Fanatstic Peckinpah-esque slo-mo gun battles to the background of burning towns and execution of civilians, often no sound other than a plaintive solo piano repaet on th sharps and flats. Some very silly characters and the politics is rammed down your throat which is annoying, and there's stupid things like cadillacs and helicopters in the 1850s, but otherwise a very intriguing if flawed offering. Ed Harris' best performance I reckon as the insane and ambivalent William Walker, American adventurer, writer, self-appointed Colonel and one-time President of Nicaragua and Man of Destiny (ie loon)

An interesting film for history buffs to watch too, although be prepared for total weirdness. tbis is by the guy who did Repo Man and Sid & Nancy, and got chucked off Fear & Loathing in Las Vegas for being too weird.
