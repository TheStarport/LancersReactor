Hello folks,

I tried the whole roleplaying thing. I became Krenesky of the Russian Mafia, ruthless anarchist and freedom fighter.

And then it dawned on me tonight:

Roleplaying in Freelancer is about as exciting as going to work and not getting paid.

Now, ask yourself, what do the MOST POPULAR MMORPGs have that Freelancer doesn't?

A fully-malleable universe.

I cannot play the role because the entire universe its based on feels fake. You cannot destroy or build stations, you have no unique character creation, you have no crafts or player economy, The economy is FIXED, and MOST OF ALL: you cannot have true political existince, let alone political hold over any region in the game.

Roleplaying in Freelancer is so unenticing because I have nothing to gain by doing it. But, unfortunately I cannot ignore it, because it has simultaneously disrupted the entire Browclops social fabric built up over the last few months.

I imagine most people will come to a similar conclusion, as HRE has disbanded after only a week, and even GOV's numbers are dwindling, and with that so are their ambitions.

Until then, I will probably not be around much. I play Freelancer to kill or be killed, which as always the intent. I'd rather go to work and get paid than try to roleplay in such a restricted universe.

Well this certainly was short lived, I think it's possible for people to continue to have alliances and allegiances, but nothing so formal.
What's been a killer here is the introduction of what feels like politics and bureaucracy, some people can get into that, but a lot of others won't, it calls for a long term commitment to something that many players aren't interested in making, this is a space sim not a political strategy game.
Look at what's happened here, right after one group declared it's intention to rule Sirius, other groups annexed territory and declared independence, many players thought this was going to be a means to mix things up a bit in the role play scenario of defending and or capturing territory. Instead the leaders of these groups had "peace talks" and made treaties, this may have been entertaining for the individuals involved, but it left many others feeling excluded and wondering what the point was, it's a small wonder that people are losing interest.......... Now If some wish to continue to role play, fine, but keep it small, keep it simple......remember you're not building an empire that will last for generations........Browclops said it himself, in a years time people may not even remember that his server existed, so let's just have fun there while we can.
