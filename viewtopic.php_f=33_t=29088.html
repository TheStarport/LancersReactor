Try flying around, fighting and/or trading some between each mission. This will allow you to garner more $ (either from salvage and loot, or traded commodities).

You can also try going to the EFA sysytem (from one of the jumpholes in each system with a House's capital planet, like New London, New Tokyo, Texas, New Berlin). Once in this system, go dock with the planet Earth, then launch, and hopefully a couple fleets will be duking it out in orbit.
You can just hang around, let 'em pound each other, and then collect the Deep Scanners ($12,000 value apiece ) from destroyed capships and Weapons Platforms. Think you can get around 14 or 16 of these in one launch -- lots of $
And (in my opinion) you deeply consider either get rid of that Patriot as soon as possible, or get some serious powerplant/engine upgrades quickly, as the ship is initially rather underpowered. Again, just my personal opinion.

Ah. Yes, if your rep is like that with Corsairs and Outcasts, try going out to the middle of a tradlane (in the middle, b/t bases and planets, where you only have to fight a few ships at a time).
Shoot a tradelane ring to make it shutdown, and then destroy a transport, a few freighters, or a few fighters of Navy, Universal, Police, Republican (basically any big corporations that are enemies w/criminals). Run if it gets too hot.

Now for the trricky bit: you will most likely be attacked by the unfriendly crimanals at first, AND the police/big corpoartions will fight if you shoot 'em.
Eventually, however, your rep w/criminals should be come nuetral/white, and they'll help you fight some.

It is a kinda lengthy method, but it DOES work; basically, shoot Navy, Police, Military, and corporations whenever you can get away with it.

Sun Mar 07, 2004 2:13 pm by Efi

Hi,
Did you consider trade? I just started a new game, and invested all the money I could collect during the first missions in a freighter. On planet LA or Jupiter research station you can buy the cargo ship from Star Wars (forgot the name). It has cargo hold of 700!
Once you have it, consider trade between planet LA and planet New Tokyo (using the EFA system as a link). Luxury consumer goods from LA, and engine components from New Tokio. Now, I still had problems with pirates and such, but this cargo ship has 3 slots for trusters. Use them all, of course your energy drain will be HUGE, but you can play with the kill engine option. If you encounter enemies, hit the trusters (you can go around 500 speed), kill the engine and switch to turrets in case some ship is able to catch up with you.
If you are willing to play even easier, you can trade luxury food from Jupiter research station to New Tokyo. On this road there are almost no enemy ships.
After collecting enough credits go and buy any ship you like! A bought the Colonial Viper, an excellent choice for early game. I had plenty of fun (especially the first 10 minutes) switching from the heavy freighter to one of the most agile ships in the game.
Hope this helps and sorry for any language mistakes (I am not a native speaker)!
Good flying!
