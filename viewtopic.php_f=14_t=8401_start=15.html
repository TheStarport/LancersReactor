Having made my share of idiot mistakes as a moderator, I'll share some of my best practices.

1. Post cyrstal clear guidance that new users are forced to read before they register on the forum.

2. Warn users when they break the rules. If they keep doing it, ban them and delete the offending posts.

3. Don't argue with people within the confines of the topic. It just leads to abuse of power. If you participate in an argument about Freelancer, it de facto becomes acceptable to the user to argue about Freelancer, because the moderator is doing it.

4. Get rid of off-topic posts. You already have this capability, because you have an Off Topic forum to move discussions that stray afield.

That's it. It's not rocket science, and it is the difference between running a universally acceptable, popular fan site, and running a personal playground where you bully and stomp on anyone who is not like-minded.
