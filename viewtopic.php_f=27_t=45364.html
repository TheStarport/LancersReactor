It's hard to understand what you did but it sounds like you activated two mods.
One may have overwritten some files from the other mod.

Technically it would be hard to answer.
You could try to run FLScan and see if that tells you what the problem is.
FLScan Version 1.3
FLScan is a program that will scan your modified Freelancer files looking for errors saving you time and headaches. You can direct the program to scan certain areas of your project or to simply scan the whole lot. An error report will be created which details the problems, common causes and possible ways to fix the problem. FlScan can also repair THN files, generate pathfinding information for a mod automatically and inspect your mod files as you work and report errors as you make them.

Other than that either don't use two mods that may clash or don't use the mod that don't work like it's suppose to.

Basically, using 2 mods together can and usually will cause problem unless the 2 mods were designed to work together.

As stated above, each mod will modify files and the second one may be over writing files that the first mod needs to work correctly or it may reference things that the first mod does not have or has removed.

So, basically, don't use the 2 mods together as you found a fix for getting the first mod to work.
