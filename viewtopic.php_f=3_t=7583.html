**nerd mode
Asynchronous Digital Subscriber Line

Means that your Upstream (the rate at which your PC sends data to the internet/other computers <normally 128 kBit/s, max 1 mBit/s> ) is different from your Downstream (the rate at which you PC receives data from the internet <normally 768 kBit/s to 1 mBit/s, max 8 mBit/s> ).
Most "DSL" users have ADSL, because SDSL (Synchronous Digital Subscriber Line) isn't that important for users who don't want to host servers etc.
