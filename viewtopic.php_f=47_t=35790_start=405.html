First, a clarification for Sisko3:

There are no Zoners in Magellan. Freeport 4 is operated by the IMG (a faction almost, but not quite, as neutral as the Zoners).


Next:

Wilde, I can see no way your ships could get to Honshu. The move was made after a declaration of war, and in order to get there you would've had to go through:

a. Shikoku and New Tokyo

or

b. Sigma-13 and Sigma-19

Sigma-13 had all the Kusari ships in it at the time, and its safe to assume they had SOMETHING in New Tokyo, if not in Shikoku. Now, the point of having ships in the Sigma Cluster would be to stop Rheinland ships from flying through there to Honshu. Now, we have a universe where space travel depends on interstellar "gates". You want to block passage between two systems. Where do you put your ships?

In front of the gate.

Rheinland could use all the fancy tactics they wanted, but unless they could get past the fleet that we can safely assume was right in front of the only way through to Kusari (for now, we will assume that the Houses cannot use Jump Holes), they aren't going to get through.

Space is three-dimensional, yes, but there are still chokepoints.


So, here's my verdict on the current situation:

1. Rheinland received false information regarding its fleet's position and supply lines (something that can easily happen; "military intelligence" is an oxymoron)

2. Rheinland, realizing it would not be able to reinforce the fleet in the Sigma systems, ordered them to retreat to Honshu, not knowing that the fleet that was sent to Honshu had never reached its destination.

3. The Rheinlander forces, unable to comply with the orders, withdrew to the nearest point they could (in this case, Hamburg).
