Puke Barwalker

Sat Jun 28, 2003 6:26 pm by Puke Barwalker

Depends on what you like. For me, it's the freedom to do what you want. You can go off exploring, be a good guy or bad guy, then go back to the main missions whenever you want. I got my first computer in 1985 and loved playing ELITE on it, the game that is the ancestor of Freelancer. Unlike all the other games at the time, you could do what you wanted to do! You had a whole universe to explore.

Then came along the Wing Commander type games, where you had to do exactly what you were told to do -- or fail the mission and maybe the game. Some people love being told what to do. I don't. I'm a born rebel, and Freelancer lets me do whatever I want.

Now admittedly there is plenty of room for improvement, especially in those non-story line missions -- which are basically just the same thing over and over again. But I'm not doing them anymore. I'm exploring the Sirius system and trying to find everything there is out there. I have to juggle my reputation with the factions to be able to do that. And that is fun, for me anyway.
