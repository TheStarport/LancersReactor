Any ship lower than class 10 (except perhaps for the class 9s) is not going to have a chance against them. Facts:

* They maneuver slower.
* Have less shield and hull
* Their weapons drain energy
* Their weapons even if armed with shield killers do not deal enough damage to break through and cause significant damage.
* Only possible way of taking down someone is by scoring lucky with a Sunslayer torpedo if you can get their shields down with shield killers. This is fun the first time but when its the only tactic for you to beat class 10s in low level ships it kills the fun.

Because of these reasons just about everyone flies a Titan/Sabre/Eagle online and hence the significant player drop in the past few days. I've waited for 10 mins to list all the servers and to my surprise the server hosted by my internet provider had the highest player count (and their server only holds 24 people at the moment). There was a total of maybe 40-50 people playing online globally. That is a massive drop from the 100s of people that were playing simultaneously just last week.

Anchor Wayfaire

Sun Mar 30, 2003 6:33 pm by Anchor Wayfaire

For me, the problem is that you can't carry your character around with you. I spent hours exploring space on the Elite server, and building a character. Then they had their problems with cheaters, and wiped the server clean. I don't fault them for this, btw, this was far out of the Elite folks' control.

But I'm left with the same problem: not only do I have nothing, I've lost all my maps. It would be a little easier to take losing the Eagle if I didn't have to re-explore New York as if I'd never been there before.

This is what kills the multiplayer game for me. I don't much care for doing the map-making routine over and over again. It's patently ridiculous, anyway. Sort of like gaining exploration XP in Earth & Beyond. If the trade lanes are there, fully marked out with beacons and what-have-you, then you can just download sector maps to your navigation computer. The way exploration is handled in these games gives you a world that, by analogy, would be like a modern-day U.S. where every driver had to explore the Interstate highway system for himself, trying to find what cities were where, instead of just being able to pick up a map at any gas station.

It's idiotic, it's not much fun at all, and it's why I stopped playing multiplayer.
