Puke Barwalker

Sun Nov 16, 2003 4:18 am by Puke Barwalker

Have fun with the game! But you will soon find that you can only buy certain ships depending on your level in the game. You'll start out in a light all purpose ship with weak armour and shields and capable of using only low level guns. (However, it is very maneuverable and can dodge a lot of enemy fire.) After a couple of missions you'll be able to upgrade to a stronger ship ship with more powerful shields and guns. Thus it goes throughout the single player game, with you only able to buy stronger and more powerful ships when you have advanced enough. This actually makes the game more fun. If you started out right away in a high level ship, the game would be too easy.

You can buy freighters for trading, but they handle like a cow in a muddy field. The fighters will steer much better, but can't carry as much cargo. You'll have to play the game to see which you like.

Once you get your hands on the game, you'll be amazed at the universe you have to explore. The single play game has only so many missions. But then you can explore forever -- or use mods or play MP on line.

It really depends on your playing style, if you fancy a floating brick with enough guns to use all your energy in 10 secs (but are able to fire at any angle at any time) then try a Humpback. If you prefer fast firing lasers or photon blasters then stick them on a Dagger, fly like a lunatic, avoid getting hit and miss everything. Or simply grab every single slow weapon you can get, equip them on a Titan, be really accurate, kick ass or get blown to pieces. If you want some kind of midway between freighting and fighting then opt for a Drone or Dromedary.

Actually, you're best not listening to me as there really is no such thing as a "Best Ship". It's all really down to personal tastes, strategies and abilities.
