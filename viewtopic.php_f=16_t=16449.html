ok, i hail alot, if you hail a freight (train/transport) AND IF YOU ARE NUETRAL OR ALLIES WITH HIS FACTION, HE WILL TELL YOU SEVERAL THINGS. sorry about the caps.

1) his ships ID (gold 1-1, you are normally freelancer 1-1)
2) where he is coming from
3) what he is carrying, or picking up if empty
4) where he is going

how can you use this info

ok, let's assume you DON'T have any 3rd party proggies telling you the price of stuff, or what the best trade routes are.

1) you can learn from the computer what cargo is good to carry from what system to where.
2) you can decide on weather or not to form on them to get somewhere easily

how can you use this info


Some other uses for this information.

If you're playing as a criminal and you're exploring an unfamiliar area, hailing nearby criminals can tell you:

- what systems can be reached by jump hole from this system
- name of a criminal base in this system

Yes, you can find jump holes and bases by following patrol routes, which is often quick, but some jump holes and bases have fewer patrol routs leading to them, meaning it will take extra time to find them. If you hail someone and he says "I'm going to such-and-such base, my patrol is over", and it's a base you haven't found yet, you can find it by following him (when playing as a criminal I tend to not join formation with other criminals because they can involve you in a fight with an otherwise friendly faction).
