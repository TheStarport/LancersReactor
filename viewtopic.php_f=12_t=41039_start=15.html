Wilde

Sat Oct 01, 2005 11:06 am by Wilde

I figured someone would make that argument Chips. The two are absolutely incomparable. I play violin and electric guitar, and I've handled electric violins. While truly there is a great difference in expression between an electric guitar and an acoustic guitar, the difference is more comparable to that between a gut-stringed violin and a metal-stringed violin.

Baroque-age French music one can't play nearly as well with a metal-stringed violin, but with gut one can't play Wagner. Similarly, Hendrix's music sounds absolutely amazing with an electric guitar, as does Sting's with an acoustic. The difference all lies in style.

With an electric violin one sacrifices everything for volume--volume that the violin virtuoso can produce on a wood instrument with expression as well . Point blank, an electric violin simply cannot produce the quality of sound or portray emotion or convey beauty in anything near the same manner as a wood violin. An electric guitar's tone is fantastic and alive and brilliant....and an electric violin is just loud.

Taw--soul as in Martin Buber's "spirit". I mean this in the way he speaks of art, as conveying a deep sense of beauty and truth and goodness and emotion that transcends any one individual and arrives at a kind of universal statement.

Note: Truly great computer art is as valid an art form as anything else. It's just that the medium in and of itself because it is so simple truly requires a true artist to use it. Otherwise its just kitch.
