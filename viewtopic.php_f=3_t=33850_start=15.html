Bargib

Wed Aug 18, 2004 7:07 am by Bargib

Hehe - this was fun to read. Honostly, I'm in a sort of extended vacation atm, and only doing some stuff to keep the wheels turning. But I honostly do belive that FL2 is underway, and I expect to take up a more prominent role once more when I get to collect some more info on THAT game...

I started TLR for three reasons:
a.) I got the option from MS Denmark to run a competition on Starlancer, if I had a Starlancer website. I only ran a Age of Kings website back then. So I made a small website on Starlancer, and it sort of just started growing and growing from there. That I loved space-sims was just a bonus!

b.) I wanted a place to practise my coding skills, since I did have positions in my real life, where I did not get to code very much.

c.) I also wanted to see if I could build a self-sustaining friendly community, where people had a good time. I based it on my experience of running games-site, the first one called "Temple of Command & Conquer" back around 1994-1995. It was made with Perl and ran on a Unix server from my school.

The first member to the forums was a collegue of mine, but Hellcat came onboard shortly after. He was also the first staffmember.

There where tough times in the sites life, both due to situations in my life, and due to things happening on the site. Closest to closing was when I lost my job a year ago - this also meant I lost my hosting deal at the time - luckely another benefactor stepped up to help us all.

You are all a big part of my life - both current and former members, and I DO have plans for the site, but had to postpone the new forums due to both performance and security concerns, but hope to get that upgrade up with the next software release of the forum-code - with my custom adaptions.

Oh - and in my real life I'm working on a solution for people to easily make their own homepages. We expect to launch an US/International solution in a few months time!

Best Regards
Christian "Bargib" Koerner
Editor in Chief, The Lancers Reactor
